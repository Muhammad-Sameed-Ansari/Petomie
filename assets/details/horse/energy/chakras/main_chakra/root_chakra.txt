#Root Chakra (Muladhara)#

###Location###
Located at the base of the tail, extending into the perineum and deep pelvic floor.
Anchors energy downward into the hind legs, hooves, and lower spine.

###Color###
Red

###Element###
Earth

###Meaning & Function###
The Root Chakra forms the foundation of the horse’s physical stability, emotional grounding, and instinctual safety.
It governs the horse’s sense of security within its herd, environment, and routine.
This chakra supports calmness, trust, balance, and the ability to regulate fight–flight responses.

##Connection##
The hind limbs, hips, pelvis, tail, and sacrum
The skeletal system and weight-bearing joints
The colon, rectum, and elimination pathways
The adrenal glands and instinctual stress responses

###Signs of Balance###
Steady posture and grounded movement
Calm temperament and relaxed body language
Confidence in new environments
Strong connection to herd, routine, and handler
Healthy elimination and digestive stability

###Signs of Imbalance###
##Emotional / Behavioral##
Spookiness, hyper-vigilance, flighty behavior
Difficulty relaxing or settling
Separation anxiety or insecurity
Overreactivity to sound, movement, or touch
Startle responses or nervous tension

##Physical##
Hind-end weakness or stiffness
Tightness in sacrum, pelvis, or tail
Digestive issues such as constipation or tension-based diarrhea
Chronic adrenal stress or low vitality
Uneven grounding or shifting weight

###Balancing Activities###
Groundwork that builds trust and stability
Calm, predictable routines and herd security
Gentle grooming, slow touch, and quiet bonding
Turnout, roaming, and contact with nature
Support through grounding energy techniques near the tail base
Earth-based crystals (e.g., red jasper, smoky quartz) placed in the environment—not on the horse
