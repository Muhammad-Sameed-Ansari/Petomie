#Gall Bladder Meridian#

##Location##
Begins at the outer canthus of the eye (GB-1) and travels along the side of the head, passing behind the ear and over the poll region.
Continues down the side of the neck and shoulder, running along the lateral thoracic and abdominal wall.
Follows the contour of the flank and hip, then travels along the lateral aspect of the hind limb over the stifle and down the fibular region.
Ends at the lateral corner of the hind hoof, just above the coronary band (GB-44).

##Shape##
A long, lateral pathway running from the side of the head to the outer hind hoof.
It flows along the outer surface of the body, connecting head, flank, and hind limb.

##Function##
Regulates decision-making and courage
Supports bile secretion and assists digestion
Coordinates balance and flexibility in the hips and hind limbs
Clears heat and tension from the sides of the body
Encourages movement, adaptability, and determination
Balances inner drive with calm responsiveness

##Signs of Imbalance##
Muscle tightness or stiffness along the lateral body
Hip or stifle soreness and uneven gait
Eye irritation or head shyness
Indecisiveness, irritability, or frustration
Overreactivity to stimuli or reluctance to move forward
Digestive disturbances linked to stress or tension
