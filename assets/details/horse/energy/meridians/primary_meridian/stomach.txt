#Stomach Meridian#

##Location##
Begins just below the eye at the depression rostral to the facial crest (ST-1).
Descends along the side of the face, passing over the masseter muscle and under the mandible, then runs down the neck along its ventrolateral surface.
Continues through the chest and abdominal region just lateral to the midline, curving along the lower abdomen and flank.
From there, it travels over the stifle region, along the craniolateral aspect of the hind limb, descending over the tibia and down the front of the cannon bone.
Ends at the lateral side of the hind hoof at the coronary band (ST-45).

##Shape##
A long, superficial meridian running from the head to the hind limb, located mainly on the ventral and lateral sides of the body.
Closely follows the digestive tract regions and major muscular lines involved in locomotion and gut function.

##Function##
Regulates digestion, appetite, and nutrient absorption
Balances energy flow through the gastrointestinal tract
Influences the mouth, teeth, lips, and stomach
Releases tension in the neck, shoulder, and hind limb extensor chains
Supports emotional grounding and calmness when facing change
Clears stagnation and harmonizes the flow of Qi through the middle and lower body

##Signs of Imbalance##
Loss of appetite, bloating, or poor digestion
Tightness or sensitivity along the abdomen or hind limb
Grinding teeth, drooling, or mouth discomfort
Anxiety, irritability, or difficulty relaxing after feeding
Laminitis tendencies or weight imbalance
Overthinking or worry leading to digestive upset
