#Heart Meridian#

##Location##
Begins in the heart, traveling internally through the diaphragm and connecting with the small intestine.
Emerges at the axillary area (HT-1) and runs down the medial side of the forelimb, following the caudal border of the biceps brachii muscle.
Continues distally along the caudomedial aspect of the forearm, passing the carpus and metacarpus on the inner side, and ends at the medial corner of the front hoof, at the caudal edge of the coronet of the first digit (HT-9).

##Shape##
A single, medial line extending from the chest to the inner front hoof.
Closely follows the pathway associated with circulation and inner limb Yin meridians.

##Function##
Regulates cardiac rhythm, circulation, and emotional balance
Calms the spirit and supports mental clarity
Influences blood flow, warmth, and vitality
Assists in releasing anxiety and emotional agitation
Connects the heart to the forelimbs through the flow of Qi and blood
Encourages emotional expression and trust

##Signs of Imbalance##
Restlessness or anxiety
Unusual sweating, especially around the chest or flanks
Loss of vitality, dull eyes, or lack of spirit
Circulatory irregularities or cold extremities
Difficulty focusing or erratic behavior
Overexcitement or sudden fatigue after stimulation
Tendency toward isolation or disconnection
