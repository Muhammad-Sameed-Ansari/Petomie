#Spleen Meridian#

##Location##
Begins on the medial side of the hind hoof at the caudal edge of the coronet (SP-1).
Ascends along the medial side of the hind limb, passing over the fetlock, up the caudomedial aspect of the tibia and stifle region.
Continues along the medial thigh, crossing the inguinal area, and runs forward along the ventral abdomen close to the midline.
Travels cranially across the abdomen toward the thorax, ending at the 7th intercostal space on the lateral chest wall, near the level of the elbow (SP-21, the Great Luo point).

##Shape##
A single, medial pathway extending from the inner hind hoof to the chest, following the inner limb and abdominal surface.
Closely related to the digestive organs and connective tissue pathways along the ventral body.

##Function##
Governs digestion, transformation, and transportation of nutrients
Regulates blood quality and maintains muscle tone and connective tissue strength
Balances the Earth element and harmonizes the stomach and pancreas
Influences immune function and fluid metabolism
Supports emotional stability, grounding, and clear focus
Strengthens energy flow in cases of fatigue, poor appetite, or weak musculature

##Signs of Imbalance##
Loose stool, gas, or bloating after feeding
Weak muscle tone or poor topline development
Fluid retention or edema
Fatigue or lack of motivation
Clinginess or worry, difficulty focusing
Weakened immunity or slow recovery from exertion
