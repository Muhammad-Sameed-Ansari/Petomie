#Kidney Meridian#

##Location##
Begins on the plantar surface of the hind hoof at the caudal edge of the coronary band between the bulbs of the heel (KI-1).
Ascends along the caudal aspect of the hind limb, running between the tendons of the flexor muscles and over the hockâ€™s medial side.
Continues up the caudomedial surface of the thigh and passes through the inguinal area to enter the abdomen.
From there it travels along the ventral abdomen close to the midline, passing the flank and ending at the chest beside the sternum in the fifth intercostal space (KI-27).

##Shape##
A deep, medial meridian running from the hind hoof to the chest.
Closely follows the path of the major tendons and vessels along the inner limb and connects internally to the kidney and bladder organs.

##Function##
Stores and governs essence (Jing) and supports growth, fertility, and longevity
Regulates water balance and supports urinary and reproductive systems
Nourishes the lower back, hocks, and bones
Anchors Qi and strengthens willpower
Supports recovery from exhaustion and deep fatigue
Balances fear and enhances inner stability

##Signs of Imbalance##
Weakness or pain in the lower back or hocks
Urinary problems or reproductive issues
Cold limbs, dull coat, or poor stamina
Delayed healing or developmental issues
Fear, insecurity, or lack of motivation
Loss of vitality or burnout after long stress
