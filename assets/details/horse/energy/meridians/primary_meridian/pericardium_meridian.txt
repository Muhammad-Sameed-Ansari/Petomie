#Pericardium Meridian#

##Location##
Begins in the chest around the pericardial sac and travels through the diaphragm, emerging at the side of the chest near the 4th intercostal space (PC-1).
Runs down the medial side of the forelimb, between the Heart and Lung meridians.
Passes over the flexor surface of the forearm and continues down the middle of the inner cannon bone, ending at the tip of the medial front hoof at the coronary band (PC-9).

##Shape##
A single medial line following the inner forelimb, connecting the chest and the front hoof.
It lies between the Heart and Lung meridians, bridging emotional and circulatory functions.

##Function##
Protects the Heart and regulates emotional energy
Controls circulation and supports overall vitality
Releases tension in the chest and forelimb
Soothes agitation and supports emotional expression
Acts as a boundary between personal and external stressors
Encourages compassion balanced with self-protection
