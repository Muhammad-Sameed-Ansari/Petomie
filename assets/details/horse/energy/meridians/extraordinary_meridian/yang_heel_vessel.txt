#Yang Qiao Mai(Yang Heel Vessel)#

##Location##
Begins at the lateral corner of the hind hoof, travels up the lateral side of the hind limb, over the hip, flank, and ribcage.
Continues along the side of the neck, passing the cheek and connecting to the outer canthus of the eye, where it links with the Bladder meridian.

##Shape##
A vertical, lateral meridian following the outer hind limb and body up to the head.
Works in coordination with the Yin Motility Vessel to maintain balance between activity and rest.

##Function##
Regulates muscular tone and coordination on the lateral body
Controls wakefulness, responsiveness, and alert posture
Supports balance and stability during motion
Clears excess Yang or agitation from the head and eyes
Maintains equilibrium between movement and stillness

##Signs of Imbalance##
Tension or stiffness along the outer hind limbs or neck
Restlessness or over-alert behavior
Head tossing, eye irritation, or excessive reactivity
Difficulty relaxing or standing still
Uneven weight-bearing or asymmetrical stride
Nervous energy or hypervigilance
