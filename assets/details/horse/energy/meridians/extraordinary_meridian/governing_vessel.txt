#Governing Vessel (Du Mai)#

##Location##
Begins at the base of the tail (DU-1), runs along the dorsal midline over the sacrum, back, and withers, continuing over the neck and poll.
Passes between the ears and ends at the upper lip and gum line above the incisors (DU-28).

##Shape##
A single midline pathway running along the dorsal surface of the body.
It links the spinal cord, brain, and central nervous system and governs all Yang meridians.

##Function##
Controls the flow of Yang energy throughout the body
Strengthens the spine, back, and central nervous system
Supports posture, coordination, and vitality
Balances emotional stability and confidence
Connects body and mind, promoting awareness and strength

##Signs of Imbalance##
Tension or soreness along the back and neck
Weak topline or difficulty engaging the hindquarters
Overstimulation, nervousness, or reactivity
Spinal misalignment or rigidity
Loss of confidence, dominance issues, or fatigue
Energetic stagnation causing uneven gait or back pain
