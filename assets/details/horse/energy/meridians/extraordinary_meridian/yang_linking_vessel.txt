#Yang Linking Vessel (Yang Wei Mai)#

##Location##
Begins near the lateral aspect of the hock and ascends the outer side of the hind limb, following the pathway of the Gall Bladder meridian.
Travels over the flank, along the lateral thorax, and up the side of the neck toward the ear and temporal region.
Continues over the side of the head and connects with the outer canthus of the eye, where it joins the Yang Motility Vessel and other Yang meridians.

##Shape##
A lateral, ascending pathway that interlinks all the Yang meridians.
Acts as an energetic bridge connecting the exterior of the body and coordinating motion, defense, and responsiveness.

##Function##
Integrates and harmonizes all Yang meridians
Regulates external defense (Wei Qi) and reaction to environmental stimuli
Supports balance, coordination, and muscular synchronization
Releases tension from the lateral body, neck, and head
Assists adaptation to stress and changing conditions
Promotes alertness while maintaining calm focus

##Signs of Imbalance##
Stiffness or tightness along the sides of the body, neck, or hindquarters
Difficulty adapting to environmental changes or stress
Restlessness or fatigue alternating with hyperactivity
Lack of coordination or balance issues
Irritability or emotional agitation
Tension around the eyes, jaw, or temporal region
