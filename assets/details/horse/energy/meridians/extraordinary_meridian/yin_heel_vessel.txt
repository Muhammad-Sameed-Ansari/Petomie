#Yin Qiao Mai (Yin Heel Vessel)#

##Location##
Begins on the medial side of the hind hoof near the heel bulb, ascends the medial aspect of the hind limb, passes over the hock, stifle, and inner thigh.
Continues upward along the ventral abdomen and chest, connecting internally with the eyes and throat region.
It travels close to the Kidney meridian, sharing several energetic intersections.

##Shape##
A vertical medial pathway following the inner hind limb and ventral body.
Closely associated with Yin energy, balance, and rest functions.

##Function##
Regulates muscular tone and postural balance on the medial body
Supports sleep, calmness, and introspection
Influences reproductive and urinary organs
Harmonizes energy flow between left and right sides
Coordinates Yin activities such as rest, repair, and emotional stillness

##Signs of Imbalance##
Stiffness or tightness along the inner hind limbs
Difficulty resting or disrupted sleep patterns
Reproductive or urinary tension
Emotional withdrawal or excessive inward focus
Uneven balance between activity and rest
Inability to stand square or hold posture when relaxed
