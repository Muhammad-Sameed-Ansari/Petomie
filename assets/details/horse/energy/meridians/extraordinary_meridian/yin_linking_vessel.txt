# Yin Linking Vessel (Yin Wei Mai)#

The **Extraordinary Meridians** are deeper energy channels described in **Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)**.  
They serve as reservoirs of **Qi and Blood**, regulating and balancing the flow within the Primary Meridians.  
In horses, they represent the profound energetic framework that connects the bodyâ€™s central axis with emotional, mental, and spiritual equilibrium.  
Each Extraordinary Vessel influences groups of meridians, maintaining harmony between Yin and Yang and ensuring internal stability during change.  

The **Yin Wei Mai**, also called the **Yin Linking Vessel**, governs **emotional depth, inner harmony, and integration of experiences**.  
It connects the Yin meridians and nurtures the ability to process feelings with calm awareness and understanding.  

###Shape and Pathway###  
Begins near the inner hind limb, travels up the inner side of the body through the abdomen and chest, and ends near the throat.  
This upward and central flow supports emotional expression and integration through the heart and chest.  

###Function###  
Physically, it influences the heart, lungs, and digestive system, harmonizing internal organ function.  
It helps regulate blood flow and emotional rhythm.  
Energetically, it connects memory, emotion, and consciousness, promoting balance between sensitivity and strength.  

###Common Imbalances###  
Digestive weakness, chest tightness, or irregular breathing.  
Energetically, emotional instability, sadness, or difficulty processing experiences.  
The horse may show mood fluctuations, tension in the chest, or inconsistent energy levels.
