#Liver Meridian#

##Location##
Begins on the medial side of the hind hoof at the caudal edge of the coronet (LR-1).
Ascends along the medial aspect of the hind limb, passing behind the stifle and through the groin region.
Travels along the ventral abdomen and chest, then connects internally with the liver and gall bladder organs.
Ends at the area between the 6th and 7th intercostal space on the lateral chest wall (LR-14).

##Shape##
A deep, medial meridian following the inner hind limb and ventral body.
It connects the lower body with the torso, integrating physical and emotional flow.

##Function##
Ensures smooth flow of Qi and blood throughout the body
Supports detoxification, hormonal regulation, and muscle tone
Balances vision, coordination, and tendon elasticity
Influences emotional harmony, patience, and assertiveness
Aids digestion and reproductive health through energy circulation

##Signs of Imbalance##
Muscle tightness, spasms, or uneven movement
Irritability, frustration, or aggression
Eye problems, tearing, or head tossing
Digestive or reproductive imbalances
Sensitivity to confinement or frustration with routine
Tendency to hold anger or resentment
