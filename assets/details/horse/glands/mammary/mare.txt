#Mammary Glands (Mare)#

The mare’s mammary glands are located between the hind legs on the lower abdomen.  
They consist of two teats, each connected to its own gland, designed for milk production and nurturing.  
Energetically, they represent nourishment, maternal love, and emotional expression.

###Parts###

##Glandular Tissue##  
Produces milk through alveoli and ducts.  
Energetically, it symbolizes creation and nurturing flow.

##Teats##  
Two main openings for milk release.  
Energetically, they represent giving and connection.

##Duct System##  
Transports milk from alveoli to the teat canal.  
Energetically, it reflects fluid communication and generosity.

###Function###  
The mare’s mammary glands secrete milk to nourish the foal and maintain maternal bonding.  
Energetically, they express love, protection, and fulfillment through giving.

###Common Imbalances###  
Mastitis: inflammation or blocked ducts.  
Engorgement: emotional or physical overflow.  
Atrophy: lack of use or suppressed nurturing energy.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect difficulty giving or receiving care.
