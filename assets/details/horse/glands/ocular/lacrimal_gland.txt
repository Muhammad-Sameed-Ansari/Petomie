#Lacrimal Glands#

The horse’s lacrimal glands are the primary tear-producing glands located above the outer corner of each eye.  
They keep the cornea moist and protect against debris.  
Energetically, they represent emotional cleansing and release.

###Parts###

##Main Lacrimal Gland##  
Produces watery tears that wash and protect the eye.  
Energetically, it symbolizes renewal and relief.

##Accessory Glands##  
Assist in consistent tear production.  
Energetically, they reflect stability and gentle care.

##Ducts##  
Carry tears to the eye’s surface.  
Energetically, they represent flow and letting go.

###Function###  
Lacrimal glands produce tears to clean, protect, and nourish the eye.  
Energetically, they encourage emotional flow and clarity.

###Common Imbalances###  
Dry eye: suppression or emotional withholding.  
Overtearing: irritation or overstimulation.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect grief or release of old emotion.
