#Apocrine Glands#

In horses, apocrine glands are the main sweat glands found throughout the skin.  
They open into hair follicles and are especially active during sweating or emotional arousal.  
Energetically, they represent purification, release, and sensitivity to environment.

###Parts###

##Skin Apocrine Glands##  
Distributed across most of the body.  
Produce watery sweat mixed with proteins.  
Energetically, they symbolize cleansing and release of inner tension.

##Neck and Flank Glands##  
Highly active regions aiding thermoregulation.  
Energetically, they reflect adaptability and balance under pressure.

##Specialized Scent Areas##  
Include glands around mane, tail, and perineum.  
Energetically, they represent individuality and subtle communication.

###Function###  
Apocrine glands regulate body temperature, excrete waste, and release scent compounds.  
Energetically, they assist in clearing emotional stress and maintaining equilibrium.

###Common Imbalances###  
Overactivity: stress or excessive sweating.  
Blockage: heat buildup or emotional stagnation.  
Infection: irritation or poor detoxification.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect tension, anxiety, or suppressed expression.
