#Xiphoid Cartilage#

##Location##
The xiphoid cartilage, or xiphoid process, is the small cartilaginous extension at the lower end of the sternum.
It marks the transition between the thoracic and abdominal regions and serves as the attachment point for abdominal muscles.

###Shape###
A soft, flexible cartilage that may become partially ossified in mature horses, tapering toward the abdomen.

###Function###
Supports the connection between the sternum and abdominal wall, aiding in breathing and posture.
Energetically, it represents grounding of the heart’s energy and integration of strength with calmness.
