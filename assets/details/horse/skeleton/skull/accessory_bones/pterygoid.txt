#Pterygoid#

##Location##
The pterygoid bones are located deep within the skull of the horse, behind the upper jaw and beneath the base of the skull.
They form part of the nasal and oral cavity walls and support the upper airway and jaw mechanics.

###Shape###
Thin, wing-like plates of bone extending downward from the skull base.
They create supportive channels for muscles, nerves, and airflow.

###Parts###

##Medial Plate##
Forms the inner wall of the nasal passage.
Energetically, represents inner balance and emotional boundaries.

##Lateral Plate##
Provides attachment for jaw and throat muscles.
Energetically, reflects strength in expression and controlled movement.

##Pterygoid Process##

###Function###

Guides airflow and muscle coordination between the jaw and throat.
