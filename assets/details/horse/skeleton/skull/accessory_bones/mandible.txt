#Mandible#

##Location##
The mandible forms the lower jaw of the horse, extending from the chin to the joint near the ear.  
It articulates with the skull at the temporomandibular joint (TMJ).

###Shape###
A large, curved bone composed of two halves fused at the midline (the mandibular symphysis).  
Each side has a horizontal body and an upward ramus.

###Parts###

##Body##
The lower portion holding the lower teeth.  
Energetically, represents foundation and nourishment.

##Ramus##
The vertical section that connects to the skull.  
Energetically, reflects strength and decision-making.

##Coronoid Process##
Upper projection for muscle attachment.  
Energetically, symbolizes drive and determination.

##Condyle##
Forms the joint with the skull at the TMJ.  
Energetically, mirrors flexibility and communication.

###Function###
Supports chewing, biting, and communication.  
Provides leverage for mastication and balance for the jaw and head.  
Energetically, governs expression, nourishment, and balance in giving and receiving.
