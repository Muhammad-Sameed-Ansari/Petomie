#Hyoid Apparatus#

##Location##
The hyoid apparatus lies in the throat region of the horse, suspended between the lower jaw bones.  
It connects the tongue, larynx, and pharynx, forming a flexible support structure.

###Shape###
A series of small bones forming a U-shaped framework: the basihyoid, stylohyoid, epihyoid, ceratohyoid, and thyrohyoid.

###Parts###

##Basihyoid##
The central anchor of the hyoid structure.  
Energetically, represents stability and communication balance.

##Stylohyoid##
The largest part, connecting the skull to the hyoid system.  
Energetically, reflects direction and expression.

##Epihyoid and Ceratohyoid##
Small linking bones that maintain flexibility and harmony.  
Energetically, embody adaptability and emotional resonance.

##Thyrohyoid##
Connects the hyoid to the larynx.  
Energetically, symbolizes grounding in expression and truth.

###Function###
Supports swallowing, vocalization, and tongue movement.  
Energetically, governs self-expression, communication, and inner truth.
