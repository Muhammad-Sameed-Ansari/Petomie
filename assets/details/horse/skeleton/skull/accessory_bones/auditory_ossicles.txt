#Auditory Ossicles#

##Location##
The auditory ossicles are three small bones located within the middle ear of the horse, enclosed in the tympanic cavity of the temporal bone.  
They connect the eardrum to the inner ear, transmitting sound vibrations.

###Shape###
Tiny, irregularly shaped bones — the malleus (hammer), incus (anvil), and stapes (stirrup) — linked together in a delicate chain.

###Parts###

##Malleus##
Attached to the inner surface of the eardrum and transmits its vibrations to the incus.  
Energetically, represents reception and awareness.

##Incus##
The intermediate bone connecting the malleus and stapes.  
Energetically, symbolizes balance and transmission of understanding.

##Stapes##
The smallest bone, attached to the oval window of the inner ear.  
Energetically, reflects fine tuning and perception.

###Function###
Transmits and amplifies sound waves from the outer ear to the inner ear for hearing.  
Energetically, governs sensitivity, awareness, and inner clarity.
