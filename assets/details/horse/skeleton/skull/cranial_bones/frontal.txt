#Frontal Bone#

##Location##
Located at the front part of the skull, forming the forehead and upper region of the eye sockets.  
It lies between the nasal bones and the parietal bones and contributes to the roof of the cranial cavity.

###Parts###
1. **Body** – Forms the main surface of the forehead and roof of the orbits.  
   Protects the frontal lobes of the brain.  
   Contributes to facial contour and skull symmetry.

2. **Frontal Sinuses** – Air-filled cavities within the frontal bone.  
   Extend backward between the eyes and into the horn core region in some species.  
   Help reduce skull weight and resonate sound.

3. **Orbital Margin** – Upper rim of the eye socket.  
   Provides protection for the eye and attachment for eyelid muscles.

##Function##
 Forms the forehead and part of the skull roof.  
 Protects the brain and eyes.  
 Houses the frontal sinus to lighten the skull.  
 Provides muscle attachment for facial and ocular structures.
