#Occipital Bone#

##Location##
The occipital bone forms the back and base of the skull, surrounding the foramen magnum through which the spinal cord passes.

###Shape###
A large, curved bone joining the parietal and temporal bones, articulating with the first cervical vertebra (atlas).

###Parts###

##Foramen Magnum##
The large central opening for the spinal cord.  
Energetically, represents transition and connection between mind and body.

##Occipital Condyles##
Smooth joints connecting to the atlas.  
Energetically, symbolize adaptability and flow.

##Nuchal Crest##
A ridge for neck muscle attachment.  
Energetically, reflects strength and grounded alignment.

###Function###
Supports the head, protects the brain, and connects it to the spine.  
Energetically, governs balance between thought and action.
