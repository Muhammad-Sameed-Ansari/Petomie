#Parietal Bone#

##Location##
Located at the top and sides of the skull, forming the upper portion of the cranial roof.  
Joined at the midline by the sagittal suture and bordered by the frontal and occipital bones.

###Parts###
1. **Body** – Flat, plate-like bone forming part of the cranial vault.  
   Protects the dorsal portion of the brain.  
   Articulates with the frontal, occipital, and temporal bones.

2. **Sagittal Crest** – Prominent ridge along the midline formed by the meeting of the parietal bones.  
   Serves as an attachment for temporal and nuchal muscles.

##Function##
 Protects the brain and forms part of the skull roof.  
 Provides muscle attachment for chewing and neck stabilization.  
 Contributes to skull shape and structural integrity.
