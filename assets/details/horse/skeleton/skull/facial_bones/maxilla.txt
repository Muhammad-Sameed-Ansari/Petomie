#Maxilla#

##Location##
Forms the main part of the upper jaw and face.  
Extends from the incisive bone at the front to the zygomatic bone at the back.

###Parts###
1. **Body** – Contains alveoli for the upper cheek teeth.  
   Houses the maxillary sinus and contributes to facial contour.

2. **Facial Surface** – Forms the lateral surface of the face.  
   Provides attachment for facial muscles involved in chewing and lip motion.

3. **Palatine Process** – Forms part of the hard palate, separating the nasal and oral cavities.  
   Joins with the opposite maxilla and palatine bones.

4. **Infraorbital Foramen** – Opening through which the infraorbital nerve and vessels emerge.  
   Important for facial sensation and circulation.

##Function##
 Houses the upper cheek teeth and forms part of the nasal and oral cavities.  
 Supports muscles of mastication and facial movement.  
 Protects the maxillary sinus and associated nerves.  
 Provides structure and strength to the upper jaw.
