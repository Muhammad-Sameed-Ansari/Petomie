#Incisive Bone#

##Location##
Located at the front of the upper jaw, forming the rostral (front) part of the face.  
It lies ahead of the maxilla and forms part of the upper dental arcade and hard palate.

###Parts###
1. **Body** – Houses the sockets for the upper incisor teeth.  
   Forms the framework of the upper lip and muzzle area.  
   Contributes to the contour of the nostrils and nasal opening.

2. **Palatine Process** – Extends caudally to form the anterior portion of the hard palate.  
   Joins with the maxilla and palatine bones, separating the nasal and oral cavities.

3. **Nasal Process** – Extends dorsally to articulate with the nasal bone.  
   Helps shape the upper part of the nasal opening.

##Function##
 Supports and anchors the upper incisor teeth.  
 Forms the front section of the hard palate and nasal aperture.  
 Provides structure for the muzzle and nostrils.  
 Contributes to prehension (grasping food) and facial expression.
