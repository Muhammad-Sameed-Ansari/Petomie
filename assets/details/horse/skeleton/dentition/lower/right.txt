#Lower Right Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the lower right quadrant of the horse's mouth, within the mandible (lower jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the lower central incisors) to the last molar on the right side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Broad, flat front teeth for cutting and cropping grass.  
, Canine (1): Found mainly in males, used for protection or social interaction.  
, Premolars (3 or 4): Strong, ridged teeth that grind fibrous plant matter.  
, Molars (3): Large, flat-crowned teeth specialized for crushing and mashing forage.

##Function##
, Grinds and processes fibrous food for efficient digestion.  
, Works with the upper teeth to maintain balanced chewing and even wear.  
, Supports jaw alignment, comfort, and steady mastication rhythm.  
, Essential for nutrient absorption and long-term oral health.
