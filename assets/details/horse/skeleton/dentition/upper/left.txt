#Upper Left Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the upper left quadrant of the horse's mouth, within the maxilla (upper jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the upper central incisors) to the last molar on the left side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Broad front teeth used for cutting and nipping grass.  
, Canine (1): Found mainly in males, used for defense or dominance displays.  
, Premolars (3 or 4): Large, ridged teeth adapted for grinding fibrous food.  
, Molars (3): Broad, flat-crowned teeth designed for crushing and breaking down rough forage.

##Function##
, Cuts, grinds, and crushes fibrous plant material for digestion.  
, Works with the lower teeth to ensure balanced chewing and food breakdown.  
, Maintains alignment and jaw balance.  
, Supports efficient nutrient absorption and overall oral health.

###Energetic Connection###
, Connected to the Stomach, Large Intestine, and Spleen meridians.  
, Reflects balance between nourishment, grounding, and emotional processing.  
, Imbalances may appear as jaw tension, digestive disturbances, or irritability during feeding.  
, Balanced energy in this region supports calm digestion, emotional stability, and physical strength.

##Notes##
An adult horse typically has **36 to 44 teeth total**, depending on gender and presence of wolf teeth.  
Balanced upper dentition supports even chewing, healthy digestion, and harmonious energy flow throughout the body.
