#Upper Right Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the upper right quadrant of the horse's mouth, within the maxilla (upper jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the upper central incisors) to the last molar on the right side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Broad front teeth used for cutting and nipping grass.  
, Canine (1): Found mainly in males, used for display and defense.  
, Premolars (3 or 4): Strong, ridged teeth for grinding fibrous food.  
, Molars (3): Large, flat-crowned teeth for crushing and grinding rough forage.

##Function##
, Cuts, grinds, and crushes fibrous plant material for digestion.  
, Maintains alignment and balance with the lower jaw.  
, Essential for efficient chewing and nutrient absorption.  
, Supports overall oral symmetry and health during grazing.

###Energetic Connection###
, Connected to the Stomach, Large Intestine, and Spleen meridians.  
, Reflects balance in nourishment, grounding, and emotional processing.  
, Imbalances may show as tension, digestive disturbances, or frustration in feeding behavior.  
, Balanced energy supports calm digestion, grounded presence, and emotional steadiness.

##Notes##
An adult horse typically has **36 to 44 teeth total**, depending on gender and presence of wolf teeth.  
Proper dental care and energetic alignment ensure balanced chewing, healthy digestion, and overall harmony.
