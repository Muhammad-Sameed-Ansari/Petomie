#Tibia#

##Location##
The tibia forms the main weight-bearing bone of the lower hind limb.
It extends from the stifle joint to the hock, forming part of both joints.

###Shape###
A large, triangular bone that narrows downward and slightly twists along its length.

###Parts###

##Proximal End##
Forms the stifle joint with the femur and patella.
Energetically, represents foundation and movement initiation.

##Shaft##
The long, slightly curved section supporting the limb.
Energetically, symbolizes stability and direction.

##Distal End##
Forms the upper surface of the hock joint.
Energetically, reflects action and grounded momentum.

###Function###
Supports weight, transfers power from the upper limb to the lower, and provides leverage for propulsion.
Energetically, governs grounded strength, endurance, and alignment in motion.
