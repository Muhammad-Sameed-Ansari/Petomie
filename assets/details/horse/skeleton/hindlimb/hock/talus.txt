#Talus#

##Location##
The talus is located in the upper part of the hock joint, between the tibia and the calcaneus.
It articulates with the tibia to form the main hinge of the hock.

###Shape###
A large, rounded bone with smooth articular surfaces allowing movement in one primary plane.

###Parts###

##Trochlea##
The grooved upper surface that articulates with the tibia.
Energetically, represents direction and movement.

##Head##
Connects with the central tarsal bone.
Energetically, symbolizes coordination and balance.

##Neck##
Narrow section connecting the head and body.
Energetically, reflects flexibility and flow.

###Function###
Acts as the primary hinge of the hock, transmitting forces between the leg and foot.
Energetically, governs mobility, adaptability, and direction.
