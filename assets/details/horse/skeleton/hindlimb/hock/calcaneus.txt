#Calcaneus#

##Location##
The calcaneus forms the prominent point of the hock at the back of the hind limb.
It lies behind the talus and extends upward, providing leverage for tendons and muscles.

###Shape###
An elongated, strong bone that projects backward, forming the heel of the horse.

###Parts###

##Body##
The main section forming the structure of the hock.
Energetically, represents strength and endurance.

##Tuber Calcanei##
The large projection that forms the point of the hock where the Achilles tendon attaches.
Energetically, symbolizes drive, propulsion, and determination.

##Articular Surface##
Joins with the talus and other tarsal bones.
Energetically, reflects flexibility and connection.

###Function###
Acts as a lever for the muscles of the hind limb, providing power for propulsion and balance.
Energetically, governs action, determination, and focused movement.

Energetically, frustration or overexertion.
