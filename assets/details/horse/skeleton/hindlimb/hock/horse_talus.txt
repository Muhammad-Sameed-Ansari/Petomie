#Talus#

##Location##
Situated in the proximal row of the tarsus, articulating with the distal tibia to form the main hinge of the hock joint.
Lies medial and slightly dorsal to the calcaneus.

##Shape##
A short, robust bone with a large trochlea on its dorsal surface for articulation with the tibia.
Its distal end articulates with the central and third tarsal bones.

##Function##
Forms the main articulation of the hock joint, permitting flexion and extension.
Transfers weight from the tibia to the tarsal bones below.
Plays a key role in the stability and motion of the hindlimb.
