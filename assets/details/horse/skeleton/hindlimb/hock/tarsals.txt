#Tarsals#

##Location##
The tarsal bones form the complex structure of the hock joint in the hind limb.
They are situated between the tibia and the metatarsals, providing flexibility and strength.

###Shape###
A group of small, irregularly shaped bones arranged in proximal, central, and distal rows.

###Parts###

##Proximal Row##
Includes the talus and calcaneus.
Energetically, represents foundation and propulsion.

##Central Row##
Contains the central tarsal bone.
Energetically, symbolizes balance and coordination.

##Distal Row##
Includes the first, second, third, and fourth tarsal bones.
Energetically, reflects adaptability and support.

###Function###
Form the hock joint, enabling movement, stability, and distribution of body weight.
Energetically, govern coordination, balance, and rhythm in motion.
