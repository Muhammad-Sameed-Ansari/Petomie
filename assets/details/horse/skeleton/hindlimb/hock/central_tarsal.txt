#Central Tarsal#

##Location##
The central tarsal bone lies between the proximal and distal rows of the tarsal bones in the hock joint.
It connects the talus and calcaneus above to the distal tarsals below.

###Shape###
A compact, cuboid-shaped bone that acts as a stabilizing bridge in the hock joint.

###Parts###

##Proximal Surface##
Articulates with the talus and calcaneus.
Energetically, represents connection and stability.

##Distal Surface##
Articulates with the distal tarsal bones.
Energetically, symbolizes cooperation and balance.

###Function###
Transfers force and allows flexibility in the hock joint during motion.
Energetically, governs flow, unity, and structural balance.
