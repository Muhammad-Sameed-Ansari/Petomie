#Phalanges#

##Location##
The phalanges form the bones of the hind foot, between the fetlock and the hoof.
They consist of three bones within each digit.

###Shape###
A series of small, strong bones that form the pastern and coffin region.

###Parts###

##Proximal Phalanx (Long Pastern)##
Connects the fetlock to the middle phalanx.
Energetically, represents direction and flow.

##Middle Phalanx (Short Pastern)##
Forms the joint above the hoof.
Energetically, symbolizes transition and adaptability.

##Distal Phalanx (Coffin Bone)##
Enclosed within the hoof capsule.
Energetically, reflects grounding and connection to the earth.

###Function###
Support the hoof, absorb shock, and allow smooth movement.
Energetically, govern stability, connection, and rooted confidence.
