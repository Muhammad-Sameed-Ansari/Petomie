#Metatarsals#

##Location##
The metatarsal bones form the lower part of the hind limb, located between the tarsus (hock) and the phalanges (toes).
The third metatarsal is the main weight-bearing bone, known as the cannon bone, while the second and fourth are smaller splint bones.

###Shape###
A long, straight bone with two thin lateral splints running along its sides.

###Parts###

##Third Metatarsal (Cannon Bone)##
The central, primary support bone of the hind limb.
Energetically, represents strength, direction, and grounded motion.

##Second and Fourth Metatarsals (Splint Bones)##
Thin supportive bones flanking the cannon bone.
Energetically, symbolize balance and assistance in motion.

###Function###
Provide structural support for the hind limb and transfer force from the hock to the fetlock joint.
Energetically, govern grounded propulsion, alignment, and teamwork in movement.
