#Sesamoids#

##Location##
The sesamoid bones are found at the back of the fetlock joint and within the hoof behind the coffin joint.
They serve as supportive structures for tendons and ligaments.

###Shape###
Small, rounded bones that act like pulleys to reduce friction and aid in smooth movement.

###Parts###

##Proximal Sesamoids##
Located behind the fetlock joint.
Energetically, represent balance and support under pressure.

##Distal Sesamoid (Navicular Bone)##
Positioned behind the coffin joint within the hoof.
Energetically, symbolizes stability and graceful flow.

###Function###
Assist in joint movement, reduce tendon strain, and distribute pressure through the limb.
Energetically, govern adaptability, flexibility, and balanced support.
