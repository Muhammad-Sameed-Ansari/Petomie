#Fibula#

##Location##
The fibula lies alongside the tibia in the lower hind limb.
It extends from the stifle joint down toward the hock, but in horses, it is reduced and fused to the upper part of the tibia.

###Shape###
A thin, elongated bone that is partially fused to the tibia and tapers toward the lower end.

###Parts###

##Head##
The upper part that articulates near the tibia.
Energetically, represents connection and stability.

##Shaft##
The narrow middle portion that fuses with the tibia.
Energetically, symbolizes cooperation and unity.

##Distal End##
Small and incomplete, ending near the hock.
Energetically, reflects refinement and adaptation.

###Function###
Provides lateral support to the tibia and serves as attachment for muscles of the lower hind limb.
Energetically, governs balance, cooperation, and structural harmony.
