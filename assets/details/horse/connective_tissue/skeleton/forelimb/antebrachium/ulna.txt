#Ulna#

##Location##  
The ulna lies in the **forelimb**, beside the **radius**, forming part of the horse’s elbow joint.  
In horses, it is largely **fused to the radius**, with only the upper portion distinct.  

###Shape###  
A **thin, elongated bone** tapering downward, partially merged with the radius.  

###Parts###  

##Olecranon Process##  
Forms the point of the elbow for triceps attachment.  
Energetically, represents **drive and strength**.  

##Shaft##  
Long, fused region along the radius.  
Energetically, symbolizes **integration and support**.  

##Distal End##  
Small and incomplete in adult horses.  
Energetically, reflects **adaptation and refinement**.  

###Function###  
Provides leverage for forelimb extension and stability during motion.  
Energetically, governs **action, propulsion, and endurance**.
