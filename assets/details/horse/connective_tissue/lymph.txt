#Lymph#

##Location##
Lymph is a **clear fluid** that travels through the **lymphatic system**, a network of vessels, ducts, and nodes found throughout the horseâ€™s body.  
It runs alongside blood vessels and drains excess fluid from tissues back into the bloodstream.  
Major lymph nodes are found in the **neck, jaw, shoulder, chest, groin, and behind the knees**.

##Shape##
Lymph is a **clear or slightly yellowish fluid** containing water, proteins, and white blood cells called lymphocytes.  
It moves slowly through thin-walled vessels with valves that prevent backflow.

##Parts##

###1. Lymph Fluid###
- Clear liquid that surrounds tissues.  
- **Function:** Removes waste, toxins, and excess fluid from body tissues.  

###2. Lymph Nodes###
- Small, bean-shaped organs throughout the body.  
- **Function:** Filter lymph and destroy harmful microorganisms.  

###3. Lymph Vessels###
- Thin tubes that carry lymph throughout the body.  
- **Function:** Return filtered lymph fluid to the bloodstream.  

###4. Lymphocytes###
- White blood cells (T and B cells).  
- **Function:** Fight infections and regulate immune response.  

###5. Spleen###
- Located near the stomach.  
- **Function:** Stores and filters blood, produces lymphocytes, and removes damaged cells.  

###6. Thymus###
- Found in the chest, larger in young horses.  
- **Function:** Helps produce and mature T cells for immunity.  

###7. Tonsils###
- Found at the back of the throat.  
- **Function:** Capture bacteria and viruses entering through the mouth or nose.  

##Function##
- **Immunity:** Defends against infections and diseases.  
- **Fluid balance:** Removes excess fluid and returns it to the blood.  
- **Filtration:** Cleans lymph before it re-enters circulation.  
- **Waste removal:** Helps eliminate toxins, bacteria, and debris.  
- **Transport:** Moves immune cells throughout the body.
