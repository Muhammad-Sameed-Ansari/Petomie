They support panoramic vision, motion detection, and environmental awareness.

###Parts###

##Retina##
The light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye.
Converts light into neural signals.
Energetically, represents perception and awareness.

##Rods##
Photoreceptors specialized for low-light and motion detection.
Highly developed in horses.
Energetically, symbolize sensitivity and instinct.

##Cones##
Photoreceptors responsible for color and detail.
Horses primarily perceive blue and yellow hues.
Energetically, represent discernment and clarity.

##Optic Disc##
The exit point of the optic nerve from the retina.
Energetically, symbolizes transition and integration.

##Optic Nerve##
Transmits visual information from the eye to the brain.
Energetically, represents communication and trust.

##Optic Chiasm##
Crossing of optic nerve fibers allowing coordination between both eyes.
Energetically, reflects balance and integration.

##Optic Tract##
Carries visual signals toward the brain.
Energetically, symbolizes direction and continuity.

##Lateral Geniculate Nucleus##
Processes visual signals within the thalamus.
Energetically, represents filtering and interpretation.

##Visual Cortex##
Located in the occipital lobe.
Interprets visual information into meaning.
Energetically, symbolizes understanding and awareness.

###Function###
Receive light, convert it into neural signals, process motion and space, and interpret the environment.
Energetically, these structures support orientation, instinctual safety, and confident movement.

###Common Imbalances###
Vision impairment, night vision issues, neurological visual disturbances.
Energetically, imbalance may reflect disorientation, fear responses, or difficulty interpreting surroundings.
