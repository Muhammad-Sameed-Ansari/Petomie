#Tail Surface Muscles#

The surface muscles of the horse’s tail control visible movement and expression.  
They lift, swing, and position the tail during motion and communication.  
Energetically, they represent sensitivity, awareness, and emotional release.

###Parts###

##Coccygeus##  
Raises and moves the tail laterally.  
Energetically, it symbolizes alertness and freedom of expression.

##Intertransversarii Muscles##  
Support smooth coordination between tail vertebrae.  
Energetically, they reflect harmony and refined awareness.

##Superficial Fascia##  
Connects skin to muscle for smooth movement.  
Energetically, it represents flow and emotional fluidity.

###Function###  
The surface tail muscles allow movement and communication, expressing the horse’s mood and balance.  
Energetically, they channel subtle emotion, helping energy move freely through the spine and aura.

###Common Imbalances###  
Stiffness: suppressed emotion or tension.  
Overuse: stress from irritation or swishing.  
Sensitivity: heightened reactivity or imbalance.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect emotional overload or disrupted energy flow.
