#Tail Muscles – Main Movers#

##Location##
Extend from the **sacrum and pelvis** along the **caudal vertebrae** to the tail tip.  
Form the bulk of the tail musculature on the **dorsal, ventral, and lateral** aspects of the tail.

###Shape###
Robust **longitudinal muscle bands** with repeated short segments attaching to consecutive vertebrae.  
Arranged as **dorsal elevators**, **ventral depressors**, and **lateral flexors/stabilizers** for strong, sweeping movements.

##Parts##

###1. Sacrocaudalis Dorsalis Medialis###
- Thick midline band on the dorsal tail.  
- **Function:** **Elevates** and stiffens the tail (dorsiflexion), aiding balance and expression.

###2. Sacrocaudalis Dorsalis Lateralis###
- Dorsolateral bands on each side.  
- **Function:** **Elevates** and **abducts** the tail; key for broad **side-to-side** swishes.

###3. Sacrocaudalis Ventralis Medialis###
- Ventral midline band under the caudal vertebrae.  
- **Function:** **Depresses** the tail (ventroflexion); assists **tail tucking** during posture changes.

###4. Sacrocaudalis Ventralis Lateralis###
- Ventrolateral bands flanking the midline.  
- **Function:** **Depresses** and **adducts** the tail; refines downward and inward arcs.

###5. Intertransversarii Caudae###
- Short muscles spanning transverse processes.  
- **Function:** **Laterally flex** the tail and **stabilize** each vertebral segment during sweeping motions.

###6. Coccygeus###
- From the ischial spine to the first caudal vertebrae.  
- **Function:** **Moves the tail base laterally**, stabilizes the **dock**, and supports the pelvic outlet.

##Function##
Produce the **primary tail movements** — lifting, lowering, and powerful **side sweeps** used for **fly defense**, **communication**, and **balance**.  
Stabilize each caudal segment for **precise positioning** during gait transitions and turns.  
Support posture at the **tail base (dock)** and coordinate with trunk muscles for smooth whole‑body motion.
