#Transversus Thoracis#

The transversus thoracis muscle lies along the inner front wall of the chest.  
It helps depress the ribs during exhalation and supports rhythmic breathing.  
Energetically, it represents the gentle balance between heart and breath, grounding calm within motion.

###Parts###

##Inner Chest Fibers##  
Pull the ribs inward to assist breathing rhythm.  
Energetically, they symbolize quiet strength and centered focus.

##Sternal Attachments##  
Connect the ribs to the sternum for structure and flow.  
Energetically, they reflect harmony between stability and openness.

###Function###  
The transversus thoracis aids exhalation, supporting the rhythm of breathing and maintaining internal balance.  
Energetically, it harmonizes breath with calm awareness, linking chest stability with emotional ease.

###Common Imbalances###  
Chest tightness: shallow or restricted breathing.  
Uneven rhythm: imbalance in exhalation control.  
Tenderness: strain or tension from effort.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect emotional pressure or loss of inner calm.
