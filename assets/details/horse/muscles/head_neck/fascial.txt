#Facial Expression Muscles#

##Location##
Located around the **eyes, lips, nostrils, and ears**, attaching primarily to the skin.  
They enable **subtle facial expressions**, ear motion, and control of the lips and nostrils for communication and sensory function.

##Shape##
Composed of **thin, flat, and highly elastic muscles**, suited for fine motor control and expressive mobility.

##Parts##

###1. Orbicularis Oculi###
Encircles the eyelids.  
- Closes the eyes  
- Protects against dust and light  

###2. Levator Nasolabialis###
Runs from the nasal bone to the upper lip.  
- Raises upper lip and nostrils  
- Aids in sniffing and emotional expression  

###3. Zygomaticus###
From zygomatic arch to corner of mouth.  
- Retracts the lips and corners of mouth  
- Creates relaxed or alert facial states  

###4. Buccinator###
Forms the muscular wall of the cheek.  
- Keeps food between teeth while chewing  
- Controls cheek tension during feeding  

###5. Orbicularis Oris###
Encircles the lips.  
- Closes lips and maintains food in mouth  
- Aids in vocalization and expression  

###6. Platysma###
Superficial sheet covering side of face and neck.  
- Retracts mouth corners  
- Tightens facial skin  

###7. Frontalis###
Located on forehead.  
- Moves forehead skin and upper eyelids  
- Works with auricular muscles for ear movement  

###8. Depressor Labii Inferioris###
Runs along lower lip.  
- Pulls lower lip downward  
- Important for grazing and chewing motions  

###9. Auricular Muscles###
Surround and move ears.  
- Rotate and tilt ears toward sounds  
- Express mood and awareness  

##Function##
- Allow **facial expression and communication**  
- Control **lip, nostril, and ear motion**  
- Aid in **feeding and breathing**  
- Assist in **protecting eyes and mouth** from debris  
- Reflect emotional states such as alertness, calmness, or irritation
