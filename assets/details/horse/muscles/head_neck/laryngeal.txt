#Laryngeal Muscles#

The horse’s laryngeal muscles control the **larynx (voice box)**, regulating airflow, sound, and protection of the airway during swallowing.  
They surround the **cartilaginous framework** of the larynx and fine-tune the vocal cords.  
Energetically, they symbolize **communication, expression, and the freedom of voice**.

###Parts###

##Cricothyroideus##  
Tenses the vocal cords by tilting the thyroid cartilage forward.  
Energetically, represents **focused intention and precise communication**.

##Cricoarytenoideus Dorsalis##  
Opens the airway by abducting the vocal folds.  
Energetically, reflects **openness and courage to express truth**.

##Cricoarytenoideus Lateralis##  
Closes the glottis during swallowing.  
Energetically, symbolizes **boundaries and discernment in speech**.

##Thyroarytenoideus##  
Adjusts vocal tension for resonance.  
Energetically, embodies **harmony and vibration of authentic sound**.

###Function###  
Controls the position and tension of the vocal folds, regulating sound and breathing.  
Prevents aspiration during swallowing.  
Energetically, the laryngeal system governs **the power of voice and integrity of communication**.

###Common Imbalances###  
Tightness: restricted airflow or vocal strain.  
Weakness: breathing noise or “roaring.”  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect **suppressed truth or loss of voice**.
