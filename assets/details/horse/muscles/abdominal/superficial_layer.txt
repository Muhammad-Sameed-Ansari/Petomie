#Abdominal Muscles – Superficial Layer#

##Location##
Covers the outer sides of the horse’s abdomen, forming the most visible abdominal layer.  
It connects the ribs, flank, and pelvis, playing a major role in trunk movement and strength.

###Shape###
Composed of **broad, sheet-like muscles** running obliquely from ribs and spine to the midline.  
Provides both protection and mobility for the abdominal wall.

##Parts##

###1. External Abdominal Oblique###
- Outermost abdominal muscle layer.  
- **Function:** Compresses abdomen, aids trunk rotation, and supports posture.  

###2. Cutaneous Trunci (lateral part)**  
- Lies beneath skin over the flank.  
- **Function:** Assists skin twitching and light trunk movement.  

##Function##
- **Protection:** Shields abdominal organs.  
- **Flexion:** Assists in bending and lateral movement.  
- **Support:** Maintains abdominal pressure.  
- **Motion:** Works with deeper layers for coordinated strength.
