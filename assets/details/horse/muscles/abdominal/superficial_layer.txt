#Abdominal Superficial Layer#

The horse’s superficial abdominal muscles lie closest to the skin and are most visible during movement.  
They provide strength for flexion, posture, and protection of the inner core.  
Energetically, they represent expression of strength, vitality, and outer confidence.

###Parts###

##Surface Fibers##  
Lie under the skin and define the horse’s abdominal tone.  
Energetically, they symbolize visible strength and readiness.

##Outer Sheets##  
Connect to the ribs and pelvis, supporting overall posture.  
Energetically, they represent alignment and resilience.

##Tendinous Intersections##  
Reinforce stability during motion.  
Energetically, they reflect structure and integrity.

###Function###  
The superficial abdominal muscles support flexion, balance, and protection of the deeper core.  
Energetically, they express vitality, outward strength, and dynamic motion.

###Common Imbalances###  
Muscle strain: overuse or sudden exertion.  
Uneven tone: imbalance in training or posture.  
Tightness: tension from stress or poor flexibility.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect overexertion or defensive energy.
