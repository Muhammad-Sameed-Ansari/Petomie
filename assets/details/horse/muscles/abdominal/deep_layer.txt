##Location##
Situated beneath all other abdominal layers, closest to the internal organs.  
This layer stabilizes the core and connects the **lumbar spine, ribs, and pelvis**.

###Shape###
Composed of **thin, broad muscle sheets** running transversely across the abdomen.  
These deep fibers form the foundation for core strength and internal pressure control.

##Parts##

###1. Transversus Abdominis###
- Deepest layer of abdominal wall.  
- **Function:** Compresses abdominal contents, supports spine, and stabilizes the trunk.  

###2. Quadratus Lumborum###
- Lies between the pelvis and lumbar vertebrae.  
- **Function:** Stabilizes the lower back and controls side bending.  

###3. Psoas Minor###
- Small muscle on ventral surface of lumbar vertebrae.  
- **Function:** Aids spinal flexion and stabilizes lumbar region.  

##Function##
- **Core Strength:** Provides foundation for stability.  
- **Protection:** Supports internal organs.  
- **Balance:** Maintains alignment during movement.  
- **Posture:** Keeps back rounded and collected.
