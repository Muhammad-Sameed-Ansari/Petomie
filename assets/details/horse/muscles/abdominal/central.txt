##Location##
Situated along the **midline of the abdomen**, connecting the left and right abdominal walls.  
This area includes the **rectus abdominis** and the **linea alba**, which form the supportive center of the abdominal wall.

###Shape###
Composed of **paired, elongated muscles** running from the sternum to the pubis.  
The central line (linea alba) is a fibrous seam that joins both sides together for strength and stability.

##Parts##

###1. Rectus Abdominis###
- Long, strap-like muscle along the belly floor.  
- **Function:** Flexes the trunk, supports abdominal organs, and assists breathing and movement.  

###2. Linea Alba###
- Fibrous midline seam between the left and right abdominal muscles.  
- **Function:** Provides structural reinforcement and attachment for abdominal layers.  

##Function##
- **Core Stability:** Maintains midline support and posture.  
- **Trunk Flexion:** Aids bending and rounding of the back.  
- **Support:** Holds abdominal organs in place.  
- **Respiration:** Works with diaphragm during expiration.
