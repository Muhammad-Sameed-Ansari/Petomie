#Perineal Muscles#

##Location##
Located in the **perineal region**, between the **anus, tail base, and external genital organs**.  
These muscles form the **pelvic floor** and surround the terminal openings of the digestive and urogenital tracts.

##Shape##
Composed of **thin, paired and circular muscles** forming a supportive layer between the tail and genital area.  
Arranged around the anus and external openings, they assist in closure, support, and movement control.

##Parts##

###1. External Anal Sphincter###
- Circular muscle surrounding the anus.  
- **Function:** Controls voluntary opening and closing of the anal canal during defecation.  

###2. Coccygeus###
- Extends from the pelvic wall to the base of the tail.  
- **Function:** Aids in tail movement and stabilizes the pelvic outlet.  

###3. Levator Ani###
- Broad, thin muscle forming part of the pelvic diaphragm.  
- **Function:** Supports rectum and assists in defecation by elevating the anus.  

###4. Bulbospongiosus###
- Surrounds the root of the penis (in males) or vestibule (in females).  
- **Function:** Assists in urination, ejaculation (in males), and vaginal contraction (in mares).  

###5. Ischiocavernosus###
- Lies along the side of the perineum, covering the root of the penis or clitoris.  
- **Function:** Compresses veins to maintain erection during mating.  

##Function##
- **Defecation:** Controls anal opening and supports rectal passage.  
- **Urination:** Assists in emptying of the bladder.  
- **Reproduction:** Aids in mating, ejaculation, and vaginal closure.  
- **Tail Movement:** Stabilizes and moves the tail base.  
- **Support:** Maintains pelvic organ position and prevents prolapse.
