#Superficial Layer – Movement and Power#

The horse’s superficial muscular layer lies closest to the skin and creates visible motion and strength.  
It powers stride, lift, and collection, expressing energy and athleticism.  
Energetically, it represents vitality, confidence, and the outward flow of inner strength.

###Parts###

##Surface Muscle Sheets##  
Large, visible muscles that drive movement.  
Energetically, they symbolize expression and action.

##Power Bands##  
Provide propulsion for the limbs and neck.  
Energetically, they reflect focus and determination.

##Outer Connectors##  
Link muscle groups for coordination and strength.  
Energetically, they represent unity and flow.

###Function###  
The superficial layer generates movement, strength, and flexibility.  
It allows the horse to express energy freely and perform with grace and confidence.  
Energetically, it mirrors outward vitality and the joy of motion.

###Common Imbalances###  
Muscle strain: overuse or tension.  
Uneven tone: imbalance in training or posture.  
Tightness: restricted flexibility.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect overexertion or suppressed expression.
