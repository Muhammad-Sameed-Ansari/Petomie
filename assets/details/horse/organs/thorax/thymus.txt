#Thymus#

##Location##
The thymus gland is found in the **upper chest**, in front of the heart and behind the sternum (breastbone).  
Itâ€™s larger and more active in foals and young horses, gradually shrinking with age.

###Shape###
A **soft, lobed organ** made of lymphatic tissue.  
It appears pale and spongy in young horses and becomes smaller and more fibrous in adults.

##Function##
- Produces **T-cells**, vital for immune defense.  
- Supports **immune development** and healing capacity.  
- Connects the **endocrine and immune systems**.  
- Helps maintain vitality and protection during growth.
