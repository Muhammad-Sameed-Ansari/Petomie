#Brain#

##Location##  
The brain lies securely within the **cranial cavity of the skull**, surrounded by the **meninges** and cushioned by **cerebrospinal fluid**.  
It connects directly to the **spinal cord** through the **brainstem**, forming the central control system for all body and behavioral functions.

###Shape###  
A **rounded, folded organ** divided into regions — the **cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem**.  
Its outer layer, the **cerebral cortex**, handles higher thinking, while inner structures manage instinct, coordination, and automatic responses.

###Parts###  

##Cerebrum##  
Responsible for thought, learning, voluntary movement, and sensory interpretation.  
It processes emotions, memory, and decisions, guiding the horse’s interaction with the environment.  
Energetically, it symbolizes **conscious awareness and focus**.

##Cerebellum##  
Coordinates movement, balance, and timing.  
It ensures fluid motion and body control.  
Energetically, it represents **precision and harmony between thought and action**.

##Brainstem##  
Connects brain and body, controlling breathing, heart rate, and reflexes.  
Energetically, it grounds **vital life functions and stability**.

###Function###  
The brain integrates sensory input, controls motor output, regulates hormones, and maintains internal balance.  
It also stores learned patterns, habits, and responses that guide instinctive behavior.  
Energetically, it bridges **instinct and awareness**, aligning body and mind.
