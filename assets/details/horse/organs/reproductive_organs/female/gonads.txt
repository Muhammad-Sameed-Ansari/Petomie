#Gonads#

##Location##  
The mare’s ovaries are located deep within the **abdominal cavity**, just below the **lumbar vertebrae**, near the **kidneys**.  
Suspended by the **broad ligament**, they connect to the uterus through the **oviducts**.

###Shape###  
Large, bean-shaped organs—distinctly different from other species.  
Their texture and size change with the mare’s estrous cycle.

###Parts###  

##Follicles##  
Fluid-filled structures containing developing eggs.  
Energetically, represent **potential, renewal, and creative flow**.

##Corpus Luteum##  
Forms after ovulation and produces progesterone to maintain pregnancy.  
Energetically, symbolizes **nurturing, stability, and emotional grounding**.

##Tunica Albuginea##  
Protective connective tissue surrounding the ovary.  
Energetically, reflects **inner strength, resilience, and safe boundaries**.

###Function###  
Produce eggs and secrete hormones (estrogen and progesterone) that regulate the estrous cycle, fertility, behaviour, and emotional balance.  
Energetically, support **creativity, intuition, and rhythmic feminine flow**.
