#Adrenal Glands#

The horse’s adrenal glands are small, paired organs located above the kidneys.  
They produce hormones that regulate stress, metabolism, and energy.  
Energetically, they symbolize courage, vitality, and resilience.

###Parts###

##Cortex##  
Outer layer producing cortisol, aldosterone, and sex hormones.  
Energetically, it supports stability, endurance, and balance.

##Medulla##  
Inner core releasing adrenaline and noradrenaline.  
Energetically, it represents action, alertness, and strength.

###Function###  
The adrenal glands regulate the body’s response to stress, blood pressure, and fluid balance.  
They help the horse adapt to change and maintain internal stability.  
Energetically, they link to life force, survival instinct, and empowerment.

###Common Imbalances###  
Fatigue: adrenal exhaustion from chronic stress.  
Anxiety or Restlessness: overstimulation or energy depletion.  
Weak Immunity: imbalance in cortisol production.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect fear, overdrive, or lack of grounding.
