#Glossopharyngeal Nerve (IX)#

The horse’s glossopharyngeal nerve supports taste, swallowing, and salivation.  
It bridges nourishment and communication, uniting physical intake with vocal and energetic expression.  
Energetically, it reflects balance between speaking and receiving.

###Location###
Arises from the medulla and extends to the tongue and pharynx.

###Parts###
Lingual branch – supplies the back of the tongue for taste.  
Pharyngeal branch – aids swallowing and throat sensation.  
Tympanic branch – connects to the ear for sensory feedback.

###Function###
Coordinates taste, swallowing, and saliva secretion.  
Energetically, it supports the ability to receive, nourish, and communicate truth from the heart.
