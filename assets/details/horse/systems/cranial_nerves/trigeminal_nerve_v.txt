#Trigeminal Nerve (V)#

The horse’s trigeminal nerve is the main sensory nerve of the face and motor nerve for chewing.  
It connects physical sensation and nourishment with emotional grounding.  
Energetically, it represents strength, communication, and stability.

###Location###
Emerges from the pons and branches into three major divisions across the face and jaw.

###Parts###
Ophthalmic branch – supplies forehead and eyes.  
Maxillary branch – supplies cheeks and upper teeth.  
Mandibular branch – supplies jaw, tongue, and chewing muscles.

###Function###
Provides sensation to the face, teeth, and lips, and controls jaw motion.  
Energetically, it unites communication with grounded presence and the ability to express through nourishment and touch.
