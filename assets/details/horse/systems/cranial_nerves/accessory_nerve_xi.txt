#Accessory Nerve (XI)#

The horse’s accessory nerve governs neck and shoulder movement, expressing physical strength and freedom.  
It connects posture with confidence and inner power.  
Energetically, it represents courage, direction, and self-expression through movement.

###Location###
Arises from both the medulla and spinal cord, passing upward through the neck to muscles of the shoulders and head.

###Parts###
Cranial branch – assists in swallowing and vocalization.  
Spinal branch – controls muscles of the neck and shoulders.

###Function###
Enables head and shoulder motion, supporting posture and strength.  
Energetically, it enhances confidence, alignment, and empowerment through movement.
