#Small Intestine#

##Location##
Extends from the stomach to the large intestine, coiled throughout the abdominal cavity.

##Shape##
A long, narrow tube (approximately 20 meters in length) lined with villi to increase nutrient absorption surface area.

##Parts##
- **Duodenum:** First section, receives bile and pancreatic juices.  
- **Jejunum:** Middle and longest section where most nutrients are absorbed.  
- **Ileum:** Final section connecting to the cecum.

##Function##
- Continues enzymatic digestion of proteins, carbohydrates, and fats.  
- Absorbs vitamins, minerals, and nutrients into the bloodstream.  
- Moves digesta toward the large intestine using peristalsis.  
- Provides a site for microbial interaction and immune defense.
