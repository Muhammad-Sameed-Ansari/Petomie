#Salivary Glands#

##Location##
Surround the back of the mouth and throat area, opening into the oral cavity through small ducts.

##Shape##
Soft, lobed glands that produce large volumes of watery saliva when the horse chews.

##Parts##
- **Parotid Glands:** Behind the jaw, near the ear.
- **Mandibular Glands:** Beneath the jawline.
- **Sublingual Glands:** Under the tongue.

##Function##
- Produce saliva to moisten and lubricate feed.
- Aid swallowing and digestion of fibrous material.
- Maintain oral health and buffer stomach acid.
- Help neutralize acidity in the digestive system.
