#Mouth#

##Location##
The horseâ€™s mouth is located at the front of the head, extending from the lips to the pharynx. It serves as the entrance to the digestive system.

##Shape##
A broad cavity with soft lips, a large tongue, strong jaws, and flat grinding surfaces on the teeth. The mouth is lined with moist mucous membranes.

##Parts##
- **Lips:** Sensitive and flexible, used to grasp and sort food.
- **Cheeks:** Hold food inside while chewing.
- **Palate:** Forms the roof of the mouth.
- **Teeth and Tongue:** Work together to grind and move feed.
- **Salivary Ducts:** Openings for saliva to moisten food.

##Function##
- Takes in and selects feed.
- Mixes food with saliva to start digestion.
- Forms a soft mass (bolus) for swallowing.
- Begins the process of breaking down fibrous material.
