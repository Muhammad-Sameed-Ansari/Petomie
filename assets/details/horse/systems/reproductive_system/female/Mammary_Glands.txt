#Mammary Glands (Mare)#

##Location##
Located in the inguinal region between the hind legs, just in front of the udder area.  
Mares typically have **two mammary glands**, each associated with one teat.  
The glands are positioned closely together and are supported by connective tissue and skin folds.

###Shape###
Compact, rounded glands surrounded by connective tissue and fat.  
Each gland contains secretory alveoli that produce milk and ducts that converge toward the teats.

##Parts##

###1. Left and Right Mammary Glands###
Paired glands positioned in the groin area.

###2. Teats###
Two short, cylindrical projections, each with two duct openings for milk flow.

###3. Glandular Tissue###
Composed of alveoli that produce milk during lactation, regulated by hormones such as **prolactin** and **oxytocin**.

###4. Duct System###
Network of milk ducts converging toward the teat openings.

###5. Supporting Structures###
Connective tissue, blood vessels, and lymphatics that support the udder and maintain circulation.

##Function##
Produces and secretes milk to nourish the foal after birth.

Supports maternal bonding and nurturing behaviors during lactation.
