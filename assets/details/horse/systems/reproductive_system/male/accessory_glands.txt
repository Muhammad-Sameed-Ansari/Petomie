#Accessory Glands#

##Location##
Situated within the **pelvic cavity** around the **urethra** near its junction with the bladder.  
These glands contribute secretions to form the seminal fluid.

###Shape###
**Lobulated glandular structures** varying in size and shape.  
Their development is influenced by hormonal activity, especially testosterone.

##Parts##

###1. Prostate Gland###
Encircles the urethra and secretes a thin fluid that nourishes and protects sperm.

###2. Vesicular Glands (Seminal Vesicles)###
Paired, elongated sacs located on either side of the bladder.  
Produce a viscous secretion that forms part of the seminal plasma.

###3. Bulbourethral Glands###
Paired glands located near the root of the penis.  
Secrete a **mucous fluid** that lubricates the urethra and neutralizes acidity.

###Function###
Produce **seminal fluids** that support and protect sperm.  
Provide nutrients and lubrication during ejaculation.  
Help **neutralize acidity** and enhance sperm motility.
