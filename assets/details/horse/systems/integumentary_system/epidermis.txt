#Epidermis#

The epidermis is the horseâ€™s outermost skin layer, forming a resilient shield that protects from the environment, moisture loss, and injury.  
It renews constantly, creating a living barrier between the inner world and the outer surroundings.

###Parts###

##Stratum Corneum##  
The top layer made of flattened, dead keratinized cells.  
Provides water resistance and protection against friction and microbes.  
Energetically, it symbolizes resilience and the ability to let go of what no longer serves.

##Stratum Germinativum (Basal Layer)##  
The bottom layer of the epidermis where new cells are born and move upward as older ones shed.  
Contains pigment-producing cells (melanocytes) that influence coat color.  
Energetically, it reflects renewal, transformation, and self-regeneration.

##Keratin and Lipid Matrix##  
The structure of the epidermis is supported by keratin proteins and natural oils.  
These keep the skin strong, flexible, and slightly waterproof.  
Energetically, this blend represents balance between firmness and flow, strength and softness.

###Function###  
The epidermis guards the horse from bacteria, sunlight, and dehydration.  
It allows sweating and sensory exchange through hair follicles and glands.  
Energetically, it supports awareness, protection, and healthy boundaries in connection with the outer world.

###Common Imbalances###  
Dry or Flaky Skin: dehydration, lack of essential oils, or poor diet.  
Cracked or Thickened Areas: friction or pressure, reflecting inner rigidity.  
Sensitivity or Itching: allergic reactions, toxin release, or emotional irritation.  
Pigment Changes: hormonal or energetic imbalances influencing vitality and self-expression.  
Slow Healing: low cell regeneration or blocked energy flow in the surface layer.
