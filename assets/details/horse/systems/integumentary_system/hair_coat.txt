#Hair Coat#

The horse’s hair coat forms a natural covering that protects the skin, regulates body temperature, and reflects overall health.  
It changes with the seasons, showing the body’s rhythm with nature.  
Energetically, the hair coat represents expression, sensitivity, and the aura’s outer reflection.

###Parts###

##Guard Hairs##  
The longer, outer hairs that shield the skin from rain and dust.  
Energetically, they represent protection and awareness of the environment.

##Undercoat##  
Shorter, softer hairs that insulate against cold and heat.  
Energetically, they symbolize comfort, nurturing, and emotional warmth.

##Hair Follicles##  
Small structures in the skin where hair grows, connected to nerves and oil glands.  
Energetically, they act as sensors for subtle energy, linking the horse’s awareness to its surroundings.

###Function###  
The coat regulates temperature, repels water, and assists in sensory communication.  
It also reflects nutrition and emotional well-being.  
Energetically, it mirrors the horse’s vibrational health and environmental harmony.

###Common Imbalances###  
Dull Coat: nutritional deficiency or energy stagnation.  
Excessive Shedding: hormonal imbalance or energetic release.  
Patchy Hair: skin irritation or emotional stress.  
Lack of Shine: dehydration or blocked life-force flow.
