##Location##  
The **mane** extends along the **crest of the neck**, from poll to withers.  
The **tail** is located at the **caudal end of the body**, extending from the dock (tail base) to the long flowing hairs.  
Both are attached to skin structures connected to the **dermis** and **hypodermis** layers.

##Shape##  
Composed of **long, coarse, keratinized hairs** growing from follicles embedded in the dermis.  
The mane grows vertically along the neck crest, while the tail grows downward from the dock.  
Each hair is thick, durable, and continually growing, offering mechanical protection and aiding in communication.

##Parts##

###1. Mane###  
- Row of long, thick hairs along the top of the neck.  
- **Function:** Protects the neck from insects and rain, aids in thermoregulation, and visually enhances expression.  

###2. Dock###  
- Muscular and bony base of the tail connected to coccygeal vertebrae.  
- **Function:** Provides movement and control for swishing and lifting.  

###3. Tail Hairs###  
- Long, coarse hairs extending from the tail dock.  
- **Function:** Repel insects and provide social and emotional signaling.  

###4. Hair Follicles###  
- Deep roots anchored in the dermis, connected to sebaceous glands.  
- **Function:** Allow continual hair growth and oil secretion for flexibility.  

##Function##  
- **Protection:** Repels insects and shields neck and hindquarters.  
- **Communication:** Expresses emotions such as irritation, relaxation, or excitement.  
- **Thermoregulation:** Aids cooling by swishing air around the body.  
- **Display:** Contributes to social and aesthetic signaling in herd dynamics.
