##Location##
Present throughout the **epidermis and hair follicles**, defining coat color and markings.

##Shape##
Varies by breed, age, and genetics, forming distinct coat colors and markings.

##Parts##

###1. Melanocytes###
- Pigment-producing cells in the epidermis.  
- **Function:** Produce melanin to color hair and skin and protect from UV radiation.  

###2. Hair Pigmentation###
- Color distributed within the hair shaft.  
- **Function:** Determines the coat shade and intensity.  

###3. Skin Markings###
- Patterns such as blazes, socks, or dapples.  
- **Function:** Provide individual identification.  

##Function##
- **Protection:** Shields against UV light.  
- **Identification:** Distinguishes individual horses and breeds.  
- **Communication:** Reflects genetics and health.
