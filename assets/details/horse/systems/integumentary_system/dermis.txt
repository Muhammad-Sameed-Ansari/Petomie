#Integumentary System – Dermis (Horse)#

##Location##
Situated **beneath the epidermis**, forming the thick middle layer of the horse’s skin.

##Shape##
Composed of **dense connective tissue** with blood vessels, lymphatics, glands, and sensory nerves.

##Parts##

###1. Papillary Layer###
- Contains capillaries and touch receptors.  
- **Function:** Nourishes the epidermis and supports sensory perception.  

###2. Reticular Layer###
- Deep layer with collagen fibers and elastic tissue.  
- **Function:** Provides strength, elasticity, and durability.  

###3. Hair Follicles and Glands###
- Distributed throughout the dermis.  
- **Function:** Anchor the coat and secrete protective oils.  

##Function##
- **Support:** Gives structure and elasticity to the skin.  
- **Circulation:** Supplies nutrients and regulates temperature.  
- **Sensation:** Contains touch and pain receptors.
