#Protective Structures#

Protective structures include hooves, chestnuts, ergots, and other keratinized formations that defend and stabilize the horse’s body.  
They serve as physical armor and support balance during movement.  
Energetically, they represent endurance, strength, and stability in life’s journey.

###Parts###

##Hooves##  
Thick keratinized coverings protecting the feet and aiding movement.  
Energetically, they symbolize grounding and connection to Earth energy.

##Chestnuts and Ergots##  
Remnants of evolutionary structures found on the legs.  
Energetically, they signify memory, adaptation, and ancient wisdom carried forward.

##Skin Calluses##  
Formed in areas of repeated friction for extra protection.  
Energetically, they represent resilience and the lessons of endurance.

###Function###  
These structures shield vulnerable areas and distribute weight evenly.  
Energetically, they connect to stability, persistence, and confidence in motion.

###Common Imbalances###  
Cracks or Chips: overuse, dryness, or emotional stress.  
Thickening or Overgrowth: stagnation or blocked grounding energy.  
Brittleness: nutritional deficiency or weakened vitality.  
Sensitivity: disrupted balance or emotional tension.
