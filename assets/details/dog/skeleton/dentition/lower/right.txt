#Lower Right Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the lower right quadrant of the dog's mouth, within the mandible (lower jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the lower central incisors) to the last molar on the right side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Small, narrow teeth used for grasping and scraping food.  
, Canine (1): Strong, pointed tooth designed for holding and tearing.  
, Premolars (4): Sharp-edged teeth used for slicing and crushing food.  
, Molars (3): Broad, flat-crowned teeth specialized for grinding and chewing.

##Function##
, Cuts, tears, and grinds food efficiently.  
, Balances with the left side for symmetrical mastication.  
, Maintains jaw alignment, bite strength, and oral stability.  
, Supports natural behaviors such as chewing, carrying, and play.

###Energetic Connection###
, Connected to the Stomach and Large Intestine meridians.  
, Represents balance between giving and receiving on the physical and emotional levels.  
, Tension or imbalance here can indicate digestive stress or difficulty letting go of old emotions.  
, Balanced energy in this area supports calm digestion, vitality, and emotional resilience.

##Notes##
A full adult dog typically has **42 teeth total**, with **22 in the lower jaw (11 right, 11 left)**.  
Healthy lower dentition ensures smooth chewing motion, strong digestion, and energetic grounding.
