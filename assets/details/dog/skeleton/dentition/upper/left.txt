#Upper Left Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the upper left quadrant of the dog's mouth, within the maxilla (upper jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the upper central incisors) to the last molar on the left side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Small front teeth for grasping and nibbling food.  
, Canine (1): Long, pointed tooth designed for holding and tearing.  
, Premolars (4): Sharp-edged teeth used for slicing and cutting.  
, Molars (2): Flat-crowned teeth for grinding and crushing.

##Function##
, Grasping, tearing, and crushing food.  
, Maintaining chewing balance and jaw alignment with the right side.  
, Supporting facial structure and muscle coordination.  
, Essential for grooming, carrying, and playful interactions.

##Notes##
A full adult dog typically has **42 teeth total**, with **20 in the upper jaw (10 right, 10 left)**.  
Balanced dentition ensures proper digestion, comfort, and energetic symmetry across both sides of the jaw.
