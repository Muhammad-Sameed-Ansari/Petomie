#Central Tarsal Bone#

##Location##  
Located at the **center of the hock (tarsus)**, between the **proximal tarsal bones** (talus and calcaneus) and the **distal tarsals**.  
It connects these layers, supporting weight transfer and joint articulation.  

###Shape###  
A **compact, cuboid bone** with several articular surfaces, providing multidirectional movement and stability.  

###Parts###  

##Dorsal Surface##  
Provides ligament attachment for hock stabilization.  
Energetically, symbolizes **structure and centered focus**.  

##Plantar Surface##  
Bears part of the body’s weight, aiding smooth motion during stride.  
Energetically, represents **grounded resilience**.  

##Articular Facets##  
Join with neighboring bones for flexibility and support.  
Energetically, reflect **adaptability and connection**.  

###Function###  
Serves as the pivotal connection between the upper and lower tarsal rows, distributing impact evenly during movement.  
Energetically, governs **stability, coordination, and harmony of motion**.
