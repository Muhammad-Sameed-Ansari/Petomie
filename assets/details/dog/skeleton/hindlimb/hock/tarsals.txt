#Tarsals#

##Location##  
Located in the **hind limb**, forming the **hock joint** between the **tibia/fibula** and the **metatarsals**.  
Composed of multiple small bones arranged in three rows for flexibility and strength.  

###Shape###  
A group of **irregular bones** designed to absorb impact and enable flexible motion.  

###Parts###  

##Proximal Row##  
Contains the talus and calcaneus, forming the upper hock joint.  
Energetically, symbolizes **foundation and power**.  

##Central Bone##  
Connects the upper and lower rows.  
Energetically, represents **balance and adaptation**.  

##Distal Row##  
Includes tarsal bones I–IV, articulating with the metatarsals.  
Energetically, reflects **precision and coordination**.  

###Function###  
Provide flexibility, shock absorption, and connection between limb and paw.  
Energetically, ensure **stability, motion, and grounded flow**.

**Balanced Energy:** Secure connection and steady progress.  

###Common Imbalances###  
Sprains, inflammation, or stiffness in the hock.  
Energetically, **resistance to flexibility or imbalance in movement**.
