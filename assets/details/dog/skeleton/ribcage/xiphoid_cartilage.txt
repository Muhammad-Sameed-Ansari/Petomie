#Xiphoid Cartilage#

##Location##
The xiphoid cartilage, or xiphoid process, is a small cartilaginous extension at the lower end of the dog’s sternum.
It marks the transition between the chest and the abdomen and serves as the attachment for abdominal muscles and connective tissue.

###Shape###
A flexible, tapering cartilage that may become partially ossified in adult dogs.

###Function###
Provides support for the lower sternum, anchors abdominal muscles, and contributes to the flexibility of the chest wall.
Energetically, it represents calm strength and balance between heart and willpower.
