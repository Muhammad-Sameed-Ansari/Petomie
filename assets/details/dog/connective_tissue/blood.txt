#Blood#

##Location##
Circulates throughout the cardiovascular system: heart, arteries, veins, and capillaries.

##Shape##
Fluid connective tissue composed of plasma and formed elements (erythrocytes, leukocytes, platelets).

##Parts##
Erythrocytes (Red Blood Cells) – Contain hemoglobin, transport oxygen and carbon dioxide.
Leukocytes (White Blood Cells) – Immune defense cells; include neutrophils, lymphocytes, monocytes, eosinophils, basophils.
Platelets (Thrombocytes) – Small fragments aiding in clot formation.
Plasma – Liquid component carrying nutrients, hormones, waste, electrolytes, and proteins.

##Function##
Transport oxygen, nutrients, hormones, and waste.
Maintain pH balance and temperature regulation.
Immune defense against pathogens.
Hemostasis (clotting) after injury.
