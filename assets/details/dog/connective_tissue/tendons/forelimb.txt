#Tendons – Forelimb#

##Location##
In the canine forelimb, tendons connect major flexor and extensor muscles of the shoulder, elbow, carpus, and digits to bones, enabling locomotion and weight-bearing.

##Shape##
Strong fibrous cords following the course of forelimb muscles, often enclosed in synovial sheaths.

##Parts##
Biceps Brachii Tendon – Crosses shoulder into intertubercular groove; stabilizes shoulder.
Triceps Tendon – Inserts on olecranon of ulna; extends elbow.
Extensor Carpi Radialis Tendon – Inserts on metacarpals II–III; extends carpus.
Common Digital Extensor Tendon – Inserts on distal phalanges II–V; extends digits.
Lateral Digital Extensor Tendon – Inserts on digits III–V; assists extension.
Superficial Digital Flexor Tendon – Inserts on middle phalanges II–V; flexes digits.
Deep Digital Flexor Tendon – Inserts on distal phalanges II–V; strong digital flexion.
Flexor Carpi Radialis Tendon – Inserts on metacarpal II–III; flexes carpus.
Flexor Carpi Ulnaris Tendon – Inserts on accessory carpal bone; flexes carpus.

##Function##
Transmit muscular force to bones of the forelimb, enabling weight support, paw placement, digging, and locomotion.
