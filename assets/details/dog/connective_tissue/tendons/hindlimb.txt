#Tendons – Hindlimb#

##Location##
In the canine hindlimb, tendons connect large propulsive muscles of the thigh and crus to bones of the hock and digits.

##Shape##
Dense fibrous cords, often merging into common tendons like the Achilles.

##Parts##
Achilles Tendon (Common Calcaneal Tendon) – Combination of gastrocnemius, superficial digital flexor, biceps femoris, semitendinosus, gracilis; inserts on calcaneus.
Quadriceps (Patellar) Tendon – Encloses patella, continues as patellar ligament to tibial tuberosity; extends stifle.
Cranial Tibial Tendon – Inserts on metatarsals I–II; flexes hock.
Long Digital Extensor Tendon – Inserts on digits II–V; extends digits, flexes hock.
Lateral Digital Extensor Tendon – Inserts on digit V; extends digit.
Superficial Digital Flexor Tendon – Inserts on middle phalanges II–V; flexes digits, contributes to Achilles.
Deep Digital Flexor Tendon – Inserts on distal phalanges II–V; powerful digital flexor.
Peroneus Longus Tendon – Inserts on metatarsals; flexes hock, everts paw.

##Function##
Transmit force of hindlimb muscles to bones, enabling propulsion, leaping, stability of the hock and stifle, and digit flexion/extension.
