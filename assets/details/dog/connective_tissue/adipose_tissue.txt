#Adipose Tissue#

In dogs, adipose tissue is a specialized connective tissue composed primarily of fat cells.  
It cushions organs, insulates the body, and stores energy reserves.  
Energetically, it represents **comfort, stability, and emotional nourishment**.

###Parts###

##White Adipose Tissue##  
Stores energy and provides insulation under the skin and around organs.  
Energetically, it symbolizes **security and support**.

##Brown Adipose Tissue##  
Found in puppies and near the shoulders and neck, it generates heat.  
Energetically, it reflects **vitality and transformation**.

##Connective Framework##  
A supportive network of fibers and blood vessels that nourish and anchor fat cells.  
Energetically, it represents **structure and balance**.

###Function###  
Adipose tissue protects organs, regulates temperature, and stores energy.  
Energetically, it maintains **grounding, comfort, and emotional stability**.

###Common Imbalances###  
- Excess fat: emotional protection or stagnation.  
- Fat loss: overexertion or lack of security.  
- Coldness: poor circulation or low vitality.  
- Energetic blockage: difficulty feeling safe or nurtured.
