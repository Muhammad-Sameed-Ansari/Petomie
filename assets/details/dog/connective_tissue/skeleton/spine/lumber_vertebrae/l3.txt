#L3#

##Location##  
Located in the lower back of the dog, between the thoracic and sacral vertebrae.  
L3 provides strength, flexibility, and support for movement of the spine and hindquarters.  

###Shape###  
A large, robust vertebra with a thick body and wide transverse processes for strong muscle attachment.  

###Parts###  

##Body##  
Supports weight and forms the main structure of the vertebra.  
Energetically, represents **stability and endurance**.

##Processes##  
Provide leverage for muscular attachment and spinal movement.  
Energetically, symbolize **resilience and adaptability**.

##Vertebral Foramen##  
Protects the spinal cord and allows nerve passage to the hind limbs.  
Energetically, reflects **flow and connection**.

###Function###  
The lumbar vertebrae support the lower spine, allowing bending, flexion, and extension.  
They stabilize the back and transfer energy between front and hindquarters.  
Energetically, they embody **strength, grounding, and the flow of life force through movement**.
