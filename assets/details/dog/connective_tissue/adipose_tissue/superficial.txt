#Superficial#

##Location##
Found directly beneath the skin within the superficial fascia, distributed across the trunk, limbs, and neck. Most visible in areas like the thorax, abdomen, and groin.

##Shape##
Lobulated masses of fat cells (adipocytes) embedded in loose connective tissue.

##Parts##
Subcutaneous Fat Pads – Beneath skin over ribs, neck, flanks.
Inguinal Fat Deposits – Common near groin area.
Tail Base Fat – Energy storage and insulation.

##Function##
Provides insulation to maintain body temperature.
Protects underlying muscles, nerves, and vessels.
Stores energy reserves for metabolism.
