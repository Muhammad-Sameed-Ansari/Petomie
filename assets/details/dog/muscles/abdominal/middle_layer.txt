##Location##
Lies between the deep and superficial abdominal layers, forming the central body wall on each side.  
Connects the ribs and pelvis to the midline.

###Shape###
Broad, diagonally oriented muscle sheets overlapping with other abdominal layers.  
They ensure strength and flexibility across the trunk.

##Parts##

###1. Internal Abdominal Oblique###
- Lies beneath the external oblique.  
- **Function:** Flexes, rotates, and stabilizes trunk; compresses abdominal cavity.  

###2. Iliocostalis###
- Extends along the ribs and lumbar area.  
- **Function:** Aids in trunk stability and lateral flexion.  

##Function##
- **Coordination:** Transfers motion between spine and limbs.  
- **Flexibility:** Enables trunk bending and twisting.  
- **Support:** Strengthens abdominal wall.  
- **Stability:** Maintains core posture.
