##Location##
Forms the **outer abdominal wall**, covering the sides and lower part of the ribcage.  
Responsible for visible body shape and trunk movement.

###Shape###
Broad, thin muscle sheets with fibers running obliquely from ribs toward the midline.  
Provides surface strength and mobility.

##Parts##

###1. External Abdominal Oblique###
- Largest and most external abdominal muscle.  
- **Function:** Compresses abdomen, rotates trunk, and supports posture.  

###2. Cutaneous Trunci###
- Thin muscle beneath the skin, over the flank.  
- **Function:** Moves skin slightly and assists trunk motion.  

##Function##
- **Protection:** Shields internal organs.  
- **Movement:** Enables lateral bending and rotation.  
- **Support:** Maintains abdominal tension.  
- **Coordination:** Works with deeper layers for balance.
