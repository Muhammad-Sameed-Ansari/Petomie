##Location##
Located along the **midline of the abdomen**, linking the left and right abdominal walls.  
Includes the **rectus abdominis** and **linea alba**, forming the central structure that supports the abdominal wall.

###Shape###
Composed of **paired, elongated muscles** running from the sternum to the pubis.  
The central seam (linea alba) unites both sides, adding strength and stability.

##Parts##

###1. Rectus Abdominis###
- Long muscle on the belly floor.  
- **Function:** Flexes the trunk, supports abdominal organs, and assists breathing.  

###2. Linea Alba###
- Fibrous seam connecting both sides of the abdomen.  
- **Function:** Provides reinforcement and serves as an attachment line for muscles.  

##Function##
- **Support:** Maintains posture and internal organ stability.  
- **Trunk Flexion:** Aids in bending the back.  
- **Protection:** Shields internal organs and reinforces abdominal wall.  
- **Breathing:** Assists diaphragm during expiration.
