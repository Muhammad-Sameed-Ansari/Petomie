##Location##
The deepest abdominal layer, located beneath all others and closest to the internal organs.  
Forms the foundation for trunk stability and posture.

###Shape###
Thin, transverse fibers forming a belt-like structure around the abdomen.  
Acts as a natural corset for support and compression.

##Parts##

###1. Transversus Abdominis###
- Deepest abdominal muscle layer.  
- **Function:** Compresses abdominal contents and stabilizes the spine.  

###2. Quadratus Lumborum###
- Between the pelvis and lumbar vertebrae.  
- **Function:** Stabilizes lower back and assists side bending.  

###3. Psoas Minor###
- Lies on the ventral surface of lumbar spine.  
- **Function:** Flexes lower back and helps maintain balance.  

##Function##
- **Core Stability:** Maintains spinal and pelvic alignment.  
- **Protection:** Supports internal organs under pressure.  
- **Balance:** Aids trunk stability during movement.  
- **Breathing:** Coordinates abdominal pressure during respiration.
