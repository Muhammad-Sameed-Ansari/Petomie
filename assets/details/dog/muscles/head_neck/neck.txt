#Neck Muscles#

##Location##
These muscles are found in the neck area, between the head and shoulders.
They help dogs move their head and neck, swallow, breathe, and support the headâ€™s weight.

##Shape##
The neck muscles are made up of long, flat, and layered muscles.
The outer muscles help move the head and shoulders, while the deeper ones keep the neck strong and stable.

##Parts##
###1. Brachiocephalicus###
Long muscle that runs from the head and neck to the front leg.
Function: Pulls the leg forward or bends the neck and head to the side.
###2. Sternocephalicus###
From the chest (sternum) to the head.
Function: Bends the neck and turns the head to one side.
###3. Sternohyoideus###
From the chest to the base of the tongue.
Function: Helps pull the tongue and throat backward when swallowing.
###4. Sternothyroideus###
Beside the sternohyoideus.
Function: Helps move the throat and voice box (larynx) backward.
###5. Omotransversarius###
Runs from the shoulder to the top of the neck.
Function: Pulls the front leg forward or turns the neck sideways.
###6. Trapezius (Cervical Part)###
Large triangular muscle at the top of the neck and shoulders.
Function: Lifts and moves the shoulder blade (scapula).
###7. Splenius###
Lies under the trapezius on the upper neck.
Function: Raises and extends the head and neck, or bends them to one side.
###8. Serratus Ventralis###
Fan-shaped muscle connecting the neck and ribs to the shoulder blade.
Function: Supports the body between the front legs and moves the shoulder.
###9. Longus Colli###
Deep muscle along the underside of the neck.
Function: Bends the neck down and helps stabilize the spine.
###10. Longus Capitis###
Lies in front of the longus colli, connecting the neck to the skull.
Function: Helps bend the head forward.
###11. Scalenus###
Connects the neck to the ribs.
Function: Helps with breathing by lifting the ribs during inhaling.

##Function##
Moves the head and neck: Helps the dog look around or bend its head.
Supports the head: Keeps the neck strong for balance and posture.
Swallowing and breathing: Moves the throat and larynx for swallowing and helps with breathing.
Moves the shoulders: Connects the neck to the front legs for coordinated movement.
