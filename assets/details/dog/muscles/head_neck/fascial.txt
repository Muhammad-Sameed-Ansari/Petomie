#Facial Expression Muscles#

##Location##
Found around the **eyes, nose, mouth, and ears**, attached mainly to the skin and superficial fascia.  
These muscles enable **facial movement and communication**, reflecting emotional and sensory responses.

##Shape##
Consist of **thin, flat, and finely controlled muscles** arranged around facial openings and features.  
They provide expressive mobility for eyelids, lips, nostrils, and ears.

##Parts##

###1. Orbicularis Oculi###
Encircles the eyes.  
- Closes eyelids and assists blinking  
- Protects and moistens the eyes

###2. Levator Nasolabialis###
Runs along the nose toward the upper lip.  
- Elevates the lip  
- Dilates the nostrils for breathing or alertness

###3. Zygomaticus###
From zygomatic arch to mouth corners.  
- Retracts lips and corners of mouth  
- Aids in producing “smiling” expressions

###4. Buccinator###
Forms the cheek wall.  
- Keeps food between teeth during chewing  
- Controls cheek tension

###5. Orbicularis Oris###
Encircles the lips.  
- Closes the mouth and controls lip shape  
- Important for barking and licking

###6. Platysma###
Superficial sheet over the lateral face and neck.  
- Retracts lips and mouth corners  
- Expresses tension or fear

###7. Frontalis###
On the forehead region.  
- Moves skin above eyes  
- Contributes to ear movement

###8. Mentalis###
Located on the chin.  
- Moves the lower lip and chin skin  
- Expresses discomfort or curiosity

###9. Auricular Muscles###
Surround ears and control ear movement.  
- Rotate and tilt ears for sound localization  
- Express alertness or submission

###10. Vibrissal Muscles###
Around muzzle and upper lip.  
- Move whiskers for sensory input  
- Aid in spatial awareness and tactile exploration

##Function##
- Allow **communication through expression**  
- Assist **feeding, grooming, and vocalization**  
- Move **whiskers and ears** for sensory awareness  
- Control **eye and lip motion** for protection and emotion
