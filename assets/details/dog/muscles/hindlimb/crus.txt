#Crus Muscles#

##Location##
Found between the **stifle (knee)** and **hock (tarsus)**, forming the muscular calf of the dogâ€™s hindlimb.  
Control the movements of the hock and digits.

##Shape##
Long and narrow muscles that taper into tendons extending toward the paw.  
Divided into cranial, caudal, and lateral compartments for controlled motion.

##Parts##

###1. Gastrocnemius###
- Large two-headed muscle forming the calf.  
- Extends hock and flexes stifle.  

###2. Soleus###
- Small muscle beside gastrocnemius.  
- Assists in hock extension.  

###3. Tibialis Cranialis###
- Located on the cranial aspect of the shin.  
- Flexes hock and rotates paw medially.  

###4. Peroneus Longus###
- Lateral muscle along the outer leg.  
- Flexes hock and rotates paw outward.  

###5. Extensor Digitorum Longus###
- Cranial muscle running to the digits.  
- Extends digits and flexes hock.  

###6. Flexor Digitorum Longus###
- Deep caudal muscle.  
- Flexes digits and extends hock.  

###7. Popliteus###
- Small triangular muscle behind the stifle.  
- Rotates leg medially and stabilizes stifle joint.  

##Function##
Flex and extend the hock and digits.  
Provide support, propulsion, and balance during walking, running, and jumping.  
Assist in precise foot placement and landing control.
