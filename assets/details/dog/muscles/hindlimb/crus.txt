#Crus Muscles#

##Between the stifle and hock##
###1. Gastrocnemius###
Two-headed muscle forming the calf.
Extends hock, flexes stifle, and supports stance.
###2. Superficial Digital Flexor###
Lies over the gastrocnemius.
Flexes digits and stifle, extends the hock.
###3. Deep Digital Flexor###
Deep muscle with three heads uniting into a common tendon.
Flexes digits and extends the hock.
###4. Peroneus Longus###
Superficial muscle on the lateral crus.
Flexes the hock and rotates the paw medially.
###5. Peroneus Brevis###
Lies deep to peroneus longus.
Flexes the hock.
###6. Tibialis Cranialis###
On the cranial side of the tibia.
Flexes the hock and rotates paw laterally.
###7. Long Digital Extensor###
Originates from the femur and extends to the digits.
Extends the digits and flexes the hock.
###8. Lateral Digital Extensor###
Small muscle lateral to the tibia.
Extends the fifth digit.
###9. Popliteus###
Small triangular muscle on the caudal surface of the stifle.
Flexes the stifle and rotates the tibia medially.

##Function##
Coordinate flexion and extension of the stifle and hock.
Control paw placement and assist with shock absorption and propulsion.
