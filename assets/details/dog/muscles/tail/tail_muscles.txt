##Location##
Extend from the **sacrum and pelvis** along the **caudal vertebrae** to the tip of the tail.  
Form the bulk of the tail musculature on the **dorsal, ventral, and lateral** aspects.

###Shape###
Composed of **longitudinal muscle bands** with repeated short segments attached to consecutive vertebrae.  
Arranged as **dorsal elevators**, **ventral depressors**, and **lateral stabilizers/flexors**.

##Parts##

###1. Sacrocaudalis Dorsalis Medialis###
- Deep dorsal band along the midline of the tail.  
- **Function:** **Elevates** and stiffens the tail (dorsiflexion).

###2. Sacrocaudalis Dorsalis Lateralis###
- Dorsolateral band on each side of the tail.  
- **Function:** **Elevates** and **abducts** the tail; assists lateral wag.

###3. Sacrocaudalis Ventralis Medialis###
- Ventral midline band beneath the caudal vertebrae.  
- **Function:** **Depresses** the tail (ventroflexion); helps tuck the tail.

###4. Sacrocaudalis Ventralis Lateralis###
- Ventrolateral band on each side.  
- **Function:** **Depresses** and **adducts** the tail; aids controlled curling.

###5. Intertransversarii Caudae###
- Short muscles spanning transverse processes between vertebrae.  
- **Function:** **Laterally flex** the tail and **stabilize** each segment.

###6. Coccygeus###
- From ischial spine to caudal vertebrae at the tail base.  
- **Function:** **Moves the tail base laterally**, supports the pelvic outlet, and stabilizes the root of the tail.

##Function##
Produce the **primary movements** of the tail: elevation, depression, lateral flexion, and controlled wagging.  
Stabilize each caudal segment for **precise positioning** during balance, communication, and locomotion.  
Assist posture at the **tail base** and help protect the pelvic outlet during motion.
