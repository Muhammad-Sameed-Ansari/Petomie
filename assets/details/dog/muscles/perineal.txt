#Perineal Muscles#

##Location##
Situated in the **pelvic outlet region**, between the **tail, anus, and external genital organs**.  
These muscles form the **posterior pelvic floor**, surrounding the anal opening and urogenital structures.

##Shape##
Composed of **thin, flat muscles** arranged around the anus and genital openings.  
They are closely associated with **anal glands** and connective tissue that stabilize the area.

##Parts##

###1. External Anal Sphincter###
- Encircles the anal opening.  
- **Function:** Provides voluntary control over defecation.  

###2. Coccygeus###
- Extends from ischial spine to base of tail.  
- **Function:** Moves the tail laterally and supports pelvic floor.  

###3. Levator Ani###
- Lies deep to coccygeus.  
- **Function:** Elevates the anus and supports rectal movement.  

###4. Bulbospongiosus###
- Surrounds base of penis (males) or vestibule (females).  
- **Function:** Aids urination and ejaculation (in males), and constricts vaginal opening (in females).  

###5. Ischiocavernosus###
- Covers the root of the penis or clitoris.  
- **Function:** Maintains erection by compressing venous return.  

##Function##
- **Defecation:** Contracts to close the anus and assists in rectal emptying.  
- **Urination:** Supports bladder emptying and closure.  
- **Reproduction:** Involved in mating and ejaculation.  
- **Tail Control:** Moves and stabilizes tail base.  
- **Support:** Maintains integrity of pelvic outlet and prevents prolapse.
