#Sperm Duct (Vas Deferens)#

##Location##
Extends from the tail of the epididymis, travels upward through the spermatic cord, and enters the pelvic cavity.
It joins the urethra near the bladder, forming part of the ejaculatory pathway.

###Shape###
A firm, smooth, muscular tube that propels sperm forward through rhythmic contractions.

##Parts##

###1. Beginning###
Connects directly to the tail of the epididymis.

###2. Middle Section###
Passes through the abdominal cavity within the spermatic cord, protected by surrounding tissues.

###3. End (Ampulla)###
An enlarged terminal portion that opens into the urethra.

##Function##
Transports sperm from the epididymis to the urethra during ejaculation.
Uses muscular contractions (peristalsis) to move sperm forward.
Stores small amounts of mature sperm before release.
