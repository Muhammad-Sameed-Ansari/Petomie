#Accessory Glands#

##Location##
Situated within the pelvic cavity, surrounding the urethra near the neck of the bladder.
In dogs, the prostate gland is the primary accessory gland, located just below the bladder and encircling the urethra.

###Shape###
Small, lobed glands that secrete fluid into the reproductive tract to mix with sperm and form semen.

##Parts##

###1. Prostate Gland###
The only major accessory gland in dogs.
Encircles the urethra near the bladder and produces a milky, nourishing fluid that supports sperm motility and longevity.
(Dogs lack seminal vesicles and bulbourethral glands, which are present in horses and other species.)

##Function##
Secretes fluids that nourish, protect, and transport sperm.
Aids in forming semen and helps it move smoothly through the urethra.
Neutralizes the urethral environment, ensuring sperm survival during ejaculation.
