#Mammary Glands (Male Dog)#

##Location##
Located along the **ventral surface** of the body, extending from the chest to the groin.  
Male dogs generally have **four to five pairs** of small, undeveloped mammary glands arranged symmetrically.

###Shape###
Flat, **nonfunctional glands** beneath the skin with small nipples.  
Covered by fine hair and connected by underdeveloped ducts.

##Parts##

###1. Mammary Glands###
Composed mainly of **connective and fatty tissue** with minimal glandular elements.

###2. Nipples###
Small, round structures on the ventral surface.  
Usually nonfunctional but may show slight changes with hormonal imbalance.

###3. Duct System###
Underdeveloped and inactive, with no capacity for milk production.

###Function###
No reproductive or lactational function.  
Remain hormonally inactive under normal testosterone levels.  
Occasionally enlarge due to hormonal imbalance or endocrine disorders.  
Represent **homologous structures** to the female mammary glands, showing shared embryonic development.
