#Trigeminal Nerve (V)#

The dog’s trigeminal nerve provides sensation to the face and mouth and motor control for chewing.  
It also carries sensory information from the whiskers and snout, key to navigation and expression.  
Energetically, it represents sensitivity, awareness, and grounded communication.

###Location###
Arises from the pons and divides into three main branches across the face and jaw.

###Parts###
Ophthalmic branch – senses the eyes and forehead.  
Maxillary branch – senses snout, lips, and upper jaw.  
Mandibular branch – controls jaw muscles and lower mouth.

###Function###
Provides facial feeling and chewing control.  
Energetically, it connects sensation with expression and physical awareness.
