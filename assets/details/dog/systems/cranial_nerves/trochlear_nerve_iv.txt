#Trochlear Nerve (IV)#

The dog’s trochlear nerve fine-tunes eye motion, allowing coordination when focusing on fast movement.  
It enhances stability and depth during play, hunting, or alert behavior.  
Energetically, it represents perspective and adaptability.

###Location###
Arises from the dorsal midbrain and supplies the superior oblique muscle of the eye.

###Parts###
Single branch – innervates the superior oblique muscle controlling rotation and downward gaze.

###Function###
Adjusts eye movement for coordination and precision.  
Energetically, it enhances fluid perspective and awareness from different angles.
