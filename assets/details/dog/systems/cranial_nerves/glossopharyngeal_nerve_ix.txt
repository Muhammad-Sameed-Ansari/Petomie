#Glossopharyngeal Nerve (IX)#

The dog’s glossopharyngeal nerve supports swallowing, taste, and throat movement.  
It connects nourishment, vocalization, and sensory perception.  
Energetically, it reflects trust, communication, and integration of physical and emotional intake.

###Location###
Emerges from the medulla and reaches the throat and tongue.

###Parts###
Lingual branch – controls taste in the back of the tongue.  
Pharyngeal branch – assists in swallowing.  
Tympanic branch – connects with the ear for sensory coordination.

###Function###
Regulates swallowing and taste, linking voice and nourishment.  
Energetically, it supports truthful communication and self-nurturing.
