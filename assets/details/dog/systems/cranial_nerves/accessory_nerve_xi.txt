#Accessory Nerve (XI)#

The dog’s accessory nerve controls head, neck, and shoulder movement, expressing confidence and alertness.  
It enables postural grace and responsiveness to sound or sight.  
Energetically, it represents courage, alignment, and strength.

###Location###
Originates in the medulla and spinal cord, extending to muscles of the neck and shoulders.

###Parts###
Cranial branch – assists in swallowing and vocalization.  
Spinal branch – powers neck and shoulder motion.

###Function###
Enables head movement, posture, and strength.  
Energetically, it enhances self-expression, alertness, and presence.
