#Oculomotor Nerve (III)#

The dog’s oculomotor nerve controls most eye movements and pupil constriction, maintaining visual focus and stability.  
It aids in coordination during motion and alertness to surroundings.  
Energetically, it represents focus, precision, and adaptability.

###Location###
Originates from the midbrain and travels to the eye socket, innervating most of the eye’s external muscles.

###Parts###
Superior branch – raises eyelid and moves eye upward.  
Inferior branch – moves eye inward and downward.  
Ciliary fibers – control focus and pupil contraction.

###Function###
Coordinates smooth eye movement and response to light.  
Energetically, it supports awareness, clarity, and direction of vision.
