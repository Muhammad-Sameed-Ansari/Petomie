#Integumentary System – Dermis (Dog)#

##Location##
The dermis lies **beneath the epidermis**, forming the thick, supportive layer of the dog’s skin.

##Shape##
Composed of **connective tissue** rich in collagen, blood vessels, glands, and sensory nerves.  
It provides elasticity and resilience to the skin.

##Parts##

###1. Papillary Layer###
- Upper dermal region with capillaries and tactile nerve endings.  
- **Function:** Nourishes the epidermis and detects touch.  

###2. Reticular Layer###
- Deeper layer with dense collagen fibers.  
- **Function:** Provides strength, flexibility, and anchors skin structures.  

###3. Hair Follicles and Glands###
- Found within the dermis.  
- **Function:** Support coat growth, oil secretion, and skin lubrication.  

##Function##
- **Support:** Provides strength, elasticity, and structural integrity.  
- **Sensation:** Contains nerve endings for temperature and pressure detection.  
- **Circulation:** Supplies nutrients and regulates temperature.
