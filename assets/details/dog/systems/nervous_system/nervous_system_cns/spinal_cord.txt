#Spinal Cord#

##Location##
The spinal cord runs inside the backbone from the brain to the lower back.  
It is protected by the vertebrae and surrounded by fluid that cushions it from bumps and pressure.

###Shape###
A long, tube-like cord made of nerves.  
It runs through the neck, chest, and back and has small branches (spinal nerves) that spread to all parts of the body.

##Function##
- Carries messages between the brain and the body.  
- Sends information from the skin, muscles, and organs to the brain.  
- Sends brain commands to the muscles for movement.  
- Controls reflexes, like pulling a paw away from something hot.  
- Helps the body stay balanced and coordinated.  
- Makes sure messages travel quickly and smoothly.
