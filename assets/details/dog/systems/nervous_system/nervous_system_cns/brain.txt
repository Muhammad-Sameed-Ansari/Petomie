#Brain#

##Location##
The brain is inside the dog’s skull and protected by bone.  
It connects to the spinal cord through an opening at the base of the skull.  
It is cushioned by fluid and soft layers of tissue called the meninges.

###Shape###
Rounded and smooth with three main parts:  
- **Cerebrum** – the biggest part, which controls thinking, learning, and voluntary actions.  
- **Cerebellum** – behind the cerebrum, helps with balance, posture, and smooth movement.  
- **Brainstem** – connects the brain to the spinal cord and controls breathing, heartbeat, and digestion.

##Function##
- Thinks, learns, and makes decisions.  
- Sends messages that move muscles and control actions.  
- Keeps balance and coordination.  
- Controls heart rate, breathing, and body temperature.  
- Stores memories and emotions.  
- Helps the dog respond to the world around it.
