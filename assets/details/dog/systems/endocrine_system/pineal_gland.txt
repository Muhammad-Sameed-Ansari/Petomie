#Pineal Gland#

##Parts##
###Pinealocytes###
Light-sensitive cells that produce melatonin, mainly during dark hours.

###Nerve Pathways###
Receive signals from the retina through the hypothalamus, helping the gland track day length.

###Capillaries###
Carry melatonin into the bloodstream to influence sleep and hormone cycles.

##Function##
The pineal gland regulates circadian rhythms, sleep-wake cycles, and seasonal timing.
Melatonin affects mood, body temperature, and energy levels, promoting rest and recovery.
It also helps synchronize hormonal cycles, making it important for dogsâ€™ adaptation to light changes (e.g., breeding, shedding).
An underactive pineal may lead to restlessness, anxiety, or sleep disturbances, while balanced melatonin supports calmness and recovery.
