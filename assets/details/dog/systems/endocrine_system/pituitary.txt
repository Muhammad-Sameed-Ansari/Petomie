#Pituitary Gland (Hypophysis)#

##Parts##
###Anterior Lobe (Adenohypophysis)###
Produces hormones that control other glands and major body functions.
Key secretions include:

Growth Hormone (GH): Stimulates growth, muscle repair, and metabolism.

Thyroid-Stimulating Hormone (TSH): Activates thyroid hormone release for energy control.

Adrenocorticotropic Hormone (ACTH): Signals adrenal glands to produce cortisol for stress and immune regulation.

Follicle-Stimulating Hormone (FSH) & Luteinizing Hormone (LH): Control sperm production, ovulation, and hormone cycles.

Prolactin: Promotes milk production after giving birth.

###Posterior Lobe (Neurohypophysis)###
Releases hormones made by the hypothalamus:

Oxytocin: Causes uterine contractions during birth and milk ejection.

Antidiuretic Hormone (ADH): Regulates body water by signaling kidneys to retain fluid.

###Infundibulum###
Connects the pituitary to the hypothalamus, transferring hormonal and nerve signals for precise control.

##Function##
Acts as the master gland that regulates most other endocrine glands.
Maintains growth, water balance, stress control, reproduction, and energy regulation.
