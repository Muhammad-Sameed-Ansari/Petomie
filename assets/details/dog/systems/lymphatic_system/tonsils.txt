#Tonsils#

##Location##
The tonsils in the dog are located at the back of the throat on each side, near the entrance to the pharynx.  
They are part of the ring of lymphatic tissue that surrounds the throat opening.

##Shape##
Small, oval masses of lymph tissue embedded in the mucous membrane.  
They are hidden within folds and can become visible when inflamed.  
Their surface is slightly rough and pink in healthy dogs.

##Function##
- Act as the first line of defense against bacteria and viruses entering through the mouth or nose.  
- Detect and respond to harmful microorganisms.  
- Produce lymphocytes and antibodies to fight infection.  
- Help prevent respiratory and digestive system infections.  
- Swell temporarily during immune responses to pathogens.
