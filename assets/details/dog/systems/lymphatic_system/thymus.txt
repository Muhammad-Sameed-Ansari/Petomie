#Thymus#

##Parts##

###Thymic Lobules###
Soft glandular tissue located in the upper chest, most active in puppies.
Energetically, they represent growth and transformation, guiding the development of strength and confidence.

###Cortex###
Outer layer where T-cells multiply and learn to recognize the dog’s own body.
Energetically, it symbolizes discernment and learning, teaching the immune system what belongs and what doesn’t.

###Medulla###
Inner region where mature T-cells are refined before being released.
Energetically, it reflects integration and mastery, preparing the dog for resilience and harmony.

###Thymic Hormones###
Regulate the immune system and maintain connection with the spleen and lymph nodes.
Energetically, they symbolize communication and unity, bridging immune balance and emotional well-being.

###Lymphatic Connections###
The thymus connects directly to the lymphatic system, sending mature immune cells throughout the body.
Energetically, this network mirrors heart-centered balance, joining courage with calmness.

##Function##
The thymus trains immune cells and strengthens the lymphatic network.
Energetically, it represents vitality and emotional resilience, allowing the dog to feel secure, open, and balanced.
