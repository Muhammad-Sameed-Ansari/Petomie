#Thymus#

##Location##
The thymus is located in the upper chest, in front of the heart and behind the sternum (breastbone).
It’s most active in young dogs and gradually shrinks as they mature.

###Shape###
A soft, lobed organ made of lymphatic tissue.
It appears pale and smooth in puppies and becomes smaller and more fibrous in adults.

##Function##
Produces T-cells, which are essential for the immune system.
Builds immune strength and vitality in young animals.
Helps connect the immune and endocrine systems.
Supports recovery and emotional balance during growth and stress.
