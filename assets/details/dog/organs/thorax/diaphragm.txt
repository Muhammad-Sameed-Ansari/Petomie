#Diaphragm#

##Location##  
The diaphragm separates the **thoracic cavity** from the **abdominal cavity**, attaching to the ribs, sternum, and lumbar vertebrae.  
It is the primary muscle responsible for breathing in dogs.

###Shape###  
A **dome-shaped, thin muscular sheet** that flattens during inhalation and relaxes during exhalation, aiding in respiration and pressure balance.

###Parts###  

##Central Tendon##  
Fibrous central portion where muscle fibers meet.  
Energetically, represents **unity and coordination**.

##Muscular Portions##  
Divided into costal, sternal, and lumbar parts.  
Energetically, symbolize **rhythm and flow**.

###Function###  
Draws air into the lungs by contracting and flattening.  
Assists in venous return, posture, and abdominal compression.  
Energetically, governs **breath, stability, and transition**.
