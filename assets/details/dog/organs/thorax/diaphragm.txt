#Diaphragm (Muscle)#

##Location##
The diaphragm is a thin dome-shaped muscle separating the thoracic cavity from the abdominal cavity.
It lies just below the lungs and heart, moving continuously with each breath.

###Shape###
Curved upward when relaxed and flattens as it contracts during inhalation.
It attaches to the ribs, spine, and sternum, linking the upper and lower body through movement.

##Function##
The main muscle for breathing — contracts to draw air in, relaxes to release air.
Helps in blood circulation and organ motion.
Coordinates the rhythm between breathing and heartbeat.
Influences posture and relaxation throughout the body.
