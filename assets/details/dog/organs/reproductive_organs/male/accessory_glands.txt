#Accessory Glands#

##Location##  
The accessory glands in the dog are located near the **neck of the bladder** and along the **urethra** within the pelvic cavity.  
Dogs possess only a **prostate gland**, while seminal vesicles and bulbourethral glands are absent.

###Shape###  
A **lobulated, walnut-shaped organ** surrounding the beginning of the urethra.  
It is soft and glandular, with ducts that open directly into the urethral lumen.

###Parts###  

##Right and Left Lobes##  
Symmetrical glandular sections producing prostatic fluid.  
Energetically, represent **balance and coordinated function**.

##Prostatic Ducts##  
Deliver secretions into the urethra.  
Energetically, symbolize **flow and release**.

###Function###  
Produces and secretes a clear fluid that nourishes and protects sperm, assisting in semen transport.  
Supports urinary and reproductive health.  
Energetically, governs **stability, vitality, and cooperative flow**.
