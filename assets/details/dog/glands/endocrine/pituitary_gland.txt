#Pituitary Gland#

The dog’s pituitary gland, also called the hypophysis, is a small oval structure at the base of the brain.  
It acts as the master gland, regulating hormone release throughout the body.  
Energetically, it represents alignment between body, mind, and higher consciousness.

###Parts###

##Anterior Lobe (Adenohypophysis)##  
Produces hormones controlling growth, thyroid, adrenal, and reproductive glands.  
Energetically, it symbolizes creation and manifestation through focused will.

##Posterior Lobe (Neurohypophysis)##  
Stores and releases oxytocin and vasopressin from the hypothalamus.  
Energetically, it reflects emotional connection and fluid balance.

##Infundibulum##  
The stalk connecting it to the brain’s hypothalamus.  
Energetically, it represents communication between intuition and action.

###Function###  
The pituitary gland controls growth, metabolism, reproduction, and water balance by directing other glands.  
Energetically, it aligns purpose with physical manifestation and harmony within the system.

###Common Imbalances###  
Overactivity: overstimulation, anxiety, or excessive drive.  
Underactivity: fatigue, apathy, or slow growth.  
Tumor pressure: hormonal chaos or emotional overwhelm.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect disharmony between mind and body or imbalance in self-leadership.
