#Von Ebner’s Glands#

The dog’s Von Ebner’s glands are small serous glands located at the back of the tongue, near the circumvallate and foliate papillae.  
They secrete watery fluid that cleanses the taste buds and enhances taste perception.  
Energetically, they represent clarity, sensitivity, and the enjoyment of life’s subtle flavors.

###Parts###

##Glandular Acini##  
Small sacs producing serous fluid rich in enzymes.  
Energetically, they symbolize refinement and awareness.

##Ducts##  
Tiny channels opening into the base of taste papillae.  
Energetically, they represent flow and receptivity to experience.

##Associated Papillae##  
Structures that house taste buds.  
Energetically, they reflect perception and sensitivity to truth.

###Function###  
Von Ebner’s glands flush and cleanse taste buds, helping detect new flavors.  
Energetically, they promote clear perception, curiosity, and emotional openness.

###Common Imbalances###  
Dryness: dull perception or disinterest.  
Infection: overstimulation or irritation.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect emotional numbness or blocked appreciation.
