#Floating Rib (Costa Fluctuans)#

##Location##
The 13th and last rib pair in the dog, located at the very caudal end of the ribcage.

##Shape##
Short and narrow rib with no sternal attachment â€” its costal cartilage ends freely in the musculature of the abdominal wall.

##Parts##
###Rib 13###
Single floating rib on each side
No ventral connection (free end)
Serves as an attachment point for abdominal wall muscles

##Function##
Provides additional muscle attachment area and partial protection for the cranial abdomen and diaphragm.
Allows maximum flexibility of the caudal thoracic cage.
