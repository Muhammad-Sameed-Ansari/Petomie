#Lower Incisors#

##Location##
Located in the mandible (lower jaw) at the very front of the mouth, between the lower canines. Dogs have six lower incisors, three on each side of the midline.

##Shape##
Small, narrow teeth with single roots. Lower incisors are slightly smaller and straighter than the upper incisors.

##Parts##
###Incisor 1 (Central Incisor)###
Medial-most tooth, closest to the midline
Smallest of the lower incisors
Used for grooming and delicate grasping
###Incisor 2 (Intermediate Incisor)###
Located between incisor 1 and 3
Slightly larger than incisor 1
Plays a role in pulling and scraping food
###Incisor 3 (Corner Incisor)###
Largest of the lower incisors
Positioned next to the lower canine
Helps lock into upper incisors to create a scissor bite

##Function##
Used for grooming (nibbling) and grasping small objects
Help create the scissor bite with upper incisors for cutting food
Assist in pulling meat or objects gently before the canines engage
