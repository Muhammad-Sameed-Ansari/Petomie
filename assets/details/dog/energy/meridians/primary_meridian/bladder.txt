#Bladder Meridian (BL)#

##Location##
Begins at the inner corner of the eye (BL-1), ascends over the head and neck, then runs in two parallel lines along both sides of the spine.
The inner line lies close to the spine, housing Back-Shu points linked to internal organs.
The outer line runs further lateral over the long back muscles and along the ribs and flank.
Continues over the sacrum, through the gluteal area, down between the hamstrings to the hock, and terminates at the lateral fifth digit of the hind paw (BL-67).

##Shape##
The longest meridian, forming twin energy channels from head to tail and down hind limbs.

##Function##
Regulates urinary and nervous systems
Connects to all organs via Back-Shu points
Releases spinal and muscular tension
Balances fear and relaxation

##Signs of Imbalance##
Back or hindquarter pain
Frequent urination or incontinence
Hindlimb weakness
Nervousness, anxiety, or startle reflex
