#Girdling Vessel (Dai Mai)#

The **Extraordinary Meridians** are deeper energy channels described in **Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)**.  
They act as reservoirs of **Qi and Blood**, regulating and balancing the flow within the Primary Meridians.  
In dogs, they create the energetic foundation for posture, movement, and coordination between upper and lower body.  
Each Extraordinary Vessel harmonizes Yin and Yang forces, maintaining internal balance and supporting the dogâ€™s adaptability and vitality.  

The **Girdling Vessel** encircles the body like a belt and governs **balance, stability, and integration**.  
It represents the ability to stay centered and composed, uniting all directions of movement into harmony.  
It embodies rhythm, coordination, and the confidence to act from a grounded state.  

###Shape and Pathway###  
Encircles the body horizontally at the level of the abdomen and lower ribs, connecting the front, sides, and back.  
This circular pathway links all vertical meridians, maintaining stability and structure in motion.  

###Function###  
Physically, it supports the abdomen, hips, and spine, ensuring balance and even weight distribution.  
It influences flexibility, muscular tone, and spinal alignment.  
Energetically, it governs emotional balance, self-control, and alignment between thought and action.  

###Common Imbalances###  
Weak topline, stiffness in the back or hips, loss of balance, or uneven movement.  
Energetic disharmony such as restlessness, overextension, or emotional instability.  
The dog may appear tense, uncoordinated, or reactive when moving or turning.
