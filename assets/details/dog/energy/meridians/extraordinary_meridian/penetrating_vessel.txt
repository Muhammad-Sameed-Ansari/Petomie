#Penetrating Vessel (Chong Mai)#

##Location##
Begins deep in the pelvic cavity near the perineum, close to the starting point of the Kidney Meridian.
Ascends through the abdomen, running internally alongside the ventral midline near the Conception Vessel.
Branches extend to the reproductive organs, stomach, and chest, connecting internally to the heart and diaphragm.
Some fibers descend into the hind limbs, linking the torso to the legs through the deep abdominal fascia and hip structures.

##Shape##
A deep, midline meridian traveling through the core of the body.
It is known as the “Sea of Blood,” connecting Yin and Yang channels and distributing life force through the organs and musculature of the trunk.

##Function##
Regulates blood flow and reproductive vitality
Connects upper and lower body, integrating movement and stability
Supports digestion and respiration from the body’s core
Balances emotional and physical strength
Promotes deep-rooted calmness and endurance

##Signs of Imbalance##
Hormonal or reproductive problems (heat cycle irregularities, infertility)
Abdominal tension, bloating, or poor digestion
Weak core muscles or postural imbalance
Unexplained fatigue or circulatory issues
Emotional instability or clinginess
