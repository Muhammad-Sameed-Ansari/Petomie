#Yang Heel Vessel (Yang Qiao Mai)#

##Location##
Begins at the lateral side of the hind paw just below the outer ankle (near BL-62).
Ascends along the lateral aspect of the hind limb, over the hock and outer thigh.
Continues upward along the flank, passes the ribcage and shoulder, then travels along the neck to the outer corner of the eye (BL-1).
Internally connects to the brain and influences muscle tone along the back and lateral sides.

##Shape##
A superficial, lateral meridian that mirrors the Yin Heel.
It governs activity, muscle tension, and reflex response, helping the dog maintain agility and coordinated movement.
Energetically, it supports alertness and readiness for action.

##Function##
Regulates Yang energy, movement, and coordination
Controls muscle tone, balance, and flexibility
Enhances alertness, reflexes, and eye coordination
Supports quick response and physical strength

##Signs of Imbalance##
Tension or tightness along the outer limbs or neck
Uneven gait, stiffness, or head tilting
Hyperactivity, restlessness, or poor sleep
Eye twitching or visual strain
Difficulty relaxing after excitement
