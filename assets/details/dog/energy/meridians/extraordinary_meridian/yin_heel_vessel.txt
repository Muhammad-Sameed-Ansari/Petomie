#Yin Heel Vessel (Yin Qiao Mai)#

##Location##
Begins on the medial side of the hind paw, just below the inner ankle (at the region of KI-6).
Ascends along the medial aspect of the hind leg, over the hock, inner thigh, and groin.
Continues upward along the ventral abdomen and chest, running parallel to the Conception Vessel near the midline.
Travels through the throat and ends at the inner corner of the eye (BL-1), connecting with the Bladder and Yang Heel Vessels.

##Shape##
A deep, medial meridian running from the inner hind paw up the body’s underside to the head.
It regulates rest, recovery, and the ability to anchor energy inward.
The Yin Heel balances the body’s left and right sides and influences posture during rest and lying down.

##Function##
Regulates Yin energy, sleep, and body fluid balance
Strengthens inner leg muscles and supports balance
Influences the throat, chest, and eyes
Encourages calmness, receptivity, and emotional grounding

##Signs of Imbalance##
Stiffness or weakness along the inner hind legs
Difficulty relaxing or maintaining sleep
Dry eyes, throat irritation, or swallowing problems
Reproductive or urinary irregularities
Excessive shyness or withdrawal
