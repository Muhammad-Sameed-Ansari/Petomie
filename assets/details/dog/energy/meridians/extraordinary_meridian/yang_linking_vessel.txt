#Yang Linking Vessel (Yang Wei Mai)#

##Location##
Begins at Bladder 63 (BL-63) on the lateral side of the hind limb.
Ascends the outer thigh and flank, joining the Gall Bladder meridian as it travels along the ribs and lateral chest.
Continues over the shoulder and neck, curves around the ear, and ends at the temple area (GB-13).
Internally connects with the Gall Bladder, Triple Heater, and all Yang meridians.

##Shape##
A lateral meridian connecting all Yang channels, regulating external energy and muscular activity.
It helps dogs adapt to changes in environment and movement, maintaining flexibility and readiness.

##Function##
Links and coordinates all Yang meridians
Regulates defensive (Wei) Qi and environmental adaptability
Supports mobility, coordination, and vitality
Balances alertness and relaxation

##Signs of Imbalance##
Stiffness or soreness in lateral body or hips
Tension headaches or eye strain
Alternating fatigue and restlessness
Over-reactivity to noise, light, or weather
Irritability or frustration after excitement
