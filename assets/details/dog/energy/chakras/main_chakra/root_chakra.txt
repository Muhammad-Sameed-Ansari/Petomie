#Root Chakra (Muladhara)#

###Location###
Located at the base of the tail, extending into the perineum and deep pelvic floor.
Anchors energy downward into the hind legs, paws, and lower spine.

###Color###
Red

###Element###
Earth

###Meaning & Function###
The Root Chakra forms the foundation of the dogâ€™s physical and emotional stability.
It governs survival instincts, grounding, and the ability to feel safe in the world.

##Connection##
The hind limbs, hips, pelvis, tail, and lower spine
The skeletal system, especially weight-bearing bones
The colon, rectum, and elimination pathways
The adrenal glands, influencing stress responses

###Signs of Balance###
Strong body awareness
Confidence in new environments
Calmness, trust, and the ability to relax
A healthy connection to routine, pack, and territory

###Signs of Imbalance###
##Emotional / Behavioral##
Nervousness, restlessness, hyper-vigilance
Fearfulness, insecurity, difficulty settling
Separation anxiety or clinginess
Startle responses, overreactivity, or shutdown behavior

##Physical##
Digestive issues (constipation, diarrhea, anal gland issues)
Weakness in hind legs or hips
Stiffness in tail or lumbar spine
Low vitality, adrenal exhaustion, chronic stress patterns

###Balancing Activities###
Regular exercise, slow walks, and time in natural environments.
Turnout or free roaming in safe spaces.
Calm, rhythmic grooming, gentle massage, and physical bonding.
Stable routines with predictable feeding, sleeping, and training.
Providing a safe, quiet resting spot and consistent pack structure.
Earth-based energy work such as placing hands near the tail base, or using earthy crystals (hematite, smoky quartz, red jasper).
