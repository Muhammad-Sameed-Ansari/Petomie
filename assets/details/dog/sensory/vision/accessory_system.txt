#Accessory System#

##Location##
The eyes are supported by external and internal structures that protect, clean, and maintain visual health.
These include the eyelids, eyelashes, third eyelid (nictitating membrane), lacrimal glands, conjunctiva, and extraocular muscles.

###Shape###
Eyelids: Thin movable folds of skin with small muscles for blinking.
Eyelashes: Short protective hairs along the upper lid.
Third Eyelid: A pink or whitish membrane in the inner corner of the eye.
Lacrimal Glands: Tear-producing glands near the upper outer eyelid.
Conjunctiva: Mucous membrane covering the inner eyelids and white of the eye.
Extraocular Muscles: Six fine muscles that coordinate eye movement.

##Function##
Eyelids and Eyelashes: Protect the eyes from dirt, injury, and dryness.
Third Eyelid: Cleans and protects the eye surface.
Lacrimal Glands: Produce tears for moisture, cleaning, and protection.
Conjunctiva: Guards against infection and keeps the eye moist.
Extraocular Muscles: Control eye direction and tracking.
