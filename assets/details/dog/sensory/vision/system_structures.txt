###Parts###

##Retina##
The light-sensitive layer at the back of the eye containing photoreceptors.
Converts light into neural signals.
Energetically, represents perception, awareness, and how reality is received.

##Rods##
Photoreceptor cells specialized for low-light and motion detection.
Highly developed in dogs for night vision.
Energetically, symbolize sensitivity, intuition, and awareness in darkness or uncertainty.

##Cones##
Photoreceptor cells responsible for color vision and detail.
Dogs primarily perceive blue and yellow hues.
Energetically, represent discernment, clarity, and focused perception.

##Optic Disc##
The point where the optic nerve exits the retina.
No photoreceptors are present.
Energetically, symbolizes transition and integration of perception.

##Optic Nerve##
Transmits visual information from the retina to the brain.
Energetically, represents communication, trust, and information flow.

##Optic Chiasm##
The crossing point of optic nerve fibers.
Allows coordination between both eyes.
Energetically, reflects balance, integration, and coordination.

##Optic Tract##
Carries visual signals from the optic chiasm to the brain.
Energetically, symbolizes direction and continuity of perception.

##Lateral Geniculate Nucleus##
Processes visual signals within the thalamus before reaching the cortex.
Energetically, represents filtering, prioritization, and interpretation.

##Visual Cortex##
Located in the occipital lobe of the brain.
Interprets visual information into meaningful images.
Energetically, symbolizes understanding, meaning-making, and conscious awareness.

###Function###
Receive light, convert it into neural signals, transmit visual information, and interpret the environment accurately.
Energetically, these structures support insight, awareness, orientation, and confident interaction with surroundings.

###Common Imbalances###
Vision loss, reduced night vision, neurological vision issues, or processing difficulties.
