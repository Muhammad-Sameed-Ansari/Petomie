#Social Environment#

###What It Is###
The social environment refers to the relationships, interactions, and social structures surrounding the animal. This includes contact with other animals, humans, herd or pack dynamics, bonding experiences, separation, leadership clarity, and social stability. Social environments strongly influence emotional safety, nervous system regulation, behaviour, and overall wellbeing. Animals are highly attuned to social cues and can be deeply affected by both harmony and conflict within their social surroundings.

###How It May Form###
Social environment stress may form through separation from bonded companions, frequent changes in herd, pack, or household members, overcrowding, bullying, unclear hierarchy, lack of companionship, forced isolation, inconsistent handling, mixed signals from humans, or unstable leadership. Stress may also arise when social needs are unmet or when the animal is placed in social situations that exceed its emotional capacity.

###Possible Effects###
Social environment stress may contribute to anxiety, attachment issues, fear-based behaviour, aggression, withdrawal, depression, excessive vocalisation, hypervigilance, shutdown, or changes in appetite and sleep. Some animals may become overly dependent, while others may disengage or display defensive behaviours.

###Energetic & Nervous System Impact###
Social stress can activate survival responses within the nervous system, particularly fight, flight, freeze, or fawn patterns. It may affect the heart space, vagus nerve regulation, emotional boundaries, and sense of safety. Long-term social stress can weaken resilience and interfere with emotional processing and trust.

###Support###
Support may include stabilising social structures, improving leadership clarity, gentle bonding work, gradual transitions, respecting social boundaries, emotional regulation support, nervous system balancing, and trust-building practices. Combining environmental awareness with energetic and emotional support often leads to lasting change.
