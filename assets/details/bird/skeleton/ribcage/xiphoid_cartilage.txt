#Xiphoid Cartilage#

##Location##
The xiphoid cartilage, or xiphoid process, forms the caudal tip of the bird’s sternum or keel.
It connects to the abdominal muscles and helps coordinate breathing and flight movements.

###Shape###
A flat, tapering extension of cartilage that may be bifurcated (split) or elongated depending on the species.
It remains flexible to assist movement of the lower sternum during respiration.

###Function###
Provides attachment for abdominal and flight muscles and contributes to chest expansion during breathing.
Energetically, represents calm confidence, grounded strength, and connection between breath and motion.
