#Humerus#

##Location##
The humerus is the upper wing bone of the bird, connecting the shoulder joint to the elbow.  
It forms the main structural support of the proximal wing and anchors major flight muscles, including those responsible for powerful downstrokes.

###Shape###
A strong, slightly curved bone with expanded ends for muscle attachment and joint stability.  
Built for both strength and lightweight efficiency to support flight.

###Parts###

##Head of the Humerus##
The rounded proximal end that fits into the shoulder joint.  
Energetically, symbolizes initiation, alignment, and empowered beginnings.

##Shaft##
The long central portion designed for strength, leverage, and attachment of flight muscles.  
Energetically, represents endurance, movement, and steady power.

##Distal Condyles##
The lower joint regions connecting to the radius and ulna at the elbow.  
Energetically, embody transition, cooperation, and coordinated action.

###Function###
Anchors major wing muscles used in flapping, gliding, and lifting.  
Supports wing movement, stabilizes the shoulder, and enables powerful flight strokes.  
Energetically, governs strength, momentum, and aligned action.
