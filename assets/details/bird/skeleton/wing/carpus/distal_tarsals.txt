#Distal Tarsals#

##Location##
The distal tarsals lie in the lower portion of the bird’s tarsal region, just above the fused metatarsal structure (tarsometatarsus).  
They form part of the ankle complex that supports balance and movement.

###Shape###
Small, irregularly shaped bones adapted for lightweight articulation and stability.

###Parts###

##Articular Surfaces##
Smooth regions connecting with the tarsometatarsus.  
Energetically, symbolize flow, grounding, and smooth transition.

##Joint Facets##
Areas that link with proximal tarsals or adjacent structures.  
Energetically, represent cooperation, alignment, and connection.

###Function###
Support flexion and stability in the ankle.  
Aid in distributing forces during walking, landing, and takeoff.  
Energetically, govern grounded action, coordinated movement, and balanced steps.
