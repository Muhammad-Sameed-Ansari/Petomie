#Tibiotarsus#

##Location##
The tibiotarsus forms the main lower leg bone in birds, extending from the knee to the ankle.  
It is created by the fusion of the tibia with several proximal tarsal bones, forming a strong, lightweight structure essential for movement.

###Shape###
A long, sturdy bone adapted for strength, shock absorption, and efficient locomotion.  
Slightly curved in many species to support landing and takeoff dynamics.

###Parts###

##Crest##
The upper ridge where major leg muscles attach.  
Energetically, symbolizes power, direction, and upward strength.

##Shaft##
The long central portion providing support and leverage.  
Energetically, represents stability, endurance, and flow.

##Distal Condyles##
The lower joint surfaces that connect with the tarsus.  
Energetically, embody grounded movement and flexible transitions.

###Function###
Supports weight during standing, walking, running, and landing.  
Provides leverage for powerful movement and stability.  
Energetically, governs strength, alignment, and steady forward motion.
