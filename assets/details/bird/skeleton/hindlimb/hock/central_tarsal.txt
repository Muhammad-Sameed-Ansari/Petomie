#Central Tarsal#

##Location##
The central tarsal bone lies within the tarsal region of a bird’s hind limb, positioned between the tibia and the fused tarsometatarsus.  
It contributes to ankle structure and movement.

###Shape###
A small, irregularly shaped bone designed for strength with minimal weight.  
It forms part of the interlocking tarsal complex that stabilizes the leg.

###Parts###

##Proximal Surface##
Connects with the tibia or other upper tarsal bones.  
Energetically, symbolizes support and upward flow.

##Distal Surface##
Articulates with lower tarsal or metatarsal structures.  
Energetically, represents grounding and direction.

###Function###
Supports weight transfer between the upper and lower leg.  
Enhances stability in walking, perching, landing, and running.  
Energetically, promotes balance, steady progression, and alignment.
