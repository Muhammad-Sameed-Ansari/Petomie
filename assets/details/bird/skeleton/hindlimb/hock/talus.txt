#Talus#

##Location##
The talus is one of the upper tarsal bones forming the primary joint between the lower leg (tibiotarsus) and the rest of the foot.  
It plays a key role in transferring force from the bird’s body to its limbs.

###Shape###
A compact, rounded bone with smooth joint surfaces designed for shock absorption and stability.

###Parts###

##Trochlea##
The upper joint surface articulating with the tibia.  
Energetically, symbolizes smooth cooperation and upward support.

##Articular Facets##
Surfaces connecting with adjacent tarsal bones.  
Energetically, represent connection, stability, and integration.

###Function###
Transfers force from the limb to the foot, stabilizes the ankle joint, and ensures smooth movement.  
Energetically, supports strength, resilience, and coordinated action.
