#Calcaneus#

##Location##
The calcaneus is one of the small tarsal bones located in the ankle region of the bird’s hind limb.  
It contributes to the structure of the tarsal joint and supports tendon movement connected to the lower leg.

###Shape###
A compact, rounded bone adapted to provide stability with minimal weight.  
Its shape varies slightly between species depending on locomotion style.

###Parts###

##Articular Surface##
The smooth area forming part of the tarsal joint.  
Energetically, symbolizes fluid cooperation and joint harmony.

##Tendon Groove##
A shallow channel guiding tendons across the bone.  
Energetically, represents direction, support, and flow.

###Function###
Supports joint structure, guides tendon movement, and aids stability during perching, walking, or landing.  
Energetically, encourages grounded motion, fluidity, and ease in transition.
