#Femur#

##Location##
The bird’s femur is the upper leg bone, connecting the hip (acetabulum) to the knee joint.  
It lies mostly horizontal within the body, contributing to the strong, compact structure needed for flight balance and locomotion.

###Shape###
A short, sturdy bone with expanded ends for muscle attachment.  
Its angled position supports both terrestrial movement and stabilization during flight.

###Parts###

##Head of the Femur##
The rounded proximal end that fits into the hip socket.  
Energetically, symbolizes connection, alignment, and core stability.

##Shaft##
The strong central portion providing leverage for major leg muscles.  
Energetically, represents endurance, strength, and forward momentum.

##Distal Condyles##
The lower joint surfaces that articulate with the tibiotarsus.  
Energetically, embody grounded action and smooth motion.

###Function###
Supports weight, enables walking, running, and controlled landings.  
Provides stability for the body during flight takeoff and landing.  
Energetically, governs strength, direction, and aligned movement.
