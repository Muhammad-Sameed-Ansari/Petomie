#Auditory Ossicles#

##Location##
The bird’s auditory ossicles lie within the middle ear, forming the connection between the tympanic membrane and the inner ear.  
Birds have a single ossicle called the **columella**, unlike mammals which have three.

###Shape###
A slender, rod-like bone adapted for transmitting sound vibrations efficiently.

###Parts###

##Columella##
The single auditory ossicle that conducts sound from the eardrum to the inner ear.  
Energetically, symbolizes clarity, communication, and resonance.

##Footplate##
The inner end that connects to the oval window of the inner ear.  
Energetically, represents transition, perception, and inner truth.

###Function###
Transmits and amplifies sound vibrations, enabling acute hearing.  
Energetically, governs clarity, intuitive listening, and sound awareness.
