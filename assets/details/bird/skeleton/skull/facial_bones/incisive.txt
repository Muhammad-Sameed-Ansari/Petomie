#Incisive (Premaxilla) Bone#

##Location##
Forms the **tip of the upper beak**, located anterior to the **maxilla** and surrounding the **nostrils**.  
It is one of the most prominent bones in the avian skull.

###Shape###
Paired or fused **elongated bones** forming the anterior upper beak.  
Houses the **beak tip** and supports keratin covering.

##Parts##

###1. Nasal Process###
- Extends toward the nasal bones.  
- **Function:** Supports the external nares (nostrils).  

###2. Palatine Process###
- Posterior extension toward the palatine bones.  
- **Function:** Forms the front of the hard palate.  

###3. Maxillary Process###
- Lateral connection to the maxilla.  
- **Function:** Integrates the premaxilla with the facial skeleton.  

##Function##
- **Feeding:** Shapes and supports the beak tip for grasping or filtering.  
- **Protection:** Surrounds the nasal openings.  
- **Structure:** Provides rigidity and form to the upper beak.
