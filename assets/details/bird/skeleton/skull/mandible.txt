#Mandible#

##Location##
The mandible forms the lower beak of the bird, articulating with the skull at the jaw joint.  
It plays a key role in feeding, grooming, communication, and environmental interaction.

###Shape###
A lightweight, elongated bone often divided into left and right halves joined at the midline.  
Its structure varies greatly depending on feeding adaptations.

###Parts###

##Rami##
The left and right halves of the mandible.  
Energetically, symbolize balance, duality, and unified action.

##Mandibular Symphysis##
The fused or flexible joint at the front where both halves meet.  
Energetically, represents unity, focus, and cohesive intention.

##Articular Region##
The joint area connecting the mandible to the skull.  
Energetically, embodies communication, strength, and precision.

###Function###
Enables pecking, cracking, holding, grooming, and vocalization.  
Plays a vital role in species-specific feeding behavior.  
Energetically, governs interaction, expression, and purposeful action.
