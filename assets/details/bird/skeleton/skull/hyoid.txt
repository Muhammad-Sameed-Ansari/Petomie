#Hyoid#

##Location##
The bird’s hyoid apparatus lies in the throat and supports the tongue and surrounding structures.  
It plays a critical role in swallowing, vocalization, and tongue extension—especially in species with long tongues.

###Shape###
A flexible, multi-part structure made of slender bones and cartilage.  
Extends backward in many birds, looping around the skull.

###Parts###

##Basihyal##
The central tongue-supporting bone.  
Energetically, symbolizes articulation and inner expression.

##Ceratobranchials##
Slender rods extending to the sides of the throat.  
Energetically, represent flexibility, flow, and adaptive movement.

##Epibranchials##
Elongated structures that may wrap around the skull in some species.  
Energetically, reflect extension, exploration, and curiosity.

###Function###
Supports tongue movement, swallowing, grooming, and vocalization.  
Energetically, governs expression, communication, and fluid articulation.
