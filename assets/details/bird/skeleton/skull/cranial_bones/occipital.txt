#Occipital Bone#

##Location##
Forms the **posterior and ventral part of the skull**, surrounding the **foramen magnum**.  
Connects the skull to the **first cervical vertebra (atlas)**.

###Shape###
Complex bone composed of **basioccipital, exoccipitals, and supraoccipital** regions.  
These may fuse to form a single occipital plate.

##Parts##

###1. Foramen Magnum###
- Large opening at the skull base.  
- **Function:** Passage for the spinal cord.  

###2. Occipital Condyle###
- Smooth articular surface below the foramen magnum.  
- **Function:** Articulates with the atlas vertebra for head movement.  

###3. Supraoccipital Region###
- Upper part forming the nuchal crest.  
- **Function:** Provides attachment for neck muscles.  

###4. Exoccipitals###
- Lateral portions flanking the foramen magnum.  
- **Function:** Support cranial nerves and vessels exiting the skull.  

##Function##
- **Support:** Connects the skull to the vertebral column.  
- **Protection:** Encases the brainstem and spinal cord junction.  
- **Movement:** Enables controlled head flexion and rotation.
