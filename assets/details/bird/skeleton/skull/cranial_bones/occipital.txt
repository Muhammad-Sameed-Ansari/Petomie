#Occipital#

##Location##
The occipital bone forms the back and lower part of the skull, surrounding the opening where the spinal cord enters.  
It connects the skull to the cervical vertebrae.

###Shape###
A curved, strong bone with a central opening and raised ridges for muscle attachment.

###Parts###

##Occipital Condyles##
Rounded surfaces that articulate with the first cervical vertebra.  
Energetically, symbolize transition, balance, and connection.

##Foramen Magnum##
The central opening for the spinal cord.
