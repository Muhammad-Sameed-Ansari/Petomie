#Scapula#

##Location##
The bird’s scapula is a long, blade-like bone that lies along the back, connecting the shoulder to the ribcage.  
It supports the wing and helps transfer muscular power during flight.

###Shape###
A narrow, elongated bone rather than the triangular mammalian form.  
Its streamlined shape is adapted for flight efficiency.

###Parts###

##Spine (or Ridge)##
A subtle ridge running along the bone’s surface.  
Energetically, symbolizes direction, structure, and balanced motion.

##Glenoid Region##
The joint area where the scapula articulates with the coracoid and humerus.  
Energetically, represents connection, strength, and coordinated action.

##Scapular Body##
The main elongated portion anchoring muscles.  
Energetically, embodies flow, continuity, and motion.

###Function###
Supports wing movement, stabilizes the shoulder, and anchors major flight muscles.  
Energetically, governs coordination, fluid action, and graceful expression.
