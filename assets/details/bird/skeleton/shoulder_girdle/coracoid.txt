#Coracoid#

##Location##
The coracoid bones are part of the bird’s shoulder girdle, positioned between the sternum and the scapula.  
They form a strong pillar that prevents the chest from collapsing during powerful wing strokes.

###Shape###
Long, thick, pillar-like bones built for strength and stability.

###Parts###

##Head##
The upper portion articulating with the scapula and clavicle.  
Energetically, symbolizes direction, support, and clear guidance.

##Shaft##
The long central section providing rigidity.  
Energetically, represents strength, structure, and unwavering stability.

##Sternal End##
The lower end attaching firmly to the sternum.  
Energetically, reflects grounding, anchoring, and secure connection.

###Function###
Supports the shoulder joint and absorbs the forces of flight.  
Prevents chest compression during wing movement.  
Energetically, governs strength, stability, and safe expression of power.
