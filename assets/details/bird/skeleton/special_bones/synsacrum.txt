#Synsacrum#

##Location##
The synsacrum lies in the center of the bird’s pelvis, formed by the fusion of thoracic, lumbar, sacral, and some caudal vertebrae.  
It connects the spine firmly to the pelvis.

###Shape###
A long, fused bony plate providing exceptional rigidity and strength.

###Parts###

##Fused Vertebrae##
Multiple vertebrae forming a single stable structure.  
Energetically, symbolize unity, strength, and steadfast support.

##Articulation Points##
Regions where the synsacrum connects to the pelvis.  
Energetically, represent grounding, stability, and integration.

###Function###
Provides support for landing, walking, and powerful leg motions.  
Offers rigidity essential for flight mechanics.  
Energetically, governs strength, grounded presence, and structural integrity.
