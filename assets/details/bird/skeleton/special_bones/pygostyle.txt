#Pygostyle#

##Location##
The pygostyle is located at the base of the bird’s tail, formed by the fusion of the final caudal vertebrae.  
It anchors the tail feathers and supports tail movement.

###Shape###
A compact, triangular or block-like bone designed for strength and efficient feather attachment.

###Parts###

##Caudal Fusion Mass##
The fused vertebral block forming the pygostyle.  
Energetically, symbolizes stability, grounding, and direction.

##Feather Attachment Surface##
The area where major tail feathers anchor.  
Energetically, represents balance, guidance, and energetic alignment.

###Function###
Supports tail movement for steering, braking, and balance during flight.  
Holds tail feathers in place for communication and maneuvering.  
Energetically, governs direction, grounding, and balanced movement.
