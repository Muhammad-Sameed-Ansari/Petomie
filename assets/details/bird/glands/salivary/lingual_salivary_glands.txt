#Lingual Salivary Glands#

The bird’s lingual salivary glands lie within the tongue and its surrounding tissues.  
They produce mucus-rich secretions that help moisten food and aid swallowing.  
Energetically, they represent expression, flexibility, and ease in communication.

###Parts###

##Glandular Cells##  
Produce mucus to lubricate food.  
Energetically, they symbolize softness and fluidity.

##Ducts##  
Carry secretions into the mouth.  
Energetically, they reflect flow and gentle movement.

##Supportive Tissue##  
Anchors the glands within the tongue.  
Energetically, it represents grounding and inner stability.

###Function###  
These glands lubricate food, assist swallowing, and protect the tongue’s surface.  
Energetically, they support smooth communication and emotional ease.

###Common Imbalances###  
Dryness: dehydration or tension.  
Irritation: rough food or stress.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect difficulty expressing needs.
