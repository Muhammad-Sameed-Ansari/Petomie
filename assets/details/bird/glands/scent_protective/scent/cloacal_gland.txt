#Cloacal Gland#

The bird’s cloacal gland, present in certain songbirds, produces pheromones used for mate attraction and social communication.  
Energetically, it represents **expression, attraction, and identity**.

###Parts###

##Secretory Cells##  
Produce species‑specific pheromones.  
Energetically symbolize **magnetism and self‑expression**.

##Cloacal Crypts##  
Release scent during posture or movement.  
Energetically represent **communication and intention**.

##Supportive Tissue##  
Maintains shape and function.  
Energetically reflects **stability and confidence**.

###Function###  
Used in mate attraction, territory communication, and social signaling.  
Energetically supports **presence, connection, and expressive clarity**.

###Common Imbalances###  
Reduced activity — linked to **withdrawal or low confidence**.  
Overactivity — associated with **stress or overstimulation**.
