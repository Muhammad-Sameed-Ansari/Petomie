#Oviduct#

##Location##
The oviduct is a long, coiled reproductive tube extending from the ovary to the cloaca.
It occupies much of the left abdominal cavity and connects the ovary to the cloaca for egg formation and laying.

###Shape###
A flexible, tubular structure with distinct functional regions.
Its walls are muscular and glandular, adapting to each stage of egg development.

###Parts###

##Infundibulum##
Receives the ovulated yolk from the ovary and is the site of fertilization.
Energetically, represents invitation and receptivity.

##Magnum##
Secretes the majority of the egg white (albumen).
Energetically, symbolizes nourishment and protection.

##Isthmus##
Forms the inner and outer shell membranes.
Energetically, reflects structure and balance.

##Uterus (Shell Gland)##
Deposits the hard eggshell and pigments.
Energetically, represents manifestation and completion.

##Vaginal Segment##
Final muscular section leading to the cloaca, guiding egg expulsion.
Energetically, represents release and transition.

###Function###
Guides the egg through sequential stages of formation and maturation.
Ensures fertilization, nourishment, shell formation, and safe egg laying.
Energetically, governs creation, flow, and completion cycles.
