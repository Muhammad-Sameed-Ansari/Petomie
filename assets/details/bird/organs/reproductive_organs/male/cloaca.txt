#Cloaca#

##Location##
Located at the **posterior ventral end** of the bird’s body, beneath the tail base.  
The cloaca is a **shared chamber** where the **digestive, urinary, and reproductive tracts** open into one passage.  
It ends externally at the **vent**.

###Shape###
A **short, expandable muscular chamber** with folded inner lining.  
Its size and tone vary by species, sex, and breeding season.

###Parts###
**Coprodeum** (fecal chamber), receives material from the large intestine.  
**Urodeum** (urinary chamber), receives urine/urates from the ureters and reproductive openings.  
**Proctodeum** (terminal chamber), leads to the vent and supports mating contact.  
**Vent** (external opening), the outward exit point of the cloaca.  
**Cloacal sphincter muscles**, control opening and closing.

###Function###
Eliminates **feces and urates** through a single exit.  
Supports **reproduction**: during mating, many birds transfer sperm via brief cloacal contact (“cloacal kiss”).  
Helps regulate **hydration and salt balance** through urate excretion.  
Energetically, it represents **release, cleansing, and the balance between giving and receiving**.

###Energetic Connection###
Connected with **root and sacral energies**, governing grounding, vitality, survival, and creative life flow.  
Also linked to **letting go** (release of waste, old patterns, emotional buildup).

###Common Imbalances###
Cloacitis (inflammation), irritation, infection.  
Prolapse (tissue protrusion), especially with strain, stress, or egg-laying issues.  
Blockage/impaction, dehydration-related urate buildup.  
Energetically, imbalance may show as **difficulty releasing**, shame, fear around intimacy, or feeling “stuck” in survival stress.

Gentle **grounding** (barefoot on earth, slow breathing, body awareness).
