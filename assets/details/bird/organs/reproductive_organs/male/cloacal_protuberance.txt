#Cloacal Protuberance#

##Location##
Located at the **dorsal (upper) margin of the cloaca/vent** in many **male birds**, most noticeable during the **breeding season**.  
It sits just beneath the tail base and appears as a **rounded swelling** around or above the vent.

###Shape###
A **soft, rounded bulge** that can range from subtle to prominent depending on species, hormones, and breeding condition.  
It is typically more obvious in **passerines (songbirds)** and some other breeding males.

###Parts###
**Swollen cloacal tissues**, creating external prominence.  
**Seminal storage/transport region** (species-dependent), supporting sperm presence near the vent.  
**Surrounding sphincter and supportive muscles**, maintaining tone and function.
