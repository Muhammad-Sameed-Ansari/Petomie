#Gallbladder#

##Location##  
In most birds, the gallbladder is located on the **right side of the liver**, attached to its caudal surface.  
Some species, such as pigeons, lack a gallbladder entirely.  

###Shape###  
A **small, pear-shaped sac** with a thin muscular wall for bile storage.  

###Parts###  

##Fundus##  
Rounded end that stores bile.  
Energetically, represents **patience and stillness**.  

##Body##  
Central chamber that holds bile fluid.  
Energetically, symbolizes **containment and readiness**.  

##Neck and Ducts##  
Connect to the bile duct and duodenum.  
Energetically, reflect **communication and release**.  

###Function###  
Stores bile produced by the liver and releases it into the duodenum to aid fat digestion.  
Energetically, governs **decision-making, flow, and emotional balance**.
