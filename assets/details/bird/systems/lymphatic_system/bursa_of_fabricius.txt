#Bursa of Fabricius#

##Location##
The bursa of Fabricius is located dorsally near the cloaca, unique to birds.  
It is a small, pouch-like organ that is most active in young birds and regresses with age.

###Shape###
A rounded, hollow sac with internal folds that increase immune-surface area.

###Parts###

##Mucosal Folds (Plicae)##
Internal ridges filled with developing B-cells.  
Energetically, symbolize **growth, learning, and inner expansion**.

##Follicles##
Clusters where B-cells mature and differentiate.  
Energetically, represent **identity, refinement, and emotional clarity**.

##Cloacal Opening##
Connects the bursa to the digestive-urogenital passage.  
Energetically, embodies **exchange, transition, and purification**.

###Function###
Responsible for B-cell maturation and immune system development in young birds.  
Plays a critical role in building long-term immune memory.  
Energetically, governs **identity formation, emotional resilience, and inner wisdom**.
