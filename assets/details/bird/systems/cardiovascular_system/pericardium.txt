#Pericardium#

##Location##
The pericardium in birds is a thin protective membrane surrounding the heart.  
It lies within the thoracic cavity, anchoring the heart while allowing smooth movement during each beat.

###Shape###
A delicate, double-layered sac containing a small amount of lubricating fluid.

###Parts###

##Fibrous Layer##
The outer protective covering providing structural support.  
Energetically, symbolizes boundaries, protection, and containment.

##Serous Layer##
Inner membrane reducing friction as the heart beats.  
Energetically, represents ease, flow, and emotional cushioning.

##Pericardial Cavity##
The space containing lubricating fluid.  
Energetically, embodies spaciousness, calm, and emotional softness.

###Function###
Protects the heart, reduces friction, stabilizes position, and prevents overexpansion.  
Energetically, governs emotional boundaries, protection, and heart-centered balance.
