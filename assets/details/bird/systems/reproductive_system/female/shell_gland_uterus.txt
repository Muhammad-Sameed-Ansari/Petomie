#Shell Gland (Uterus)#

##Location##
Between the **isthmus** and **vagina**, near the cloaca.  
Forms the thickest, most glandular part of the oviduct.

###Shape###
Short, saclike organ with thick muscular walls and calcium-secreting glands.  
Inner surface folded to increase surface area.

##Parts##
###1. Glandular Lining###
Rich in calcium-secreting cells.  
**Function:** Forms hard calcareous eggshell.

###2. Pigment Cells###
Present in many species.  
**Function:** Add coloration and patterns to shell.

###3. Muscular Wall###
Thick and elastic.  
**Function:** Helps push egg toward vagina.

##Function##
- **Shell Formation:** Deposits calcium carbonate for hard shell.  
- **Pigmentation:** Adds unique color patterns.  
- **Water Regulation:** Adjusts shell permeability.  
- **Retention:** Holds egg until complete shell formation.
