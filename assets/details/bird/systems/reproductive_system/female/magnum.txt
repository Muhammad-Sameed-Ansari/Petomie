#Magnum#

##Location##
Second and longest section of the **oviduct**, located between the infundibulum and isthmus.

###Shape###
Thick-walled, glandular, and highly coiled tube about 30â€“40 cm long in hens.  
Inner surface lined with secretory cells.

##Parts##
###1. Glandular Epithelium###
Contains albumen-secreting glands.  
**Function:** Produces layers of egg white around yolk.

###2. Ciliated Cells###
Assist in egg movement.  
**Function:** Propel egg to next section.

###3. Connective and Muscular Layers###
Provide structure and peristalsis.  
**Function:** Move egg smoothly through tract.

##Function##
- **Albumen Secretion:** Adds thick and thin egg white around yolk.  
- **Protection:** Cushions yolk during movement.  
- **Nutrient Supply:** Provides proteins for embryonic nourishment.  
- **Transport:** Moves egg toward isthmus.
