#Pituitary Gland (Hypophysis)#

##Location##
Situated at the **base of the brain**, beneath the hypothalamus, connected by the infundibulum.  
Enclosed within the **sella turcica** of the sphenoid bone.

###Shape###
A small, oval, reddish gland divided into anterior and posterior lobes.  
Highly vascular to allow hormone exchange with the hypothalamus.

##Parts##
###1. Anterior Lobe (Adenohypophysis)###
Produces hormones controlling other glands.  
**Function:** Secretes growth hormone, TSH, ACTH, LH, FSH, and prolactin.

###2. Posterior Lobe (Neurohypophysis)###
Stores and releases hormones from hypothalamus.  
**Function:** Secretes oxytocin and vasotocin for water balance and reproduction.

###3. Infundibulum###
Stalk connecting pituitary to hypothalamus.  
**Function:** Transmits neurohormones and signals.

##Function##
- **Regulation:** Controls thyroid, adrenal, and gonadal activity.  
- **Growth:** Influences metabolism and development.  
- **Reproduction:** Coordinates reproductive cycles.  
- **Homeostasis:** Maintains water and hormonal balance.
