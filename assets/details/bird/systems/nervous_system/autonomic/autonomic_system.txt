#Autonomic Nervous System#

##Location##
The autonomic nervous system (ANS) extends throughout the bird’s entire body, regulating involuntary functions in the heart, lungs, digestive tract, glands, and smooth muscles.  
It operates through sympathetic, parasympathetic, and enteric components.

###Shape###
A distributed network of nerves and ganglia forming two chains along the spine and extensive branches within organs.

###Parts###

##Sympathetic Division##
Activates alertness, flight reactions, and energy mobilization.  
Energetically, symbolizes **strength, protection, and decisive action**.

##Parasympathetic Division##
Promotes rest, digestion, and internal repair.  
Energetically, represents **calmness, renewal, and emotional softness**.

##Enteric Division##
Controls digestive motility and secretions.  
Energetically, embodies **intuition, inner processing, and emotional digestion**.

###Function###
Regulates heart rate, respiration, digestion, glandular activity, circulation, and internal balance.  
Energetically, governs **emotional regulation, internal rhythm, and harmony**.
