#Vagus Nerve (Cranial Nerve X)#

##Location##
Originates from the **medulla oblongata** and travels down both sides of the neck beside the **common carotid arteries**, continuing to thoracic and abdominal organs.

###Shape###
A long mixed (motor, sensory, and parasympathetic) nerve branching extensively through the thorax and abdomen.

##Parts##
###1. Cervical Branches###
Supply neck muscles and glands.  
**Function:** Assist in swallowing and vocalization.

###2. Thoracic Branches###
Form cardiac and pulmonary plexuses.  
**Function:** Slow heart rate and regulate respiration.

###3. Abdominal Branches###
Reach the stomach, liver, and intestines.  
**Function:** Stimulate digestion and peristalsis.

###4. Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve###
Loops around major arteries.  
**Function:** Controls syrinx and vocal muscles for sound production.

##Function##
- **Parasympathetic Control:** Slows heart rate and promotes digestion.  
- **Respiratory Regulation:** Coordinates breathing rhythm.  
- **Digestive Activation:** Enhances glandular secretion and motility.  
- **Vocal Function:** Innervates syrinx for vocalization.
