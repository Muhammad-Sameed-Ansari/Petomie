##Location##
Emerge directly from the **brain and brainstem**, exiting through openings in the skull to reach the head, neck, and internal organs.

###Shape###
Twelve paired nerves, each with distinct sensory, motor, or mixed functions.  
Arranged in sequential order from the forebrain to the medulla oblongata.

##Parts##
###1. Olfactory Nerve (I)###
**Function:** Smell and chemical detection.

###2. Optic Nerve (II)###
**Function:** Vision; transmits retinal information to the optic lobes.

###3. Oculomotor Nerve (III)###
**Function:** Eye movement and pupil constriction.

###4. Trochlear Nerve (IV)###
**Function:** Controls dorsal oblique muscle for eye rotation.

###5. Trigeminal Nerve (V)###
**Function:** Sensory input from face and beak; motor control for jaw muscles.

###6. Abducens Nerve (VI)###
**Function:** Controls lateral rectus muscle for eye abduction.

###7. Facial Nerve (VII)###
**Function:** Facial muscles, taste, and gland secretion.

###8. Vestibulocochlear Nerve (VIII)###
**Function:** Hearing and balance via inner ear.

###9. Glossopharyngeal Nerve (IX)###
**Function:** Taste, salivation, and pharyngeal control.

###10. Vagus Nerve (X)###
**Function:** Parasympathetic control of heart, lungs, and digestion.

###11. Accessory Nerve (XI)###
**Function:** Neck muscle movement and respiratory assistance.

###12. Hypoglossal Nerve (XII)###
**Function:** Tongue and syrinx muscle movement.

##Function##
- **Sensory Input:** Vision, hearing, taste, smell, balance.  
- **Motor Output:** Controls muscles of eyes, jaw, tongue, and neck.  
- **Visceral Regulation:** Governs organs through autonomic fibers.  
- **Communication:** Enables vocalization and feeding coordination.
