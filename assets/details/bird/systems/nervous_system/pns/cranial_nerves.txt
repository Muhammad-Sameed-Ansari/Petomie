#Cranial Nerves#

##Location##
The cranial nerves originate directly from the bird’s brain—primarily the brainstem—and exit through openings in the skull to reach sensory organs, muscles, and glands.

###Shape###
Twelve paired nerves, each with a distinct pathway and specialized function, forming a radiating network from the brain.

###Parts###

##Olfactory Nerve (I)##
Sense of smell.  
Energetically: intuition, instinct, subtle sensing.

##Optic Nerve (II)##
Vision and light perception.  
Energetically: clarity, insight, inner vision.

##Oculomotor Nerve (III)##
Controls most eye movements.  
Energetically: focus and direction.

##Trochlear Nerve (IV)##
Controls a single eye muscle for precision.  
Energetically: refinement and subtle adjustment.

##Trigeminal Nerve (V)##
Facial sensation and jaw muscles.  
Energetically: boundaries and communication.

##Abducent Nerve (VI)##
Moves the eye laterally.  
Energetically: perspective and openness.

##Facial Nerve (VII)##
Facial expression, glands, taste.  
Energetically: expression, emotional tone.

##Vestibulocochlear Nerve (VIII)##
Balance and hearing.  
Energetically: equilibrium and inner listening.

##Glossopharyngeal Nerve (IX)##
Throat function, taste, swallowing.  
Energetically: truth, integration.

##Vagus Nerve (X)##
Heart, lungs, digestion.  
Energetically: calmness, emotional flow, grounding.

##Accessory Nerve (XI)##
Neck and wing stabilizer muscles.  
Energetically: support and empowerment.

##Hypoglossal Nerve (XII)##
Tongue movement.  
Energetically: expression and communication.

###Function###
Controls sensory input, movement of eyes, tongue, throat, facial structures, and internal organs.  
Energetically, governs perception, expression, and emotional alignment.
