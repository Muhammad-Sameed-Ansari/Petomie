#Larynx#

##Location##
The bird’s larynx is located at the top of the trachea, sitting just behind the tongue at the floor of the oropharynx.  
Unlike mammals, it does not produce sound—its primary role is airway protection.

###Shape###
A small cartilaginous structure forming a triangular opening that regulates airflow into the trachea.

###Parts###

##Glottis##
The slit-like opening into the trachea.  
Energetically: symbolizes **breath entry, choice, and opening**.

##Cartilaginous Supports##
Keep the airway open.  
Energetically: represent **structure and stability**.

###Function###
Regulates airflow, protects the airway during swallowing, and supports calm breathing.  
Energetically, governs **airway clarity, expression, and inner calm**.
