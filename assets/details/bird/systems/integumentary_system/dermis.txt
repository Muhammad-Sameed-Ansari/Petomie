#Integumentary System – Dermis (Bird)#

##Location##
Situated **beneath the epidermis**, forming the main structural layer of the bird’s skin.

##Shape##
A **dense connective tissue layer** rich in blood vessels, nerves, and muscles that move feathers.

##Parts##

###1. Papillary Layer###
- Upper layer of dermis with feather follicles.  
- **Function:** Anchors feathers and supplies nutrients to the epidermis.  

###2. Reticular Layer###
- Deep layer with collagen and elastic fibers.  
- **Function:** Provides strength, elasticity, and support.  

###3. Smooth Muscle Fibers###
- Found near feather bases.  
- **Function:** Allow movement and positioning of feathers for insulation or display.  

##Function##
- **Support:** Provides structure and elasticity to the skin.  
- **Feather control:** Enables movement for thermoregulation and communication.  
- **Circulation:** Supplies nutrients and regulates temperature.
