##Location##
Scales cover the **legs and feet**, while the beak covers the mouth and jaw region.

##Shape##
Both structures are **keratinized extensions** of the epidermis, shaped for protection and feeding.

##Parts##

###1. Scales###
- Overlapping keratin plates on legs and feet.  
- **Function:** Protect against friction and injury.  

###2. Beak (Rhamphotheca)###
- Hard keratin covering over jaw bones.  
- **Function:** Used for feeding, grooming, and defense.  

###3. Claws###
- Keratinized tips on digits.  
- **Function:** Assist in perching, climbing, and catching prey.  

##Function##
- **Protection:** Shields sensitive tissues from damage.  
- **Feeding:** Aids in grasping and manipulating food.  
- **Adaptation:** Specialized for different ecological roles.
