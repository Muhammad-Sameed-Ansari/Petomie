#Epidermis#

##Location##
The epidermis forms the outermost layer of the bird’s skin, covering the entire body, beak, legs, and feet.  
It creates the protective interface between the bird and the environment.

###Shape###
A thin, multilayered sheet of cells, thicker on the legs and thinner beneath feathers.

###Parts###

##Stratum Basale##
The deepest layer where new skin cells are created.  
Energetically, symbolizes renewal and fresh beginnings.

##Stratum Intermedium##
Middle layers where cells mature and strengthen.  
Energetically, represents growth, transition, and adaptation.

##Stratum Corneum##
Outermost keratinized layer providing protection.  
Energetically, embodies boundaries and resilience.

###Function###
Protects the body, prevents dehydration, supports feather and scale growth, and forms the beak sheath.  
Energetically, governs **protection, boundaries, and interaction with the world**.
