#Beak (Rhamphotheca)#

##Location##
Covers the **upper and lower jaws** at the entrance of the digestive tract.  
Forms the external structure of the mouth and replaces teeth.

###Shape###
Made of **keratinized sheaths** (rhamphotheca) covering the premaxilla and mandible bones.  
Beak shape varies greatly among species based on dietâ€”hooked in raptors, cone-shaped in seed eaters, long and slender in nectar feeders.

##Parts##
###1. Upper Beak (Maxillary Rhamphotheca)###
Covers upper jaw bone (premaxilla).  
**Function:** Cuts, tears, or manipulates food.

###2. Lower Beak (Mandibular Rhamphotheca)###
Covers the lower jaw.  
**Function:** Grasps or crushes food items.

###3. Beak Tip###
Often hardened or pointed.  
**Function:** Precise handling, pecking, or tearing.

##Function##
- **Feeding:** Picks up, tears, or crushes food.  
- **Preening:** Cleans feathers.  
- **Defense:** Used for protection or dominance.  
- **Nest Building:** Manipulates materials.
