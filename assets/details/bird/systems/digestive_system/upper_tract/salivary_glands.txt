#Salivary Glands#

##Location##
Scattered throughout the **roof, floor, and sides** of the oral cavity.  
Open into the mouth via small ducts.

###Shape###
Small, lobulated glands producing mucus and enzymes.  
Their number and size vary by species and diet.

##Parts##
###1. Palatine Glands###
Located in the roof of the mouth.  
**Function:** Secrete mucus to lubricate food.

###2. Lingual Glands###
Situated at the base of the tongue.  
**Function:** Aid in swallowing and taste.

###3. Labial and Buccal Glands###
Along sides of mouth.  
**Function:** Secrete mucus for moistening.

##Function##
- **Lubrication:** Moistens food for swallowing.  
- **Protection:** Prevents tissue drying and irritation.  
- **Digestion:** Produces enzymes (amylase) in some birds.  
- **Nesting/Feeding Aid:** In certain species, mucus helps form pellets or feed chicks.
