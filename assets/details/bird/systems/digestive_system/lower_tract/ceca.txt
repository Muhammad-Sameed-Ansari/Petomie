#Ceca (Paired Ceca)#

##Location##
Located at the **junction of the ileum and large intestine**.  
Usually paired and positioned symmetrically on both sides.

###Shape###
Two elongated, blind-ended sacs varying in length and development among species.  
Lined with lymphoid tissue and microbial flora.

##Parts##
###1. Openings###
Connect ceca to the ileum.  
**Function:** Allow entry of undigested material.

###2. Lumen###
Interior cavity containing bacteria.  
**Function:** Site of microbial fermentation.

###3. Lymphoid Tissue###
Located in walls.  
**Function:** Provides immune defense.

##Function##
- **Fermentation:** Breaks down cellulose and complex carbohydrates.  
- **Absorption:** Reabsorbs water and nutrients.  
- **Immunity:** Houses beneficial bacteria and immune cells.  
- **Waste Processing:** Prepares material for excretion.
