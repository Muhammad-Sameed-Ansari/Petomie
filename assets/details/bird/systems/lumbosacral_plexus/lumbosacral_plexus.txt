#Lumbosacral Plexus#

##Location##
Located in the **lumbosacral region** within and adjacent to the **synsacrum**, where ventral rami exit the fused vertebrae and converge to supply the **hindlimbs, pelvis, and tail base**.  
Closely associated with the **kidneys** and **pelvic girdle**.

###Shape###
A network (plexus) of **ventral rami** forming large mixed nerves (motor and sensory).  
Appears as intertwined cords and trunks that course toward the thigh as the **sciatic** and other branches.

##Parts##
###1. Ventral Rami of Synsacral Nerves###
Roots emerging from lumbar, sacral, and anterior caudal segments.  
**Function:** Provide motor and sensory fibers to form the plexus.

###2. Lumbar Component###
Rostral contributions to the plexus.  
**Function:** Supply anterior thigh and contribute to the sciatic trunk.

###3. Sacral Component###
Caudal contributions from sacral levels.  
**Function:** Reinforce innervation to leg, pelvic floor, and tail base.

###4. Sciatic Trunk (Common Ischiadic Component)###
Large mixed nerve formed by plexus convergence.  
**Function:** Primary motor and sensory supply to **thigh, leg, and foot**.

###5. Pudendal/Perineal Branches###
Smaller branches near cloaca and pelvic floor.  
**Function:** Innervate pelvic musculature and skin around the vent, cloaca, and reproductive outlets.

###6. Lumbosacral Organ (Adjacency)###
Specialized expansion of the vertebral canal in birds.  
**Function:** Integrates with balance/locomotion; not a nerve but regionally associated.
