#Pudendal / Perineal Plexus#

##Location##
Small plexiform network near the **caudal sacral/caudal nerves** at the pelvic outlet, close to the **cloaca** and **urogenital tracts**.

###Shape###
Fine **mixed nerve branches** that travel along the pelvic floor and pericloacal region.

##Parts##
###1. Cloacal Branches###
Nerves running to the cloacal wall.  
**Function:** Sensory and motor control for cloacal function and reflexes.

###2. Perineal Branches###
To skin and superficial muscles around the vent.  
**Function:** Sensation and sphincter support.

###3. Reproductive Branches###
Small fibers to oviduct/ductus deferens (species‑dependent).  
**Function:** Modulate reproductive tract reflexes and tone.

##Function##
- **Pelvic Floor Control:** Supports sphincters and pericloacal muscles.  
- **Sensation:** Provides sensory input for mating, egg‑laying, and defecation behaviors.  
- **Reflex Integration:** Coordinates with vagal and autonomic inputs for visceral control.
