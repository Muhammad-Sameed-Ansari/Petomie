#Brachial Plexus#

##Location##
The brachial plexus is located in the shoulder region, formed by spinal nerves emerging from the lower cervical and upper thoracic vertebrae.  
It supplies the wings, shoulder muscles, and parts of the thoracic region.

###Shape###
A branching network of interconnected nerve roots that merge, divide, and extend into the wing.

###Parts###

##Cervical Nerve Roots##
Upper contributors to the plexus.  
Energetically, symbolize **clarity, readiness, and directed movement**.

##Thoracic Nerve Roots##
Lower contributors stabilizing the plexus.  
Energetically, represent **strength, grounding, and support**.

##Major Branches##
Include the radial, median, and ulnar nerves.  
Energetically, embody **coordination, expression, and flow of action**.

###Function###
Controls wing motion, shoulder strength, and sensory feedback from the wings and upper body.  
Energetically, governs **freedom, expansion, and expressive movement**.
