#Areolar Connective Tissue#

In birds, areolar connective tissue is a loose, flexible tissue found beneath the skin, around vessels, nerves, and between organs.  
It fills spaces, provides cushioning, and supports nutrient exchange.  
Energetically, it represents **soft support, openness, and adaptability**.

###Parts###

##Fibrous Network##  
Contains collagen and elastic fibers that gently hold structures in place.  
Energetically, it symbolizes **subtle strength and flexibility**.

##Ground Substance##  
Gel-like medium for fluid and nutrient movement.  
Energetically, it reflects **flow, receptivity, and ease**.

##Embedded Cells##  
Fibroblasts and immune cells support repair and defense.  
Energetically, they embody **responsiveness and gentle protection**.

###Function###  
Areolar tissue cushions, connects, and allows movement between tissues without friction.  
Energetically, it supports **comfort, adaptability, and smooth interaction**.

###Common Imbalances###  
Edema or swelling: emotional or energetic stagnation.  
Inflammation: irritation or boundary stress.  
Weakness: lack of support or scattered energy.
