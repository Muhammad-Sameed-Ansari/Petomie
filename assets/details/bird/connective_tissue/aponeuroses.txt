#Aponeuroses#

##Location##
Broad, flattened **tendinous sheets** connecting muscles to bones or other muscles.  
Prominent in the **pectoral region**, **abdomen**, and **legs**.

###Shape###
Strong **fibrous layers** of dense collagen arranged in parallel or interlacing patterns.  
White, glistening, and slightly elastic.

##Parts##

###1. Pectoral Aponeurosis###
- Spreads across the **breast muscles**.  
- **Function:** Distributes the powerful pull of flight muscles.

###2. Abdominal Aponeurosis###
- Connects the **oblique muscles** of the abdomen.  
- **Function:** Supports trunk movement and abdominal pressure.

###3. Crural Aponeurosis###
- Extends along the **hindlimb**.  
- **Function:** Transfers muscular force during perching or walking.

##Function##
- **Force Distribution:** Spreads muscle tension across large areas.  
- **Protection:** Strengthens and covers underlying muscles.  
- **Stability:** Reinforces the body wall and limb attachments.
