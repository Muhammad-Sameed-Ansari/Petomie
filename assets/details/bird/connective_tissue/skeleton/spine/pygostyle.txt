#Pygostyle#

##Location##  
The pygostyle is the fused terminal bone at the end of the bird’s spine.  
It anchors the tail feathers and controls the tail fan used for flight steering and braking.  

###Shape###  
Short, triangular, fused bone forming a strong platform for feather attachment.  

###Parts###  

##Body##  
Forms the rigid base of the tail fan.  
Energetically, represents **completion and stability**.  

##Feather Attachment Processes##  
Anchor muscles and ligaments that control tail feathers.  
Energetically, symbolize **communication and precision**.  

###Function###  
Controls tail feather movement for steering, braking, and balance in flight.  
Energetically, governs **direction, boundaries, and purposeful movement**.
