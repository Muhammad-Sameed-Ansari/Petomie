#Furcula#

##Location##
The furcula, commonly known as the “wishbone,” is located at the front of the bird’s chest.  
It connects both clavicles and forms part of the shoulder girdle, playing a major role in flight mechanics.

###Shape###
A flexible, V-shaped bone formed by the fusion of the left and right clavicles.  
Its spring-like design allows elasticity during wingbeats.

###Parts###

##Fusion Point##

Energetically, represents integration, harmony, and coordinated effort.

###Function###
Acts as a spring to store and release energy during flight.  
Stabilizes the shoulders and supports wing movement.
