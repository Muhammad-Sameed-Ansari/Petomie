#Intercostal Muscles#

##Location##
The intercostal muscles lie between the ribs, forming the muscular walls of the bird’s thoracic cavity.
They are divided into external and internal layers that assist the ribcage in expanding and contracting during flight and respiration.

###Shape###
Thin layers of muscle fibers arranged diagonally between the ribs, allowing smooth and coordinated motion.

###Function###
Assist in breathing by lifting and lowering the ribs, helping the bird’s unique air-sac system maintain continuous airflow.
Energetically, represent rhythm, coordination, and harmony between strength and movement.
