###Parts###

##Pectoralis Major##  
The largest flight muscle, powering the strong downward wingbeat for lift and propulsion.  
Energetically, it symbolizes drive, forward movement, and powerful expression.

##Pectoralis Minor (Supracoracoideus)##  
Lies beneath the pectoralis major and lifts the wing during the upstroke through a unique tendon-pulley system.  
Energetically, it reflects elevation, lightness, and rising above challenges.

##Sternocoracoideus##  
Supports the sternum and shoulder girdle during flapping and stabilizes the wing joint.  
Energetically, it represents stability, grounding, and centered action.

###Function###  
The pectoral muscles generate lift, enable takeoff, guide wingbeat strength, and stabilize the shoulder girdle.  
Energetically, they support courage, momentum, and the freedom to move upward and forward.

###Common Imbalances###  
Fatigue: repetitive strong wingbeats or long flights.  
Tension: strain from takeoff, landing, or asymmetrical wing use.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect exhaustion, hesitation, or difficulty moving forward.
