#Thymus#

##Parts##

###Thymic Lobules###
Soft lobules located in the upper chest between the lungs, most active in kittens.
Energetically, they embody **growth and transformation**, developing immune intelligence.

###Cortex###
Outer layer where immature lymphocytes (T-cells) multiply and learn self-recognition.
Energetically, it reflects **learning and purification**, teaching discernment between inner and outer energies.

###Medulla###
Inner layer where T-cells are refined before release.
Energetically, it represents **mastery and balance**, ensuring harmony in action and reaction.

###Thymic Hormones###
Regulate immune activity and coordinate with the spleen and lymph nodes.
Energetically, they symbolize **communication and unity**, aligning the lymphatic system with emotional health.

###Lymphatic Connections###
Connect the thymus with the larger lymphatic network, releasing mature immune cells.
Energetically, this network signifies **heart-centered flow**, linking protection with compassion.

##Function##
Trains immune cells and harmonizes the lymphatic system.
Energetically, the thymus represents **courage and resilience**, helping the cat stay open yet secure.
In balance, it supports vitality and self-confidence; under stress, immunity and energy flow may weaken.
