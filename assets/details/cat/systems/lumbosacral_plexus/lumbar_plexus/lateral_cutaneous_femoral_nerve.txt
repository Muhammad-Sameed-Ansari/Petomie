#Lateral Cutaneous Femoral Nerve#
Part of the Nervous System: A sensory nerve from the lumbar plexus (L4–L5) supplying the skin of the lateral thigh.  
Energetically, it represents sensitivity, awareness of boundaries, and adaptability in movement.

###Parts###

##Origin##  
Derived from the ventral branches of L4–L5.  
Energetically, symbolizes awareness of outer balance and protection.

##Pathway##  
Runs along the iliacus muscle, crossing the hip to reach the lateral thigh.  
Energetically, reflects sensitivity and flexible awareness.

##Distribution##  
Supplies sensory input to the skin of the outer thigh.  
Energetically, enhances emotional awareness and spatial balance.

###Function###  
Provides cutaneous sensation to the lateral thigh, aiding in proprioception and comfort.  
Energetically, maintains healthy sensitivity to external stimuli and balance of personal space.

###Common Imbalances###  
Numbness: disconnection or emotional withdrawal.  
Tingling or hypersensitivity: overstimulation or boundary tension.  
Energetic blockage: difficulty perceiving personal limits.
