#Obturator Nerve#
Part of the Nervous System: A major lumbar plexus nerve (L4–L6) that innervates the adductor muscles of the inner thigh.  
Energetically, it represents balance, control, and the power to stay centered during motion.

###Parts###

##Origin##  
Emerges from spinal nerves L4–L6.  
Energetically, symbolizes core alignment and inner strength.

##Pathway##  
Travels through the pelvic cavity and exits via the obturator foramen to the medial thigh.  
Energetically, represents inner direction and movement through obstacles.

##Branches##  
Supplies the adductor, gracilis, and pectineus muscles.  
Energetically, reflects unity, control, and graceful coordination.

###Function###  
Adducts the hind limbs, supporting balance and stability during walking or standing.  
Energetically, maintains centeredness, trust, and emotional balance.

###Common Imbalances###  
Weak adduction: instability or lack of control.  
Pain: inner resistance or emotional tension.  
Energetic stagnation: indecision or blocked direction.
