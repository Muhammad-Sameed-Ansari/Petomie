#Trochlear Nerve (IV)#

The cat’s trochlear nerve enables subtle rotational eye movement, assisting with depth and agility.  
It supports precise visual tracking of movement.  
Energetically, it reflects adaptability, subtlety, and the ability to shift perspective.

###Location###
Emerges from the dorsal midbrain and travels to the superior oblique muscle of the eye.

###Parts###
Single branch – supplies the superior oblique muscle responsible for controlled eye rotation.

###Function###
Facilitates smooth eye coordination and stability.  
Energetically, it enhances fluidity of awareness and adaptability to change.
