#Abducent Nerve (VI)#

The cat’s abducent nerve controls outward eye movement, expanding visual field.  
It assists in spatial balance and focus when stalking prey.  
Energetically, it represents openness, curiosity, and exploration.

###Location###
Arises from the medulla and extends to the eye, supplying the lateral rectus muscle.

###Parts###
Single branch – controls lateral eye movement.

###Function###
Moves the eye outward, widening perspective and awareness.  
Energetically, it supports curiosity, freedom, and new ways of seeing.
