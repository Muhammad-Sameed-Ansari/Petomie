#Metatarsals#

##Location##  
Located in the **hind paw**, between the **tarsal bones** and the **phalanges**.  
They form the main supportive framework of the paw and play a key role in movement and balance.  

###Shape###  
Composed of **five elongated bones (I–V)**, slightly curved and proportionate to the cat’s agile build.  

###Parts###  

##Proximal Base##  
Articulates with the distal tarsals to stabilize the hind paw.  
Energetically, symbolizes **foundation and grounded movement**.  

##Shaft##  
Supports structure and transmits motion through the paw.  
Energetically, represents **purpose and alignment in movement**.  

##Distal Head##  
Connects with the proximal phalanges to form flexible joints.  
Energetically, reflects **transition and expression in motion**.  

###Function###  
Provide leverage, support, and balance during walking, jumping, and climbing.  
Energetically, embody **confidence and fluid progression**.
