#Tarsals#

##Location##  
Located in the **hind limb**, forming the **hock joint** between the **tibia/fibula** and the **metatarsals**.  
They are arranged in **three rows**: proximal, central, and distal.  

###Shape###  
A collection of **irregularly shaped bones**, adapted for strength and flexibility in motion.  

###Parts###  

##Proximal Row##  
Includes the talus and calcaneus, forming the main joint articulation.  
Energetically, represents **foundation and support**.  

##Central Bone##  
Acts as the bridge between upper and lower rows.  
Energetically, symbolizes **balance and integration**.  

##Distal Row##  
Includes the small tarsal bones (I–IV), connecting to the metatarsals.  
Energetically, reflects **adaptability and fine control**.  

###Function###  
Support the cat’s agile movement, absorbing shock and enabling spring-like flexibility.  
Energetically, sustain **grounded movement and harmony with the Earth**.
