#Central Tarsal Bone#

##Location##  
Situated in the **middle of the hock (tarsus)**, between the **proximal tarsal bones** (talus and calcaneus) and the **distal tarsals**.  
It forms the central link of the tarsal structure, ensuring stability and smooth articulation.  

###Shape###  
A **cuboid-shaped bone** with multiple articular surfaces, allowing flexible connection between upper and lower tarsal rows.  

###Parts###  

##Dorsal Surface##  
Connects to tendons and ligaments that stabilize the hock joint.  
Energetically, symbolizes **support and alignment**.  

##Plantar Surface##  
Supports the base of the foot and weight transfer during movement.  
Energetically, reflects **grounding and strength**.  

##Lateral and Medial Facets##  
Articulate with surrounding tarsal bones to enable multidirectional flexibility.  
Energetically, represent **adaptation and flow**.  

###Function###  
Acts as a stabilizing bridge between upper and lower tarsal bones, distributing load and maintaining joint flexibility.  
Energetically, maintains **balance, adaptability, and fluid transitions**.
