#Upper Left Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the upper left quadrant of the cat's mouth, within the maxilla (upper jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the upper central incisors) to the last molar on the left side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Small, sharp teeth used for grooming and grasping prey.  
, Canine (1): Long, curved tooth designed for puncturing and gripping.  
, Premolars (3): Sharp-edged teeth specialized for slicing and shearing meat.  
, Molar (1): Small, flat-crowned tooth used for crushing and grinding.

##Function##
, Grasps, tears, and slices meat effectively.  
, Works with the lower teeth for precise cutting during feeding.  
, Maintains jaw strength and chewing balance.  
, Supports natural feline behaviors such as hunting, feeding, and grooming.

###Energetic Connection###
, Connected to the Stomach and Large Intestine meridians.  
, Represents nourishment, instinct, and emotional processing.  
, Imbalances may relate to digestive upset, frustration, or difficulty expressing primal instincts.  
, Balanced energy here promotes grounded instinct, vitality, and emotional harmony.

##Notes##
An adult cat typically has **30 teeth total**, with **16 in the upper jaw (8 right, 8 left)**.  
Balanced upper dentition ensures efficient feeding, strong digestion, and overall energetic alignment.
