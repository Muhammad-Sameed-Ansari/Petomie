#Upper Right Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the upper right quadrant of the cat's mouth, within the maxilla (upper jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the upper central incisors) to the last molar on the right side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Small front teeth used for grooming and grasping prey.  
, Canine (1): Long, curved tooth specialized for gripping and puncturing.  
, Premolars (3): Sharp-edged teeth designed for cutting and shearing meat.  
, Molar (1): Small, flat-crowned tooth used for crushing and grinding.

##Function##
, Grasps, tears, and slices meat efficiently.  
, Works in coordination with the lower teeth for precise cutting action.  
, Supports hunting, feeding, and grooming behaviors.  
, Maintains jaw strength and balance.

###Energetic Connection###
, Linked to the Stomach and Large Intestine meridians.  
, Represents nourishment, instinct, and emotional clarity.  
, Imbalances may manifest as digestive disturbances, frustration, or overcontrol.  
, Balanced energy promotes vitality, grounded instinct, and harmonious self-expression.

##Notes##
An adult cat typically has **30 teeth total**, with **16 in the upper jaw (8 right, 8 left)**.  
Healthy upper dentition ensures efficient feeding, strong digestion, and balanced energetic flow.
