#Lower Right Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the lower right quadrant of the cat's mouth, within the mandible (lower jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the lower central incisors) to the last molar on the right side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Small front teeth used for grooming and nibbling.  
, Canine (1): Long, curved tooth designed for gripping and puncturing prey.  
, Premolars (2): Sharp-edged teeth adapted for cutting and shearing meat.  
, Molar (1): Small, flat-crowned tooth used for crushing small pieces of food.

##Function##
, Cuts and tears food during chewing.  
, Supports hunting, biting, and tearing behaviors.  
, Maintains alignment and balance with the opposite side.  
, Assists in grooming and oral control of prey or objects.

###Energetic Connection###
, Linked to the Stomach and Large Intestine meridians.  
, Reflects balance between instinctual drive and nourishment.  
, Imbalances may relate to digestive disturbances or suppressed aggression.  
, Balanced energy here supports confidence, clarity, and smooth emotional digestion.

##Notes##
An adult cat typically has **30 teeth total**, with **14 in the lower jaw (7 right, 7 left)**.  
Healthy lower dentition maintains jaw strength, efficient chewing, and balanced energetic flow.
