#Lower Left Dentition#

##Location##
Located in the lower left quadrant of the cat's mouth, within the mandible (lower jaw).  
Extends from the midline (between the lower central incisors) to the last molar on the left side.

###Parts###
, Incisors (3): Small, chisel-like teeth used for nibbling and grooming.  
, Canine (1): Long, curved tooth adapted for gripping and puncturing prey.  
, Premolars (2): Sharp-edged teeth specialized for slicing and shearing meat.  
, Molar (1): Small, flat-crowned tooth used for crushing and grinding small food particles.

##Function##
, Tears and slices food during feeding.  
, Works in coordination with the upper teeth for precise cutting action.  
, Maintains chewing balance and jaw stability.  
, Supports natural feline behaviors like grooming, hunting, and play.

###Energetic Connection###
, Connected to the Stomach and Large Intestine meridians.  
, Reflects the balance between instinct, nourishment, and emotional processing.  
, Imbalances here may correspond to digestive upset or tension from suppressed instincts.  
, Balanced energy promotes confidence, self-expression, and healthy assimilation of energy.

##Notes##
An adult cat typically has **30 teeth total**, with **14 in the lower jaw (7 right, 7 left)**.  
Balanced lower dentition supports comfortable chewing, smooth digestion, and grounded vitality.
