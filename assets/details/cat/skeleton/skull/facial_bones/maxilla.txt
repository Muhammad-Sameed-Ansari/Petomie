#Maxilla#

##Location##
Forms the main portion of the upper jaw and the sides of the face.  
Extends from the incisive bone in front to the zygomatic bone behind.

###Parts###
1. **Body** – Contains alveoli for the upper teeth.  
   Houses the maxillary sinus and forms part of the nasal and oral cavities.

2. **Facial Surface** – Forms the lateral wall of the face.  
   Provides muscle attachment for facial and chewing muscles.

3. **Palatine Process** – Extends medially to form part of the hard palate.  
   Separates the oral and nasal cavities and joins with the opposite maxilla.

4. **Infraorbital Foramen** – Opening through which the infraorbital nerve and vessels pass.  
   Supplies sensation to the upper lip and face.

##Function##
 Houses upper teeth and forms part of the nasal and oral cavities.  
 Supports muscles used for chewing and facial expression.  
 Provides structural stability and protects the nasal passage.  
 Contributes to the overall shape of the upper jaw.
