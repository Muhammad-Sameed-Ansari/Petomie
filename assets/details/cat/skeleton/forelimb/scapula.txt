#Scapula#

##Location##  
The scapula, or shoulder blade, lies on the lateral surface of the thorax.  
In cats, it is long and narrow with exceptional mobility, allowing agile forelimb motion.  
It connects to the body solely through muscles, enhancing flexibility and reach.

###Shape###  
A **thin, triangular bone** with a prominent spine and a slightly curved surface.  
Its glenoid cavity forms the socket for the humerus, completing the shoulder joint.

###Parts###

##Supraspinous Fossa##

##Infraspinous Fossa##  
Provides surface for infraspinatus muscle that stabilizes the shoulder.

##Glenoid Cavity##  
Receives the head of the humerus for articulation.  
Energetically, reflects **connection and partnership in motion**.

##Acromion Process##  
Extends from the spine to support shoulder musculature.  
Energetically, represents **graceful strength and adaptability**.

###Function###  
The scapula supports limb movement, allowing rotation, elevation, and protraction.  
It absorbs force during landing and contributes to stealth and balance.  
Energetically, it symbolizes **fluid coordination, independence, and lightness in action**.
