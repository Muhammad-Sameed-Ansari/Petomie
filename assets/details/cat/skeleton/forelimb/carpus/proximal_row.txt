#Proximal Tarsals#

##Location##  
Situated at the **upper portion of the hock**, between the **tibia/fibula** and the **central and distal tarsals**.  
These bones form the foundation of the hock joint, providing strength and leverage.  

###Shape###  
Composed of **two main bones** — the **talus** and **calcaneus**, both strong and irregularly shaped to withstand tension and force.  

###Parts###  

##Talus (Tibial Tarsal Bone)##  
Forms the main joint with the tibia, enabling flexion and extension of the hock.  
Energetically, symbolizes **movement, trust, and balance in direction**.  

##Calcaneus (Heel Bone)##  
Projects upward to form the hock’s point, serving as a lever for powerful muscles.  
Energetically, reflects **drive, propulsion, and resilience**.  

###Function###  
Transmit and transform force between the limb and paw, enabling push-off during locomotion.  
Energetically, sustain **strength, determination, and grounding**.
