#Distal Tarsals#

##Location##  
Located in the **lower part of the hock (tarsus)**, between the **central tarsal bone** and the **metatarsal bones**.  
These bones form part of the distal row of the tarsus, supporting flexibility and precise paw movement.  

###Shape###  
A group of **small, irregularly shaped bones**, closely packed to provide stability and coordinated movement of the hind paw.  

###Parts###  

##First Tarsal Bone##  
Connects to the first metatarsal, supporting the inner paw.  
Energetically, symbolizes **balance and adaptability**.  

##Second and Third Tarsal Bones##  
Lie medially, bearing weight and allowing smooth energy transfer through the paw.  
Energetically, reflect **coordination and fluid motion**.  

##Fourth Tarsal Bone##  
Larger and laterally placed, connecting with the calcaneus and metatarsals IV–V.  
Energetically, represents **grounded power and stability**.  

###Function###  
Support body weight, absorb impact, and ensure flexibility during locomotion.  
Energetically, govern **adaptation, connection to earth, and balanced steps**.
