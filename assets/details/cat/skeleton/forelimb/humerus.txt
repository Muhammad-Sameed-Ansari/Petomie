#Humerus#

##Location##  
The humerus is the **upper limb bone** connecting the **scapula** with the **radius and ulna**.  
In cats, it is slender and slightly curved, allowing flexible and graceful forelimb motion.  
It forms the shoulder joint proximally and the elbow joint distally.

###Shape###  
A **long bone** with a rounded proximal head, smooth shaft, and a broad lower end with well-defined condyles.

###Parts###  

##Head##  
Articulates with the glenoid cavity of the scapula.  
Energetically, represents **connection between stability and grace**.

##Greater and Lesser Tubercles##  
Prominent projections for muscular attachment.  
Energetically, symbolize **confidence and adaptability**.

##Deltoid Crest##  
A ridge along the lateral surface for deltoid muscle attachment.  
Energetically, embodies **assertive motion and self-expression**.

##Shaft##  
Supports weight transmission and controlled limb swing.  
Energetically, reflects **stability, rhythm, and strength in flow**.

##Distal End##  
Includes the trochlea and capitulum forming the elbow joint.  
Energetically, represents **balance between power and flexibility**.

###Function###  
The humerus acts as a lever for forelimb movement, allowing cats to climb, jump, and land softly.  
It supports strength with elasticity for controlled precision.  
Energetically, it expresses **confidence in movement and the courage to explore**.
