#Auditory Structures#

##Location##
The auditory system is located in the head and includes the outer, middle, and inner ear. 
It connects to the brain through the auditory nerve and also supports balance through its connection to the vestibular system.

###Shape###
- Outer Ear: The pinna (external ear flap) and ear canal collect and direct sound waves toward the eardrum.  
- Middle Ear: Contains three small bones — malleus (hammer), incus (anvil), and stapes (stirrup) — which transmit and amplify sound vibrations.  
- Inner Ear: Contains the cochlea, responsible for hearing, and the vestibular system for balance.  
- Auditory Nerve: Connects the inner ear to the brain’s auditory centers.

##Function##
- The pinna moves independently to detect and locate sound sources from multiple directions.  
- Sound waves travel down the ear canal and cause the tympanic membrane (eardrum) to vibrate.  
- These vibrations pass through the ossicles to the cochlea, where fluid motion activates tiny hair cells that send signals through the auditory nerve to the brain.  
- The system provides acute hearing for communication, hunting, and environmental awareness.  

Cats can detect sounds up to around 65,000 Hz — far higher than humans — making them highly sensitive to subtle noises and movements.
