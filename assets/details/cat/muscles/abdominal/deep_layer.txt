##Location##
Located beneath the middle and superficial layers, closest to the internal organs.  
Forms the foundation for abdominal support and internal stability.

###Shape###
Composed of **thin, transverse muscle sheets** that run across the abdomen like a belt.  
These deep fibers maintain tension and pressure inside the abdomen.

##Parts##

###1. Transversus Abdominis###
- Deepest abdominal layer.  
- **Function:** Compresses abdomen, supports spine, and stabilizes the trunk.  

###2. Quadratus Lumborum###
- Lies between pelvis and spine.  
- **Function:** Stabilizes back and helps with side bending.  

###3. Psoas Minor###
- Small muscle on ventral lumbar area.  
- **Function:** Aids in spinal flexion and supports lumbar stability.  

##Function##
- **Core Strength:** Provides foundation for abdominal and spinal control.  
- **Protection:** Shields and supports internal organs.  
- **Balance:** Maintains posture during motion.  
- **Breathing:** Helps regulate internal abdominal pressure.
