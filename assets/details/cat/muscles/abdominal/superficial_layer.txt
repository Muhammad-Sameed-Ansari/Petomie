##Location##
Covers the **outer abdominal wall**, extending from the ribs to the pelvis.  
Forms the most visible layer responsible for movement and strength.

###Shape###
Composed of **broad, oblique muscles** that run diagonally across the abdomen.  
These fibers provide protection and flexibility for dynamic movement.

##Parts##

###1. External Abdominal Oblique###
- Largest and most external abdominal muscle.  
- **Function:** Compresses abdomen, rotates and flexes trunk, and supports posture.  

###2. Cutaneous Trunci###
- Thin muscle beneath the skin.  
- **Function:** Assists minor skin and flank movements.  

##Function##
- **Protection:** Shields organs from impact.  
- **Movement:** Aids trunk rotation and bending.  
- **Support:** Maintains abdominal pressure.  
- **Coordination:** Works with deeper layers for balance and motion.
