##Location##
Found on the **underside of the abdomen**, stretching from the ribs and sternum to the pubic area.  
These muscles form the ventral belly floor and support internal organs.

###Shape###
Made of **broad, flat muscle sheets** with tendinous fibers running longitudinally.  
They provide flexible yet strong support for abdominal movement.

##Parts##

###1. Rectus Abdominis###
- Central belly muscle.  
- **Function:** Flexes trunk and stabilizes abdomen during movement.  

###2. Transversus Abdominis###
- Deep layer beneath other abdominal muscles.  
- **Function:** Compresses abdomen and supports internal pressure.  

###3. Abdominal Fascia###
- Connective tissue covering ventral wall.  
- **Function:** Provides elasticity, tension, and structural integrity.  

##Function##
- **Organ Support:** Holds abdominal contents.  
- **Core Stability:** Assists posture and movement.  
- **Breathing:** Compresses abdomen during exhalation.  
- **Protection:** Shields organs and absorbs pressure.
