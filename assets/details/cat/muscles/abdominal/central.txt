##Location##
Situated along the **midline of the abdomen**, connecting the left and right abdominal walls.  
Includes the **rectus abdominis** and **linea alba**, forming the central support structure for the abdominal wall.

###Shape###
Composed of **paired, elongated muscles** running from the sternum to the pubic bone.  
The linea alba forms a strong fibrous seam joining both sides together.

##Parts##

###1. Rectus Abdominis###
- Long, strap-like muscle extending along the belly.  
- **Function:** Flexes the spine, supports internal organs, and assists breathing.  

###2. Linea Alba###
- Fibrous seam connecting the left and right abdominal muscles.  
- **Function:** Reinforces abdominal wall and serves as a key surgical reference.  

##Function##
- **Support:** Maintains abdominal tone and posture.  
- **Trunk Flexion:** Aids bending and movement of the back.  
- **Protection:** Shields internal organs and stabilizes the abdomen.  
- **Breathing:** Works with diaphragm during expiration.
