#Tail Muscles#

The cat’s tail muscles form a flexible extension of the spine, allowing fine control, communication, and balance.  
They coordinate movement from base to tip for expression and stability.  
Energetically, they represent **flow, release, and emotional balance — the freedom to express and let go**.

###Parts###

##Superficial Muscles##  
Move the tail side to side and up and down.  
Energetically, symbolize **flexibility and expression**.

##Intermediate Layers##  
Coordinate smooth, flowing motion and stability.  
Energetically, embody **grace and harmony**.

##Deep Muscles##  
Anchor the tail base to the pelvis and spine for power and precision.  
Energetically, represent **core alignment and grounded control**.

###Function###  
The tail muscles provide balance, coordination, and communication.  
They also aid in stabilization during jumps and turns.  
Energetically, they mirror **emotional state and energetic flow**.

###Common Imbalances###  
Tension: emotional holding or stiffness.  
Weakness: poor balance or tail carriage.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect **suppressed expression or emotional tension**.
