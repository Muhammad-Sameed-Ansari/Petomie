#Perineal Muscles#

##Location##
Found in the **space between the anus, tail, and external genital organs**, forming the **pelvic outlet**.  
These muscles control closure of the anal and urogenital openings and support nearby structures.

##Shape##
Thin, paired muscles surrounding the anus and genital area.  
Arranged in circular and longitudinal layers to allow precise control of movement and closure.

##Parts##

###1. External Anal Sphincter###
- Surrounds the anus.  
- **Function:** Voluntarily controls anal opening during defecation.  

###2. Levator Ani###
- Runs along the inner pelvic wall.  
- **Function:** Lifts the anus and assists in expelling feces.  

###3. Coccygeus###
- From ischium to tail base.  
- **Function:** Moves and stabilizes tail during balance or emotion display.  

###4. Bulbospongiosus###
- Surrounds vestibule (in females) or penis root (in males).  
- **Function:** Aids in urination, ejaculation, and genital closure.  

###5. Ischiocavernosus###
- Lies along the root of penis or clitoris.  
- **Function:** Compresses venous return to help erection and maintain blood flow.  

##Function##
- **Defecation:** Controls anal closure and bowel movement.  
- **Urination:** Assists in bladder emptying and urogenital control.  
- **Reproduction:** Aids in mating and ejaculation or vaginal contraction.  
- **Tail Movement:** Stabilizes and moves the tail base for communication and balance.  
- **Pelvic Support:** Supports organs in pelvic cavity and prevents tissue prolapse.
