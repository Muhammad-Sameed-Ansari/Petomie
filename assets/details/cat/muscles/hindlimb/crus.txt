#Crus Muscles#

##Location##
Located between the **stifle (knee)** and the **hock (tarsus)**, forming the muscular calf or shank region.  
These muscles control hock and digital motion.

##Shape##
Long, tapering muscles with strong tendons extending distally to the foot.  
Arranged into cranial, caudal, and lateral compartments.

##Parts##

###1. Gastrocnemius###
- Large, two-headed muscle forming the calf.  
- Extends hock and flexes stifle.  

###2. Soleus###
- Small muscle beside gastrocnemius.  
- Assists hock extension.  

###3. Tibialis Cranialis###
- Located on the front of the shin.  
- Flexes hock and rotates paw medially.  

###4. Peroneus Longus###
- Lateral muscle on the outer shank.  
- Flexes hock and rotates paw outward.  

###5. Extensor Digitorum Longus###
- Runs along cranial surface of tibia.  
- Extends digits and flexes hock.  

###6. Flexor Digitorum Longus###
- Deep muscle running caudally to the digits.  
- Flexes digits and extends hock.  

###7. Popliteus###
- Small triangular muscle behind the stifle.  
- Rotates leg medially and stabilizes stifle joint.  

##Function##
Flex and extend the hock and digits.  
Provide propulsion, support, and stability during stance and locomotion.  
Assist in landing and balance during jumping or climbing.
