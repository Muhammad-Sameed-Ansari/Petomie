#Thymus#

##Location##
The thymus gland is located in the **upper chest**, in front of the **heart** and behind the **sternum** (breastbone).  
It is more active and larger in kittens, gradually shrinking as the cat matures.

###Shape###
A **soft, lobed organ** consisting of lymphatic tissue.  
In young cats, it appears pale and spongy; in older cats, it becomes smaller and fibrous.

##Function##
- Produces **T-cells**, essential for immune defense.  
- Regulates the **immune system’s development** during growth.  
- Supports **healing, vitality**, and protection from infection.  
- Links the **endocrine** and **immune** systems.
