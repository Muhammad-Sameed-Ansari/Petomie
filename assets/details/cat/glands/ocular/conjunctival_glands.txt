#Conjunctival Glands#

The cat’s conjunctival glands lie within the conjunctiva — the thin membrane covering the eye and eyelids.  
They produce mucous components of the tear film for lubrication and defense.  
Energetically, they represent sensitivity, adaptability, and emotional awareness.

###Parts###

##Goblet Cells##  
Scattered cells producing mucin for tear stability.  
Energetically, they symbolize compassion and fluid understanding.

##Accessory Glands##  
Enhance tear protection and surface moisture.  
Energetically, they reflect balance and responsiveness.

##Conjunctival Layer##  
Forms a clear, protective covering.  
Energetically, it represents gentle awareness and transparency.

###Function###  
Conjunctival glands maintain tear film stability and defend against pathogens.  
Energetically, they help maintain emotional clarity and sensitivity.

###Common Imbalances###  
Redness or irritation: emotional strain or overstimulation.  
Dryness: disconnection or tension.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect sensitivity without boundaries or emotional fatigue.
