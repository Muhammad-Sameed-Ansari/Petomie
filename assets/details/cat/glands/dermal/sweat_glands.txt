#Apocrine Glands#

In cats, apocrine glands are sweat-type glands associated with hair follicles, particularly in scent-producing areas.  
They play a role in temperature regulation and scent communication.  
Energetically, they represent **expression, individuality, and emotional signaling**.

###Parts###

##Skin Apocrine Glands##  
Located throughout the skin, often near hair roots.  
Produce a milky secretion that combines with sebum for coat conditioning.  
Energetically, they symbolize **self-expression and communication through presence**.

##Scent Glands##  
Highly active in facial, tail, and anal regions for marking behavior.  
Energetically, they reflect **identity and emotional connection**.

##Specialized Areas##  
Found near the nipples and external ear canal, aiding pheromone and wax production.  
Energetically, they embody **intuition and subtle awareness**.

###Function###  
Apocrine glands assist with thermal regulation, scent marking, and chemical communication.  
Energetically, they help **release emotional signals and strengthen self-identity**.

###Common Imbalances###  
Overactivity: anxiety or overstimulation.  
Blockage: suppressed self-expression or emotional stagnation.  
Infection: stress or boundary invasion.  
Energetically, imbalance may reflect **difficulty expressing true self or emotional overload**.
