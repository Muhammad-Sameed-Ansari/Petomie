#Submandibular Glands#

The cat’s submandibular glands are scent-producing glands located beneath the chin.  
They release subtle pheromones used in social bonding and territory marking through chin rubbing.  
Energetically, they represent **connection, affection, and gentle expression**.

###Parts###

##Sebaceous Units##  
Produce oily secretions for scent marking.  
Energetically symbolize **self-identity and affectionate bonding**.

##Follicular Ducts##  
Carry secretions toward the skin surface.  
Energetically represent **expression and communication**.

##Soft Connective Capsule##  
Supports and protects the gland.  
Energetically reflects **stability and trust**.

###Function###  
Enhances social bonding, territory marking, and emotional communication.  
Energetically strengthens **connection and comfort**.

###Common Imbalances###  
Overuse: excessive rubbing — linked to **stress or need for reassurance**.  
Underuse: withdrawal — tied to **emotional distance or insecurity**.
