#Liver Meridian#

##Location##
Begins on the medial side of the hind paw at the lateral edge of the second digit (LV-1).
Ascends along the medial side of the hind limb, passing in front of the hock and behind the tibia.
Continues up the inner thigh, crossing the inguinal region and entering the abdomen.
Internally connects with the liver, gall bladder, and diaphragm, and travels upward along the costal (rib) area.
Ends on the chest between the sixth and seventh intercostal spaces (LV-14).

##Shape##
A deep and medial meridian running from the inner hind paw up to the lower chest.

##Function##
Stores and regulates Blood and Qi
Ensures smooth flow of emotions and energy throughout the body
Supports vision, tendons, and ligaments
Assists in detoxification and reproductive balance
Encourages creativity, patience, and emotional harmony

##Signs of Imbalance##
Irritability, frustration, or aggression
Eye issues or sensitivity to light
Muscle or tendon stiffness, especially in hind limbs
Menstrual or reproductive disturbances (in females)
Digestive sluggishness or bloating
Depression or suppressed emotions
