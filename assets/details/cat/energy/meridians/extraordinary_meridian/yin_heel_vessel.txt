#Yin Heel Vessel (Yin Qiao Mai)#

##Location##
Begins on the medial side of the hind paw, just below the inner ankle at the region of Kidney 6 (KI-6).
Ascends along the medial aspect of the hind limb, passing over the hock and the inner thigh.
Continues upward along the ventral abdomen and chest, running close to the Conception Vessel.
Travels through the throat and ends at the inner corner of the eye (BL-1), where it meets the Yang Qiao Mai and Bladder Meridian.

##Shape##
A deep, medial meridian that mirrors the Yin aspects of movement and stability.
It flows upward from the hind limb to the head, supporting posture, balance, and the restful state of the body.

##Function##
Regulates Yin energy, body fluids, and sleep cycles
Supports balance between movement and stillness
Influences the eyes, reproductive organs, and throat
Nourishes muscles along the inner leg and chest
Encourages introspection, receptivity, and calm presence

##Signs of Imbalance##
Weakness or tightness along the inner legs
Sleep disturbances, especially difficulty staying asleep
Dry eyes or throat
Reproductive or urinary irregularities
Emotional withdrawal, exhaustion, or inward stagnation
