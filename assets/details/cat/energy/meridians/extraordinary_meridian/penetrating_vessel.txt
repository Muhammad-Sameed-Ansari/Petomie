#Penetrating Vessel (Chong Mai)#

##Location##
Begins near the perineum, close to the origin of the Kidney Meridian.
Ascends through the pelvis and ventral abdomen, running parallel to the Conception Vessel but slightly deeper.
Travels internally through the reproductive organs, stomach, and chest.
Branches upward to the throat and downward to the hind limbs, connecting with the Kidney and Stomach meridians.
Its energy spreads through the abdomen, thorax, and spine, linking all major Yin meridians.

##Shape##
A deep, central meridian running along the core of the body, forming the energetic “sea” that nourishes all twelve regular meridians.
It serves as a bridge between Yin and Yang and connects the lower abdomen to the chest and heart.

##Function##
Acts as the “Sea of Blood” and “Sea of the Twelve Meridians”
Regulates blood flow, menstruation, and reproductive vitality
Strengthens the connection between torso and limbs
Balances emotions and supports deep ancestral Qi (Jing)
Encourages stability, centeredness, and internal harmony

##Signs of Imbalance##
Circulatory or reproductive disorders
Abdominal fullness, cramps, or poor digestion
Back pain or weakness in the core muscles
Irregular heat sensations or hot flashes
Emotional instability, insecurity, or loss of grounding
