#Yang Heel Vessel (Yang Qiao Mai)#

##Location##
Begins on the lateral side of the hind paw, just below the outer ankle at the region of Bladder 62 (BL-62).
Ascends along the lateral aspect of the hind limb, passing over the hock and along the outer thigh.
Continues upward over the flank and lateral side of the body, crossing the shoulder and neck.
Travels to the face, passing over the cheek and outer corner of the eye (BL-1), where it joins the Yin Qiao Mai and Bladder Meridian.
Internally connects with the brain and influences the spine and nervous system.

##Shape##
A lateral meridian that mirrors the Yin Qiao Mai, governing activity, alertness, and muscular tone.

##Function##
Regulates Yang energy, movement, and agility
Controls muscle tone, balance, and posture
Supports coordination of eyes and head movements
Stimulates alertness and wakefulness
Encourages outward expression, action, and responsiveness

##Signs of Imbalance##
Muscular tension or weakness along the outer limbs
Restlessness, insomnia, or excessive activity
Uneven gait or imbalance between left and right sides
Eye twitching or visual disturbances
Hyperactivity, impatience, or difficulty relaxing
