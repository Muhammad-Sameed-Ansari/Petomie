#Triple Heater Meridian (San Jiao Jing)#

The **Primary Meridians** are the twelve main energy channels described in **Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)**.  
They form the core network through which **Qi (vital life force)** flows, connecting every organ, tissue, and emotion in the catâ€™s body.  
Each meridian is associated with a **Yin or Yang organ**, reflecting the dynamic balance between nourishment and activity, rest and motion, inner stillness and outward expression.  
In cats, these meridians regulate both **physical functions** such as digestion, circulation, and respiration, and **energetic aspects** such as intuition, awareness, and emotional grace.  
Together, they ensure harmony between body, mind, and spirit, allowing the cat to move with natural elegance and instinctive precision.  

The **Triple Heater Meridian** governs **temperature regulation, communication, and energy flow between body sections**.  
It represents harmony and coordination between physical, emotional, and spiritual aspects.  

###Shape and Pathway###  
Begins at the outer tip of the back paw, travels up the outer side of the forelimb, continues over the shoulder and neck, and ends near the outer corner of the eye.  
This pathway unites the upper, middle, and lower parts of the body, ensuring smooth Qi movement.  

###Function###  
Physically, it regulates metabolism, hormonal balance, and temperature.  
It supports communication between organs and maintains overall vitality.  
Energetically, it harmonizes internal systems, connecting body and spirit, fostering adaptability and flow.  

###Common Imbalances###  
Hormonal imbalance, temperature sensitivity, or sluggish digestion.  
Energetic disharmony such as restlessness or emotional disconnection.  
Emotional signs include imbalance between giving and receiving energy.
