#Spleen Meridian#

##Location##
Begins on the medial side of the hind paw at the caudal edge of the second digit (SP-1).
Ascends along the medial aspect of the hind limb, passing in front of the hock and behind the tibia.
Continues up the inner thigh and groin, traveling along the ventral abdomen close to the midline.
Passes through the flank and chest, connecting internally with the spleen, stomach, and heart before ending on the lateral chest wall in the sixth intercostal space (SP-21).

##Shape##
A medial and superficial meridian running from the inside of the hind paw up to the chest.
Closely follows the major vessels, lymphatic channels, and muscles involved in circulation and digestion.

##Function##
Transforms and transports food energy into Qi and Blood
Supports digestion, absorption, and overall nourishment
Regulates fluids and helps prevent dampness or stagnation
Strengthens muscles and limbs, particularly the inner thighs
Balances worry and overthinking with trust and stability

##Signs of Imbalance##
Poor appetite or sluggish digestion
Loose stool, bloating, or abdominal fullness
Weakness of the hind limbs or muscle atrophy
Edema, lethargy, or fluid retention
Anxiety, overthinking, or feeling ungrounded
