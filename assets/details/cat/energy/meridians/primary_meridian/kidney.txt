#Kidney Meridian#

##Location##
Begins on the plantar surface of the hind paw between the pads of the third and fourth digits (KI-1).
Ascends along the caudal aspect of the hock, passing between the tendons of the flexor muscles.
Continues up the medial side of the hind limb, traveling behind the tibia and through the inner thigh and groin region.
Enters the abdomen near the ventral midline, connecting internally with the kidneys, bladder, and reproductive organs.
Externally, it continues upward along the ventral abdomen and chest, ending near the sternum in the fifth intercostal space (KI-27).

##Shape##
A deep, medial meridian running from the hind paw to the chest.

##Function##
Stores and governs Essence (Jing)
Supports growth, fertility, and longevity
Regulates water metabolism and fluid balance
Strengthens bones, back, and hind limbs
Anchors Qi and willpower, promoting resilience and calm
Connects to the ears and hearing

##Signs of Imbalance##
Weakness in the lower back or hindquarters
Urinary or reproductive problems
Infertility or hormonal imbalance
Cold intolerance, lethargy, or hair loss
Fear, insecurity, or exhaustion
