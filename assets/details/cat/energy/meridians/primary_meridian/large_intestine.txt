#Large Intestine Meridian#

##Location##
Begins on the medial (inner) side of the front paw, just distal to the dewclaw at the radial aspect of the second digit (LI-1).
Ascends along the cranial-medial side of the forelimb, passing the carpus and continuing up the extensor groove along the radius.
Travels over the craniolateral surface of the shoulder and neck region, crossing in front of the shoulder joint toward the lower cheek.
Ends beside the nostril on the same side of the face (LI-20), connecting internally with the nose and large intestine.

##Shape##
A superficial meridian running from the front paw up the lateral (outer) side of the forelimb to the face.
Closely follows muscle groups responsible for forelimb extension and head movement.

##Function##
Eliminates waste and regulates bowel function
Balances body fluids and helps release toxins
Supports immune response and clears exterior heat
Assists with sinus, nasal, and dental issues
Encourages emotional release and letting go of the old

##Signs of Imbalance##
Constipation or loose stools
Nasal congestion, sneezing, or dental pain
Skin inflammation, itching, or dryness
Stiffness or pain along the forelimb or shoulder
Irritability or difficulty “letting go” emotionally
