#Large Intestine Meridian (Da Chang Jing)#

The **Primary Meridians** are the twelve main energy channels described in **Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)**.  
They form the core network through which **Qi (vital life force)** flows, connecting every organ, tissue, and emotion in the catâ€™s body.  
Each meridian is associated with a **Yin or Yang organ**, reflecting the dynamic balance between nourishment and activity, rest and motion, inner stillness and outward expression.  
In cats, these meridians regulate both **physical functions** such as digestion, circulation, and respiration, and **energetic aspects** such as intuition, awareness, and emotional grace.  
Together, they ensure harmony between body, mind, and spirit, allowing the cat to move with natural elegance and instinctive precision.  

The **Large Intestine Meridian** governs **elimination, release, and purification**.  
It represents the ability to let go of what no longer serves the body or spirit.  

###Shape and Pathway###  
Begins at the tip of the front paw, travels up along the outer side of the forelimb, over the shoulder, across the neck, and ends at the side of the nose.  
This upward flow symbolizes transformation and renewal through release.  

###Function###  
Physically, it regulates bowel movement, waste elimination, and detoxification.  
It supports the skin and mucous membranes.  
Energetically, it helps the cat release emotional tension and old energy, fostering clarity and lightness.  

###Common Imbalances###  
Constipation, diarrhea, or skin issues.  
Energetic disharmony such as holding on to grief or rigidity.  
Emotional signs include stubbornness or inability to forgive.
