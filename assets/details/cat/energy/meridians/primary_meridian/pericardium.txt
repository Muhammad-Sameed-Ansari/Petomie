#Pericardium Meridian#

##Location##
Begins in the chest at the pericardium (the protective membrane surrounding the heart) and emerges at the thoracic inlet near the shoulder (PC-1).
Travels along the medial side of the forelimb, following the groove between the flexor carpi muscles.
Passes through the elbow and continues down the inner forearm, crossing the palmar side of the carpus.
Ends at the tip of the middle digit on the front paw (PC-9).

##Shape##
A smooth, medial meridian flowing from the chest down the inner forelimb.
Runs parallel to the Heart Meridian but acts as its protector, maintaining emotional and physical equilibrium through circulation and Qi flow.

##Function##
Protects and supports the Heart
Regulates circulation of Blood and Qi
Calms the mind and harmonizes emotions
Opens the chest and relieves tension or anxiety
Balances affection, compassion, and boundaries

##Signs of Imbalance##
Restlessness, agitation, or emotional sensitivity
Chest tightness or labored breathing
Circulatory or cardiac irregularities
Forelimb weakness or stiffness
Overattachment, jealousy, or emotional burnout
