#Small Intestine Meridian#

##Location##
Begins at the caudomedial corner of the front paw on the lateral side of the fifth digit (SI-1).
Ascends along the caudolateral surface of the forelimb, crossing the carpus and running behind the ulna along the extensor muscles.
Passes over the lateral aspect of the shoulder and continues up the side of the neck, behind the jaw.
Curves around the base of the ear and ends at the front of the ear canal near the temporomandibular joint (SI-19).
Internally, it connects to the small intestine, heart, and stomach.

##Shape##
A superficial meridian flowing along the outer edge of the forelimb to the head.
Closely related to the muscular and nervous pathways that coordinate balance between forelimb action, hearing, and digestion.

##Function##
Separates pure from impure substances in digestion
Assists absorption and assimilation of nutrients
Balances body temperature and distribution of fluids
Supports hearing and jaw relaxation
Encourages discernment and clarity in decisions and emotions

##Signs of Imbalance##
Digestive disturbances or abdominal pain
Stiffness or soreness in the shoulder or neck
Ear infections, head shaking, or tinnitus
Jaw tension or teeth grinding
Confusion, indecision, or hypersensitivity
