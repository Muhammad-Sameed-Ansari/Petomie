#Stomach Meridian#

##Location##
Begins just below the eye at the infraorbital foramen (ST-1), descending along the side of the face and jaw.
It crosses the throat and runs down the ventral side of the neck and chest, passing through the pectoral region.
Continues along the cranial and lateral side of the abdomen, near the line of the nipples, and travels over the flank toward the thigh.
Runs down the cranial aspect of the hind limb, over the stifle and the front of the tibia, then along the dorsolateral side of the cannon bone.
Ends on the dorsolateral surface of the second toe near the claw (ST-45).

##Shape##
A long, bilateral meridian flowing from the head down the front of the body to the hind toes.
Superficial along most of its course, following the digestive organs internally and the major flexor and extensor muscles of the hind limbs externally.

##Function##
Transforms and transports food and fluids
Regulates digestion, appetite, and nutrient absorption
Supports muscle tone and vitality of the limbs
Balances emotions related to nourishment and security
Connects the mind-body link between physical sustenance and emotional stability

##Signs of Imbalance##
Loss of appetite, vomiting, or poor digestion
Bloating, gas, or abdominal discomfort
Weakness or trembling of the hind limbs
Excess salivation or dental discomfort
Worry, anxiety, or overthinking tendencies
