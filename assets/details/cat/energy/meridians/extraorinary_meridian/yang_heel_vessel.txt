#Yang Motility Vessel (Yang Qiao Mai)#

The **Extraordinary Meridians** are special channels in **Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM)** that act as reservoirs of Qi and Blood.  
They regulate, store, and distribute energy throughout the body, maintaining harmony between the twelve Primary Meridians.  
Unlike the main meridians, they do not correspond to a specific organ but rather balance the entire energetic network.  
In cats, they represent deep instinctive wisdom, energetic protection, and alignment between spirit and body.  

The **Yang Motility Vessel** governs **activity, alertness, and outward expression**.  
It coordinates Yang movement, responsiveness, and interaction with the environment.  

###Shape and Pathway###  
Begins at the outer side of the hind paw, travels up the outer leg, along the back, neck, and head, ending near the outer corner of the eye.  
This pathway mirrors agility, coordination, and readiness for motion.  

###Function###  
Physically, it controls muscle tone on the outer body and balance during movement.  
It influences coordination, reflexes, and energy distribution to limbs.  
Energetically, it enhances vitality, action, and assertiveness, balancing rest with alertness.  

###Common Imbalances###  
Tension, hyperactivity, or imbalance between left and right movement.  
Energetic disharmony such as restlessness, nervousness, or burnout.  
Emotional signs include impulsiveness or inability to relax.
